### Idea 1: Wasserstein-Distributionally Robust Routing for Test-Time Model Merging (WDR-Merge)
- **Description**: Standard routers trained on a tiny offline calibration set assume that the online inference query distribution is identical to the calibration distribution. However, test streams exhibit severe, non-stationary task-distribution shifts. We propose a Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Optimization (WDRO) framework. Instead of minimizing empirical loss, we minimize the worst-case expected classification error over an uncertainty ball of probability distributions defined by the Wasserstein metric centered at the calibration set.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Formulate the distributionally robust routing problem as:
  $$\min_{\boldsymbol{\tau}} \sup_{Q: W_2(Q, P_{\text{cal}}) \le \rho} \mathbb{E}_{(x, y) \sim Q} [\mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}}(f_{\text{merged}}(x; \boldsymbol{\tau}), y)]$$
  Using Kantorovich duality, derive a tractable convex reformulations or upper bounds that can be optimized on the calibration set using a regularization penalty based on the dual norm of the router's gradients.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Mathematically guarantees robustness of the dynamic router under arbitrary test-time task-frequency shifts. Outperforms SABLE and unregularized ERM under extreme shift settings.

### Idea 2: PAC-Bayesian Temperature-Entropy Calibration over Dirichlet Parameter Space (Dirichlet-PAC)
- **Description**: PAC-ZCA optimizes a Gaussian prior and posterior over unconstrained log-temperatures. However, the routing weights lie on the probability simplex $\Delta^{K-1}$. We propose a mathematically rigorous PAC-Bayesian framework where the prior and posterior are Dirichlet distributions over the simplex itself.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Let the ensembling weights $\boldsymbol{\alpha}$ for a given query be drawn from a Dirichlet distribution:
  $$\boldsymbol{\alpha} \sim \text{Dirichlet}(\boldsymbol{\gamma})$$
  We define a Dirichlet prior $P = \text{Dirichlet}(\boldsymbol{\gamma}_0)$ and learned posterior $Q = \text{Dirichlet}(\boldsymbol{\gamma})$. Derive the exact KL divergence between two Dirichlet distributions:
  $$D_{\text{KL}}(Q || P) = \ln \frac{\Gamma(\sum_k \gamma_k)}{\Gamma(\sum_k \gamma_{0,k})} - \sum_k \ln \frac{\Gamma(\gamma_k)}{\Gamma(\gamma_{0,k})} + \sum_k (\gamma_k - \gamma_{0,k})(\psi(\gamma_k) - \psi(\sum_j \gamma_j))$$
  We minimize the PAC-Bayesian generalization bound under McAllester's theorem to learn the optimal Dirichlet concentration parameters $\boldsymbol{\gamma}$.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Ensures a mathematically consistent simplex-constrained parameter space. Prevents the "log-temperature explosion" of Gaussian formulations, significantly reducing ensembling variance and providing tight generalization guarantees.

### Idea 3: Contraction Mapping and Fixed-Point Convergence for Continuous-Depth Dynamic Merging (FixedPointMerge)
- **Description**: ChemMerge models ensembling weights as continuous-time ODE states, but lacks formal proofs of stability. We model the layer-by-layer representation update as a discrete dynamical system and use Banach's Fixed-Point Theorem to derive contraction mapping conditions.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Let the representation at layer $l$ be updated as:
  $$h^{(l)} = T(h^{(l-1)}) = h^{(l-1)} + \eta \left( \sum_{k=1}^K \alpha_k(h^{(l-1)}) \mu_k - h^{(l-1)} \right)$$
  Using Banach's Fixed-Point Theorem, we derive the exact conditions on the feedback strength $\eta$ and the Lipschitz constant of the routing function $\alpha_k$ such that the operator $T$ is a strict contraction:
  $$\|T(h_1) - T(h_2)\| \le L_T \|h_1 - h_2\| \quad \text{with} \quad L_T < 1$$
  This guarantees convergence to a unique, stable attractor representing the correct task manifold.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Provides the first formal stability guarantees for continuous-depth ensembling, eliminating representational drift and preventing activation spikes across deep layers.

### Idea 4: Information-Theoretic Rate-Distortion Bounds for Dynamic Routing (InfoRateMerge)
- **Description**: Formulate dynamic model merging as a rate-distortion optimization problem. We model the routing weights as a compressed representation of the incoming activation. The objective is to maximize routing performance (minimize distortion) while minimizing the information rate (mutual information between the early activations and the routing weights) to restrict model capacity.
- **Mathematical Foundation**:
  $$\min I(Z; \boldsymbol{\alpha}) + \beta \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}}(f_{\text{merged}}(x; \boldsymbol{\alpha}), y)]$$
  Using the variational Information Bottleneck (VIB) framework, we derive a tractable variational upper bound on the mutual information $I(Z; \boldsymbol{\alpha})$ and optimize the ensembling coefficients.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Provides a principled, information-theoretic way to control the capacity of the router, mathematically protecting against overfitting on small calibration sets.

### Idea 5: Conformal Prediction for Uncertainty-Bounded Selective Ensembling (ConformalMerge)
- **Description**: Under noisy or out-of-distribution (OOD) test streams, a standard router may confidentially dispatch queries to incorrect experts. We propose a conformal prediction framework for model ensembling. Instead of a single point-estimate of ensembling weights, we produce a mathematically guaranteed coverage set of task experts.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Define a non-conformity score based on the subspace projection coordinates:
  $$E_i = 1 - e_{y_i, i}$$
  Given a user-specified error tolerance $\epsilon \in (0, 1)$, we construct a conformal prediction set of experts $\mathcal{S}(x)$ such that:
  $$\mathbb{P}(y \in \mathcal{S}(x)) \ge 1 - \epsilon$$
  We then ensemble only the expert adapters that belong to the conformal set $\mathcal{S}(x)$, setting the weights of all other experts to zero.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Mathematically guarantees that the true task expert is included in the active ensembling set with high probability, significantly improving safety and accuracy under extreme noise and OOD queries.

### Idea 6: Lyapunov Stability and Attractor Dynamics in Recurrent Activation Blending (LyapunovMerge)
- **Description**: Reconstruct the layer-by-layer activation blending in multi-task networks as a recurrent dynamical system. We use Lyapunov stability theory to design a closed-loop controller that regulates the representation trajectory across network depth.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Define a Lyapunov energy function over the expert concentration states:
  $$V(\mathbf{C}) = -\sum_{k=1}^K \bar{C}_k \ln C_k$$
  We design the kinetic update rate equations such that the Lie derivative of $V(\mathbf{C})$ is strictly negative semi-definite ($\dot{V}(\mathbf{C}) \le 0$).
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Guarantees global asymptotic stability of the representation trajectories, completely preventing representation saturation and routing weight jitter across layers.

### Idea 7: PAC-Bayesian Generalization Bounds for Matrix-Variate Bilinear LoRA Merging (BiPACMerge)
- **Description**: Standard model merging approaches average LoRA adapter matrices linearly. However, LoRA parameters are product-form (bilinear): $W = W_0 + B A$. Linear averaging of $B$ and $A$ is highly non-linear in weight space. We propose a PAC-Bayesian framework formulated directly over the matrix-variate bilinear product-space of LoRA adapters.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Formulate a joint matrix-variate Gaussian prior and posterior over the low-rank factor matrices $B_k$ and $A_k$. Derive the exact PAC-Bayesian generalization bounds specifically accounting for the bilinear low-rank factorization structure.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Provides the first mathematically sound generalization guarantees for weight-space adapter ensembling, resolving the loose bounds of flat parameter-space averages.

### Idea 8: Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) Projection for Non-Linear Subspace Routing (Kernel-SEP)
- **Description**: PAC-ZCA uses linear PCA/SVD to project features into task-specific subspaces. However, real representation manifolds are highly non-linear. We propose projecting intermediate activations into a high-dimensional Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) using Kernel PCA.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Let $\Phi(z) \in \mathcal{H}$ be the mapping of the activation $z$ into the RKHS. We compute Kernel PCA on the calibration set using a kernel function $k(x, y) = \langle \Phi(x), \Phi(y) \rangle$. The projection coordinate is given by the RKHS norm of the projection onto the top $d$ kernel principal components. We derive generalization bounds in RKHS using kernel Rademacher complexity.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Captures highly non-linear geometric structures of task manifolds. Solves representation fragmentation under complex, non-linear deep representations where linear PCA collapses.

### Idea 9: Martingale Convergence and Bernstein Bounds for Sequential Multi-Task Serving (MartingaleMerge)
- **Description**: In streaming edge serving, input queries arrive sequentially and are often highly non-i.i.d. (e.g., sudden task switches or temporal correlation). We model the sequence of routing errors as a martingale difference sequence and apply martingale concentration inequalities.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Let $X_t$ be the indicator of a routing error at step $t$. We define the martingale sequence $S_n = \sum_{t=1}^n (X_t - \mathbb{E}[X_t | \mathcal{F}_{t-1}])$. We apply Azuma-Hoeffding and Bernstein's inequality for martingales to prove high-probability generalization bounds on the cumulative routing error rate under non-i.i.d. streaming workloads.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Provides rigorous statistical guarantees for streaming serving, explaining and bounding the error under non-stationary task shifts without relying on the i.i.d. assumption.

### Idea 10: Rademacher-Bounded Optimal Transport Projection (OT-Rademacher)
- **Description**: We frame test-time model merging as an optimal transport problem between the test stream distribution and the calibration distributions. We constrain the transport plan using a low-degree polynomial trajectory and derive Rademacher complexity bounds over the combined transport-space and trajectory-space.
- **Mathematical Foundation**: Formulate the optimal transport problem with a Kantorovich dual regularizer. Derive the empirical Rademacher complexity of the joint transport-trajectory hypothesis space to bound the generalization gap on the target test-time distribution.
- **Expected Results & Impact**: Combines the strengths of optimal transport and Rademacher trajectory regularization, providing a unified mathematical framework that is both highly robust and theoretically bounded.
