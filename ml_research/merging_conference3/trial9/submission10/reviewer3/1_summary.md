# 1_summary.md: Comprehensive Summary of the Submission

## Main Topic and Motivation
The paper addresses the challenge of test-time multi-task model serving using parameter-efficient expert adapters (such as LoRA) built on shared frozen backbones. While dynamic ensembling/blending in activation space is highly scalable, doing so at inference time with extremely scarce calibration data (typically $N \le 64$ samples per task) is prone to transductive noise overfitting. This leads to extreme temperature scales, overconfident routing, and generalization collapse. The authors propose to replace unconstrained heuristic routers with a mathematically rigorous, simplex-constrained learning-theoretic framework called **Dirichlet-PAC**.

## Proposed Approach
1. **Multi-Task Serving & Activation Blending:** Expert adapters are executed in parallel, and their activation outputs are dynamically blended at each downstream layer using sample-specific ensembling weights $\boldsymbol{\alpha}_b$ constrained to the probability simplex $\Delta^{K-1}$.
2. **Subspace Energy Projection (SEP):** To extract coordinate routing features without labels, Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) is performed on early-layer activations of a disjoint "prior calibration split" ($\mathcal{S}_{\text{prior}}$) to obtain task-specific orthonormal bases $V_{k, d} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times d}$. Query activations are projected onto these bases, and the projection energies are normalized to create a simplex-constrained coordinate system.
3. **Simplex-Constrained Dirichlet Routing Policy:** The weight vector $\boldsymbol{\alpha}_b$ is modeled as a random vector drawn from a Dirichlet posterior $Q_b$, whose concentration parameters are driven by learned, task-specific routing temperatures $\boldsymbol{\tau}$ and the SEP coordinates. A Dirichlet prior $P_b$ is established with a uniform prior temperature $\tau_0$.
4. **Analytical Dirichlet KL Complexity Control:** Under McAllester's PAC-Bayesian theorem adapted to prediction-space conditional measures, the global temperatures are optimized by minimizing a closed-form PAC-Bayesian bound on the calibration split $\mathcal{S}_{\text{opt}}$. This utilizes the exact analytical Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between Dirichlet distributions over the simplex $\Delta^{K-1}$, which is computed in closed form using Gamma and digamma functions.
5. **Unsupervised test-time serving (PEM-Div):** A fully unsupervised variant is proposed by minimizing the Prediction Entropy Minimization (PEM) loss combined with a diversity penalty (PEM-Div) over unlabeled streaming queries.

## Key Findings
- **Optimization Stability and Safety:** Under extreme data-scarcity ($N=64$), Dirichlet-PAC achieves $77.88\%$ accuracy on orthogonal and $76.32\%$ on overlapping task manifolds in a 14-layer Analytical Coordinate Sandbox, outperforming unregularized Empirical Risk Minimization (ERM) by $+1.76\%$ (orthogonal) and $+0.65\%$ (overlapping) and Gaussian PAC-ZCA by $+2.21\%$ (orthogonal) and $+0.37\%$ (overlapping). Crucially, the PAC-Bayes complexity penalty prevents temperature collapse and reduces optimization variance.
- **Unsupervised Victory:** The unsupervised PEM-Div variant achieves outstanding accuracies ($79.43\%$ orthogonal and $78.73\%$ overlapping), outperforming the supervised version. This is analyzed as a transductive semi-supervised regularizer over unlabeled streaming queries.
- **Robustness to Entanglement:** In a dense sweep over representation entanglement $\rho \in [0.0, 0.5]$, Dirichlet-PAC degrades gracefully compared to unregularized and static baselines.
- **Real-World Validation:** Physical evaluation on pre-trained BERT backbones (\texttt{bert-tiny}, \texttt{bert-mini}, \texttt{bert-medium}, \texttt{bert-base}) with Multi-LoRA adapters confirms that unregularized learned routers suffer from severe representation corruption, whereas Dirichlet-PAC and PEM-Div consistently maintain high ensembling performance (e.g., $99.33\%$ on BERT-Mini and $92.00\%$ on BERT-Base).

## Explicitly Claimed Contributions and Evidence
- **Simplex-Constrained PAC-Bayesian Theory:** The first learning-theoretic framework for test-time model ensembling that operates directly on the probability simplex $\Delta^{K-1}$. Evidence is provided through the formal mathematical setup and derivation of conditional prediction-space bounds in Section 3.
- **Analytical Dirichlet KL Complexity Control:** Closed-form complexity penalty utilizing the exact analytical KL divergence between Dirichlet distributions. Evidence is provided through the step-by-step mathematical derivation in Appendix A.
- **Unsupervised Subspace Projection with Energy Normalization:** Introduction of SEP and energy-normalization, with proven mathematical properties of basis independence and scale-invariance. Evidence is provided through the first-principles proofs of Proposition 3.1.
- **Superior and Stable Serving Performance:** Empirically verified improvements and reduced variance over unregularized ERM, PAC-ZCA, and static baselines under extreme data scarcity. Evidence is provided by results in the synthetic sandbox (Table 1 and Table 2) and physical multi-scale BERT backbones (Table 3).
