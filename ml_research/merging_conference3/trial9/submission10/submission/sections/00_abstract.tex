\begin{abstract}
Test-time multi-task model serving using parameter-efficient expert adapters (e.g., LoRA) on shared frozen backbones offers a highly scalable and cost-effective serving paradigm. However, dynamic routing and activation blending at inference time face a severe challenge: serving-time calibration streams are extremely data-scarce (often containing fewer than $64$ samples per task), making unregularized empirical routers highly susceptible to transductive noise overfitting and overconfident dispatching. To address this, we introduce \textbf{Dirichlet-PAC}, a mathematically rigorous learning-theoretic framework that guarantees generalization of dynamic ensembling over the probability simplex $\Delta^{K-1}$. Instead of unconstrained heuristic optimization, we model the ensembling weights directly as a random vector drawn from a Dirichlet distribution. Under McAllester's PAC-Bayesian theorem, we derive a closed-form generalization bound utilizing the exact analytical Kullback-Leibler divergence between Dirichlet distributions over the simplex. This formulation acts as an elegant complexity-control penalty, restricting the learned task-specific temperature parameters from exploding and naturally promoting smooth, cooperative expert blending on task boundaries. Guided by Subspace Energy Projection (SEP) for unsupervised manifold coordinate extraction, Dirichlet-PAC achieves outstanding performance under extreme data-scarce splits. In our rigorous 14-layer Analytical Coordinate Sandbox, Dirichlet-PAC improves average accuracy over standard unregularized Empirical Risk Minimization by \textbf{+1.76\%} (orthogonal) and over SABLE (SEP-Block) Norm by \textbf{+4.56\%} (orthogonal), while demonstrating remarkable grace and stability under intense task-manifold entanglement.
\end{abstract}
