# 1. Summary of the Paper

## Core Problem
This paper addresses a fundamental limitation in dynamic model ensembling and model merging, particularly when combining low-rank projection operators (such as those representing specialized task-specific representation spaces like LoRA adapters). Standard ensembling techniques (e.g., SABLE, weight averaging, task arithmetic) assume a flat Euclidean geometry. When they linearly blend projection matrices ($P_{\text{flat}} = \sum_k \alpha_k P_k$), the resulting operator violates the projection manifold's geometric properties (e.g., it is no longer idempotent, $P_{\text{flat}}^2 \neq P_{\text{flat}}$). This leads to **eigenvalue shrinkage**, where the eigenvalues decay strictly below 1. As representations propagate through multiple sequential layers in deep neural networks, this decay compounds exponentially, causing **projected coordinate collapse** (representation norms decay to zero) and a catastrophic loss in downstream performance (dropping to random-guess levels).

## Proposed Solution: C-Lie-MM
The authors propose **Continuous Riemannian-Geometric Homotopical Model Merging via Grassmannian Geodesic Blending (C-Lie-MM)**. This framework treats task-specific projection bases as points on the curved **Grassmannian Manifold** $\mathcal{G}(d, D)$—the space of all $d$-dimensional subspaces in $\mathbb{R}^D$.
Key components of C-Lie-MM include:
1. **Fixed Coordinate Reference Point ($Y_0$):** To resolve the differentiability discontinuities (the "argmax jump" or "step-function boundary instability") of standard dynamic barycenter methods, C-Lie-MM utilizes a fixed, pre-computed reference point $Y_0 \in \mathcal{G}(d, D)$. This reference is computed offline as the unique centroid under the topologically equivalent projection (chordal) metric of the expert bases.
2. **Offline Logarithmic Mapping:** Task-specific orthonormal bases $\{V_k\}_{k=1}^K$ are mapped onto the tangent space $T_{Y_0}\mathcal{G}(d, D)$ offline via a cut-locus-aware closed-form logarithm map: $H_k = \log_{Y_0}(V_k)$. This step is computationally decoupled from online inference.
3. **Gibbs Soft Gating Policy:** A temperature-calibrated Gibbs policy evaluates sample-specific routing weights $\alpha_k(z_b)$ based on activation projection norms.
4. **Online Geodesic Tangent Blending:** Dynamic ensembling of tangent matrices is performed in the flat tangent space: $H_{\text{merged}}(\alpha) = \sum_k \alpha_k H_k$.
5. **Online Exponential Mapping:** The blended tangent matrix is projected back onto the manifold via the Grassmannian exponential map: $Y_{\text{merged}} = \exp_{Y_0}(H_{\text{merged}})$. This ensures that $P_{\text{merged}} = Y_{\text{merged}}Y_{\text{merged}}^T$ is guaranteed to be strictly symmetric, idempotent, and of rank $d$ at all times.
6. **Bypassing SVD with Polynomial Approximations:** To solve serving latency bottlenecks on edge hardware, the authors derive a square-root-free, coordinate-free matrix trigonometric formulation of the exponential map. It approximates the map using 4th-order or 6th-order Taylor series or Chebyshev polynomials, replacing online SVD with fast matrix multiplications (GEMMs) on tiny $d \times d$ matrices.

## Key Claims and Contributions
- **Formal Proofs:** Mathematically proves eigenvalue shrinkage (Proposition 3.1) and exponential norm decay (Proposition 3.2) under flat ensembling, as well as manifold preservation (Theorem 3.3) and differentiability (Theorem 3.4) of C-Lie-MM. It also derives a strict tangent space metric distortion bound (Proposition 3.3) and Chebyshev approximation bounds (Proposition 3.5).
- **Empirical Validation:**
  - In a **14-layer Analytical Coordinate Sandbox**, C-Lie-MM achieves $70.30\% \pm 4.01\%$ accuracy under overlapping manifolds, completely preventing the coordinate collapse of uniform merging ($25.00\%$) and outperforming flat ensembling baselines.
  - In a **Simulated GLUE LoRA Benchmark** (matching RoBERTa-Large hidden dimensions), C-Lie-MM achieves near-oracle average accuracy ($97.0\%$), outperforming SABLE and TIES-merging ($55.0\%$) by $+42.0\%$ absolute.
- **Systems-Level Advantages:** Showcases perfect immunity to heterogeneity collapse (maintains identical performance under homogeneous and heterogeneous sequence streams) and analyzes latency/memory overhead (showing $12.6\times$ speedup using order-6 Chebyshev polynomial expansions, reducing latency overhead to just $0.03$ ms on GPU). It also releases a complete, syntactically correct Triton GPU kernel.
- **OOD Robustness:** Proves that under extreme out-of-distribution uncertainty, routing weights distribute evenly ($\alpha_k \to 1/K$), causing the blended tangent matrix to approach zero, which projects the representation directly onto the robust Karcher mean centroid $Y_0$.
