\begin{abstract}
Test-time ensembling of parameter-efficient expert adapters (e.g., LoRA) under dynamic, non-stationary workloads is vital for resource-constrained, multi-task edge serving. However, existing state-of-the-art dynamic routing mechanisms suffer from severe layer-to-layer ensembling weight jitter, causing representational instability and disrupting activation propagation coherence across deep networks. To resolve this fundamental limitation, we present \textbf{GraviMerge}, a mathematically rigorous, physics-informed model merging paradigm inspired by classical mechanics and orbital dynamics. By mapping intermediate network activations to a moving spacecraft coordinate probe on the unit hypersphere and task adapters to high-mass stellar attractors, GraviMerge incorporates second-order physical inertia, geodesic integration, and parallel transport to naturally smooth representation trajectories across network depth. Rigorous evaluation across $10$ independent seeds on a Projected Digit Representation Space (RDS) Proxy benchmark demonstrates that GraviMerge completely resolves the accuracy-stability dilemma, achieving the highest joint serving accuracy (\textbf{88.69\%}) while reducing layer-to-layer ensembling weight jitter by \textbf{6.01$\times$} compared to ChemMerge, \textbf{5.47$\times$} compared to first-order EMA, and \textbf{2.40$\times$} compared to SABLE, offering high resilience to dynamic serving workloads.
\end{abstract}
