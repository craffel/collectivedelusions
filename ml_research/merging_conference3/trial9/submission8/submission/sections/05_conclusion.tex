\section{Conclusion and Future Horizons}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this work, we introduced \textbf{GraviMerge}, a novel, physics-informed dynamic model merging paradigm that maps deep representation trajectories to a virtual spacecraft probe traversing a softened gravitational field. By integrating Newtonian mechanics on spherical manifolds, GraviMerge introduces physical inertia, velocity, and viscous drag into deep weight routing, preserving geometric invariants via exact geodesic exponential maps and parallel transport. Across $10$ seeds on the RDS benchmark, GraviMerge achieves the highest serving accuracy (\textbf{88.69\%}) while reducing routing jitter by \textbf{6.01$\times$} compared to ChemMerge and \textbf{5.47$\times$} compared to EMA, completely resolving the accuracy-stability dilemma.

Looking ahead, we envision modeling representation flows through relativistic metric deflections, analyzing representational refraction, and exploring harmonic orbital resonance to construct cooperative, self-stabilizing multi-model serving networks (see Section~\ref{sec:appendix_future} for detailed discussion on empirical profiling, adaptive drag, and geometric generalizations). Ultimately, we hope this work inspires researchers to build stable, robust deep learning controllers grounded in physical and geometric principles.
