# 2. Novelty Check

## Assessment of Key Novel Aspects
The paper introduces several highly novel concepts, both theoretical and empirical:
1. **Discrete-Time Dynamical Systems Treatment of Sequential Routing**: Framing the joint evolution of representation vectors and routing coefficients across network layers as a dynamical feedback system. While analyzing deep networks as dynamical systems has been done (e.g., in Neural ODEs), doing so specifically for joint representation-routing maps in multi-task ensembling is a novel and powerful formulation.
2. **Derivation of Joint representation-routing Lipschitz Bounds (Theorem 3.1 & 3.2)**: This represents the first explicit contraction mapping framework for deep sequential model ensembling. The proof elegantly binds the joint Lipschitz constant through a product-rule style decomposition of the representation and the Softmax routing projection.
3. **Joint Spectral-Temperature Regularization Penalty**: Prior works often use standard $L_2$ weight decay (regularizing weights only). The paper highlights a major, mathematically grounded insight: without bounding the inverse temperature, the temperature $\tau_l$ can collapse to zero, rendering the routing function discontinuous (infinite Lipschitz constant). The proposed joint penalty on both the Frobenius norm of routing weights and the inverse squared temperature ($1/\tau_l^2$) is mathematically required to guarantee contraction, which is a novel conceptual contribution.
4. **Update-Space Quasi-Contraction**: Recognizing that standard residual connections (where the identity mapping has a Lipschitz constant of 1) cannot form strict contractions, the authors propose a practical theoretical relaxation. By regularizing the update operator $U_l$ such that $L_{U_l} < \epsilon$, they stabilize gating trajectories without modifying frozen backbone parameters. This is highly novel and bridges the gap between functional analysis and practical large-scale frozen model serving.
5. **Adaptive Test-Time Temperature Annealing**: Decoupling optimization stability during calibration (which benefits from a smooth, contractive landscape) from inference-time sharpness (which benefits from categorical, sharp routing) by scaling down the temperature post-hoc. This simple yet highly effective technique completely resolves the "expert dilution" dilemma.
6. **Label-Free Online Tuning Heuristics**: Formulating three differentiable, label-free metrics (Gating Depth-Variance, Shannon Gating Entropy, and Running Gating Lipschitz Bound) to guide hyperparameter tuning under data scarcity.

## The 'Delta' from Prior Work
- **ChemMerge \cite{chemmerge2025kinetics}**: Uses continuous-time kinetics equations to smooth trajectories. This is primarily a heuristic approximation and lacks formal convergence or stability proofs. CR-Router provides a solid mathematical foundation to explain *why* representations converge. Additionally, ChemMerge requires prototype-based routing which adds substantial computational overhead.
- **SABLE \cite{sable2025samplewise}**: Uses non-parametric nearest-centroid projections. It is highly accurate but requires storing and computing nearest-neighbor distances across the entire class prototype matrix at every layer, which is extremely expensive in terms of memory and latency. CR-Router is a parametric learned router that bypasses this, offering a significant throughput advantage.
- **L2-Fixed Router**: Evaluated as a strong baseline, L2-Fixed uses standard $L_2$ weight decay but keeps the temperature fixed at a sharp value ($\tau = 0.05$). Under depth-heterogeneous settings, this heuristic fails (achieving only 38.98% classification accuracy in Exp 1 and 35.23% in Exp 2, compared to CR-Router's 53.35% and 43.48%). This delta highlights the absolute necessity of joint spectral-temperature contraction regularizers.
- **Shared Router**: Uses a single routing head across all layers. CR-Router maintains depth-wise flexibility (layer-specific routing parameters) while guaranteeing trajectory convergence, outperforming Shared Router by +14.68% absolute accuracy under overlapping subspaces.

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this paper is **significant**. Instead of introducing another empirical heuristic or trajectory-smoothing trick, it builds a rigorous, first-principles mathematical framework to analyze sequential ensembling. It then translates this theory directly into a highly successful regularized objective function and a series of practical, robust training/inference strategies (centroid-based warm-starting, test-time annealing, online tuning heuristics) that yield massive empirical improvements. The synthesis of deep mathematical theory and highly practical, high-throughput model serving engineering makes the novelty exceptionally strong and impactful.
