### 1. Literature Review & Context Mapping
We reviewed the current ecosystem of dynamic model merging, activation blending, and on-device serving:
- **SABLE (Sample-wise Activation Blending of Low-Rank Experts):** Dynamically blends expert activations using cosine similarity between intermediate features and task classification heads.
- **SPS-ZCA (Single-Pass Sample-Wise Routing with Zero-Shot Centroid Alignment):** Resolves the temporal paradox by pre-computing task centroids in the early representation space (Layer 3) and performing zero-shot alignment. Incorporates Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC) and Intra-Task Dispersion Calibration (IDC) to handle representation drift and asymmetric task manifold variance, and utilizes GMMs for out-of-distribution (OOD) rejection.
- **ChemMerge (Non-Equilibrium Chemical Kinetics):** Introduces continuous state tracking (modeled after reaction kinetics / EMA low-pass filtering) to resolve layer-to-layer routing weight jitter and representation spikes.
- **Q-SPS (Quantized Activation-Space Blending):** Quantizes LoRA experts to INT8/INT4 and implements conditional gating to skip executing experts under a coefficient threshold ($\theta = 0.01$).

### 2. Brainstorming 10 Novel Pragmatic Ideas
In accordance with our **Pragmatist** persona, we brainstormed 10 novel research ideas focusing strictly on real-world edge deployment, latency reduction, memory optimization, and environmental robustness.

#### Idea 1: Dynamic Expert Early Exit (DE-Exit)
- **Core Concept:** SABLE and SPS-ZCA execute parallel expert adapters across all adapted layers. However, representation consensus is often achieved in early-to-mid adapted layers. Once the Shannon entropy of the routing distribution falls below a threshold $\epsilon$ and stabilizes for $H$ consecutive layers, we freeze the routing coefficient and bypass all subsequent adapter paths, running only the shared base backbone.
- **Expected Results:** Reductions of up to 40% in average adapter FLOPs with negligible (<0.2%) accuracy drop.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Drastically reduces edge serving latency and energy consumption during multi-task stream serving.

#### Idea 2: Resource-Budgeted Top-M Expert Serving (RB-TopM)
- **Core Concept:** Edge devices face fluctuating workloads and battery states. We propose a router that dynamically adjusts the active expert budget ($M$) and skips adapter paths on-the-fly based on a system-level hardware latency or battery budget (e.g., fallback from soft blending $M=3$ to hard routing $M=1$ under low battery).
- **Expected Results:** Smooth, graceful degradation of task accuracy with strict, guaranteed execution latency bounds.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Enables highly predictable, battery-safe serving on real-world microcontrollers and edge chips.

#### Idea 3: Task-Specific Layer Pruning (TS-Prune)
- **Core Concept:** Different PEFT adapters display varying layer-wise importance. During offline calibration, we measure the relative contribution (activation norm) of each adapter at each layer. At test-time, we completely skip executing adapter paths for layers where their expected contribution is below a threshold.
- **Expected Results:** Bypasses up to 35% of low-rank computation while preserving the overall performance ceiling.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Drastically reduces DRAM-to-SRAM weight transfer bandwidth and execution latency without any retraining.

#### Idea 4: Quantization-Calibrated Centroid Alignment (QCCA)
- **Core Concept:** Low-bitwidth quantization (INT4/INT8) shifts model representations, causing severe routing coordinate misalignment when using FP32 centroids. We propose a calibration method that re-aligns task centroids in the quantized activation space to neutralize this representation shift.
- **Expected Results:** Restores routing accuracy of quantized models directly to the unquantized FP32 ceiling.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Enables ultra-low footprint serving without accuracy degradation under extreme quantization.

#### Idea 5: Temporal Inertia Routing for Video Streams (TI-Route)
- **Core Concept:** In continuous streaming applications (like video or sensor feeds), task identity exhibits high temporal coherence. We introduce a temporal momentum/inertia term into the sample-wise routing coefficient update, preventing high-frequency routing jitter caused by sensor noise.
- **Expected Results:** Eliminates high-frequency layer-wise and frame-wise routing oscillations.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Crucial for physical stability and smooth predictions in video analytics and autonomous systems.

#### Idea 6: Noise-Robust Subspace Projection (NRSP)
- **Core Concept:** Nearest-centroid early routing degrades under real-world input corruption (e.g., JPEG artifacts, camera blur, motion noise). We propose projecting early-stage features onto a noise-invariant subspace computed via contrastive calibration before performing nearest-centroid alignment.
- **Expected Results:** Maintains high classification accuracy (>95% of clean performance) even under severe pixel-level noise.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Highly critical for reliable real-world edge deployments in unpredictable environments.

#### Idea 7: Quantized Gaussian Mixture Models for Edge OOD (Q-GMM)
- **Core Concept:** Coordinate GMMs for out-of-distribution (OOD) rejection require expensive floating-point exponentiation. We propose an integer-only, quantized GMM that evaluates coordinate log-likelihoods using bit-shifts and pre-computed integer log tables.
- **Expected Results:** 10x reduction in OOD evaluation latency with zero change in the OOD detection boundary.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Makes robust OOD protection viable on ultra-low-power microcontrollers and specialized NPUs.

#### Idea 8: Feedback-Driven Running Task Priors (FDRP)
- **Core Concept:** Real-world workloads often arrive in contiguous blocks of the same task. We introduce a feedback loop that uses a running average of recent logits to construct a dynamic task prior, boosting routing confidence and stability on structured task streams.
- **Expected Results:** Faster convergence to correct experts and higher overall routing stability on task blocks.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Tailor-made for structured industrial and diagnostic streaming workloads.

#### Idea 9: Memory-Optimized Dual-Precision Expert Blending (MDEP)
- **Core Concept:** Executing multiple parallel adapters in high precision consumes high memory bandwidth. We propose a dual-precision scheme: the top-1 expert adapter is executed in INT8, while the secondary blending experts are executed in ultra-low INT4 precision or dynamically skipped.
- **Expected Results:** Saves up to 50% of expert activation memory bandwidth while matching FP32 blending accuracy.
- **Pragmatic Impact:** Maximizes serving throughput and SRAM utilization under heavy multi-task workloads.

#### Idea 10: Layer-wise Adaptive Scaling of Expert Interventions (LASER)
- **Core Concept:** Instead of executing all adapters uniformly across all adapted layers, we dynamically scale down the influence of secondary experts at deeper layers where feature specialization is high, preventing representation dilution and stabilizing activations.
- **Expected Results:** Sharper, cleaner activations at semantic layers, preventing "activation bleeding" and representation saturation.
Pragmatic Impact: Restores performance under highly fine-grained or overlapping task domains.
