\begin{abstract}
Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) via low-rank adapters (LoRA) enables the deployment of specialized task-specific experts on a shared pre-trained backbone. However, dynamically ensembling these experts on a sample-by-sample basis at intermediate layers---known as dynamic layer-wise merging---often suffers from severe \emph{transductive overfitting} when calibrated on ultra-low data regimes ($N = 16$). Unregularized empirical risk minimization (ERM) yields high-frequency parameter oscillations and unstable generalization across network depth. 
In this work, we present a rigorous learning-theoretic framework, \textbf{PAC-Bayesian Smooth Trajectory Merging (PAC-STM)}, to establish mathematical guarantees over deep ensembling trajectories. By modeling layer-wise routing log-temperatures under a Markovian random walk prior, we derive an exact, closed-form Kullback-Leibler (KL) complexity penalty that mathematically drives first-order finite-difference smoothness. 
Under a simulated 14-layer Analytical Coordinate Sandbox, PAC-STM mitigates transductive overfitting, outperforming unregularized ERM by up to $2.05\%$ in heterogeneous batch accuracy, while exhibiting complete, simulation-validated immunity to both \emph{Heterogeneity Collapse} and \emph{Vectorization Collapse} (which destroys GPU tensor parallelism under sequential multi-task execution) on mixed multi-task streaming workloads.
\end{abstract}
