# Experimental Check and Evaluation: PAC-STM

## Experimental Setup and Datasets
- **Simulated 14-layer Analytical Coordinate Sandbox (ICS):** The main quantitative evaluation (Tables 1 & 2, Figures 1 & 2) is conducted in a high-fidelity 14-layer simulated sandbox. The sandbox is parameterized to reflect standard feature spreads and accuracies typical of MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN. While the sandbox enables highly controlled, isolated studies of transductive overfitting, routing trajectories, and noise levels (orthogonal vs. overlapping), it is still a synthetic environment, which is a minor empirical weakness.
- **Real-World Validation on Pre-trained Vision Transformer (\texttt{ViT-B/16}):** To bridge the simulation-to-reality gap, the authors conduct a real-world validation using a pre-trained \texttt{ViT-B/16} backbone on a heterogeneous mixed stream of MNIST and CIFAR-10 classification tasks. While this real-world validation is highly valuable, it is relatively small-scale (only 2 tasks, MNIST and CIFAR-10, which are highly separable) and on clean, low-resolution datasets.

## Baseline Analysis and Missing Citations
- **Extensive Baseline Suite:** The paper evaluates an excellent, comprehensive range of baselines:
  - **Weight-Space Merging:** QWS-Merge, Linear Router, Parameter-Free Subspace Routing (PFSR).
  - **Activation-Blending Routers:** SABLE (Block), SABLE (PCA), PAC-ZCA (Global), Temp-Only ERM.
  - **Oracle & Uniform:** Serving as upper and lower performance bounds.
- **Critical Citation Gaps:** 
  - Although **SABLE (Block)**, **SABLE (PCA)**, and **PAC-ZCA (Global)** are positioned in Section 1, Section 2, and Section 4 as "existing systems" or "prior methods" and are used as central baselines, the paper **completely fails to cite any publications or provide references** for them in the text or bibliography. This lack of bibliographic attribution is a major oversight, making it impossible to locate the original works or verify if the baseline implementations are faithful to prior literature.
  - The paper completely overlooks the highly related, contemporary theoretical work **"Model Merging is Secretly Certifiable: Non-Vacuous Generalisation Bounds for Low-Shot Learning" (Kim et al., UAI 2026 / arXiv:2505.15798)**. Since both works apply PAC-Bayesian theory to model merging in low-shot data regimes, the lack of discussion and citation of Kim et al. represents a major gap in the scholarly contextualization of the contribution.

## Support for Central Claims
The empirical results provide exceptionally strong, clear, and statistically sound support for the paper's central claims:
1. **Mitigation of Transductive Overfitting:** In Table 1, PAC-STM achieves $73.62\% \pm 1.48\%$ under heterogeneous batching ($B=16$), outperforming unregularized Temp-Only ERM ($71.57\% \pm 1.50\%$) by **2.05%** in the Orthogonal Sandbox configuration. The authors go beyond simple mean reporting by executing a paired t-test across individual seeds, proving that the improvement is highly statistically significant ($p < 0.008$ in both Orthogonal and Overlapping settings).
2. **Absolute Collapse Immunity:** Under heterogeneous batching ($B=16$), all weight-space merging techniques (QWS-Merge, Linear Router, PFSR) collapse catastrophically to near-random levels ($\approx 39\% - 40\%$), verifying the **Heterogeneity Collapse** claim. PAC-STM achieves complete immunity, maintaining near-Oracle performance. Furthermore, under single-sequence heterogeneous serving ($B=1$), PAC-STM maintains high accuracy ($73.15\% \pm 0.79\%$), whereas weight-space routers suffer from **Vectorization Collapse** (sequential execution on GPUs).
3. **Trajectory Smoothing Verification:** Figure 2 visually demonstrates that PAC-STM's learned layer-wise temperatures are smooth and stable across depth, whereas unregularized ERM oscillates wildly. This qualitative finding is empirically validated on real-world ViT-B/16 activations in Section 4.5, where PAC-STM reduces trajectory roughness by almost **3 times** compared to the ERM baseline ($0.109547$ vs. $0.275478$ smoothness score) while preserving the same classification accuracy ($86.25\%$).
4. **Non-linear Kernel PCA Validation:** In Section 4.6 (Table 4), the uncentered Kernel PCA projection (UN-KPCA-SEP) achieves $51.98\% \pm 1.82\%$ accuracy under severe representational non-linearity, outperforming Linear PCA ($45.35\% \pm 0.66\%$) by **+6.63%** ($p < 0.01$). This validates that the local Kernel PCA projection can successfully untangle curved manifolds. Crucially, the centered Kernel PCA fails catastrophically ($24.62\% \pm 0.79\%$), verifying the theoretical claim that kernel centering destroys the task centroid identity.
5. **Skip-Aware Prior Validation:** In Section 4.7 (Table 5), the residual-skip prior topology outperforms the sequential prior by **+1.05%** absolute accuracy ($65.70\% \pm 2.15\%$ vs. $64.65\% \pm 0.70\%$) while reducing trajectory roughness by 3.33%, validating that incorporating skip-aware priors provides a superior inductive bias for residual networks.

## Empirical Gaps
- **Lack of LLM Experiments:** In the introduction, the paper heavily motivates its framework using the rise of Large Language Models (LLMs), text generation, multi-task streaming serving workloads, and PEFT via LoRA. However, the actual empirical validation is limited to image classification (simulated image manifolds and a pre-trained Vision Transformer). The lack of any direct NLP/LLM experiments (e.g., LLaMA-7B on instruction-following or arithmetic benchmarks) creates a minor discrepancy between the paper's heavy LLM-focused motivation and its empirical validation.
