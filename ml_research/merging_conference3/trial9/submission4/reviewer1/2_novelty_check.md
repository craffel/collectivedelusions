# Intermediate Evaluation 2: Novelty and Prior Work Comparison

## Assessment of Key Novel Aspects
The primary novelty of this paper is **conceptual, diagnostic, and methodological**:
1. **Deconstruction of the Biochemical Metaphor:** Rather than proposing an even more complex model, the paper applies Occam's razor to strip away the complex physical apparatus of the previous state-of-the-art model, ChemMerge. By mathematically proving that ChemMerge's continuous rate equations simplify to a simple Exponential Moving Average (EMA) under standard discretization and simplex conservation constraints, the paper reveals that the biochemical rate equations, Arrhenius activation barriers, and ODE solvers are mathematically redundant.
2. **First Systematic Analysis of Routing Jitter and the Accuracy-Stability Trade-off:** While routing consistency across depth has been observed as a general challenge in the sparse MoE training literature, this paper is the first to systematically characterize and map the **Accuracy-Stability trade-off** in training-free dynamic model ensembling. It rigorously defines a routing trajectory stability metric (Routing Jitter) and shows how a single parameter $\beta$ controls this trade-off from absolute local plasticity ($\beta=0$) to static uniform merging ($\beta=1$).
3. **Advanced Minimalist Variants:** The introduction of:
   - **Layer-wise Centroid Calibration:** Addresses representational rotation and shift across depth, aligning similarities inside local representation manifolds.
   - **Raw Boundary Initialization:** Solves the transient lag issue of standard recurrences (such as a uniform prior) by starting the EMA in its stationary state, leading to a massive drop in routing jitter.

## The 'Delta' from Prior Work
- **Delta from SABLE (Stateless):** SABLE calculates routing weights independently at each layer, making it highly sensitive to representational noise. Momentum-Merge introduces statefulness across depth using an EMA filter, carrying over task-specific ensembling weights across layers to act as a low-pass filter on representational noise and suppress layer-to-layer ensembling weight oscillations.
- **Delta from ChemMerge (Stateful Biochemical):** ChemMerge tracks state variables using a first-order chemical ODE integrated via numerical exponential solvers, requiring multiple uninterpretable hyperparameters (activation energies, virtual step size, decay rates). Momentum-Merge replaces this entire continuous-time biochemical metaphor with a single discrete EMA equation, requiring only one hyperparameter ($\beta$) and zero numerical solver overhead.

## Characterization of Novelty
Although the mathematical operator employed (the Exponential Moving Average) is highly standard and classic, the paper's novelty should be characterized as **significant and highly valuable**:
- **De-escalation of Complexity:** In modern deep learning research, there is a prominent trend of wrapping simple mathematical operations in complex, pseudo-physical metaphors to make them appear more novel or rigorous. This paper performs the rare and highly original service of de-escalating this complexity, mathematically proving that a simple momentum update can match or exceed the performance of a highly complex biochemical ODE solver.
- **Rigor of Ablation and Bounds Analysis:** Rather than just claiming equivalence, the authors conduct a deep, rigorous mathematical check, delineating the exact theoretical boundaries where the equivalence holds (uniform activation energies) and where it is challenged (non-uniform activation energies / task-asymmetric noise). They systematically stress-test these boundaries under task-asymmetric noise regimes, proving that even when the equivalence is challenged, the constant-inertia EMA remains the superior engineering choice due to its extreme stability.
- **Practical Engineering Impact:** By demonstrating that a single line of standard code can replace an entire ODE numerical solver, the paper provides immense practical utility for low-latency, resource-constrained edge serving of deep neural networks.
