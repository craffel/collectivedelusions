# 3. Soundness and Methodology

## Clarity of the Description
The methodology is exceptionally clear, precise, and well-structured. The authors formalize the dynamic PEFT serving problem clearly, providing concrete mathematical expressions for activation blending (Eq. 1), unit-norm similarity matching (Eq. 2 & 3), and layer-wise centroid calibration (Eq. 5). The description of the core Momentum-Merge dynamics (Eq. 4) is wonderfully parsimonious, requiring exactly one equation and a single constant momentum parameter $\beta$.

The mathematical deconstruction of the ChemMerge biochemical SOTA (Section 3.5 and Theorem 3.1) is a model of expository clarity. It walks the reader step-by-step through the process of simplifying the complex continuous reactor ODE (biochemical kinetics, Arrhenius reaction rates, decay rates, Euler discretization) into the simplex-conserving constant EMA of Momentum-Merge. The authors are careful to point out the theoretical boundary of this deconstruction (i.e., when activation energies are non-uniform across tasks), ensuring a high degree of mathematical honesty.

## Appropriateness of Methods
The proposed method, Momentum-Merge, is highly appropriate and elegant for resolving the problem of layer-to-layer routing weight oscillations (routing jitter). 
* **Elegant and Simple:** Rather than introducing complex continuous-time ordinary differential equation (ODE) solvers or non-equilibrium biochemical rate equations (which require virtual time step-size boundaries and heavy system-level integration overhead), the authors apply a standard Exponential Moving Average (EMA). This is a classical, computationally lightweight, and mathematically pure low-pass filter.
* **Residual Representation Continuity:** The authors highlight a key physical insight: modern Transformer architectures already carry task-relevant representations forward across depth via standard residual and skip connections. Consequently, intermediate states exhibit representational continuity, which justifies why a simple constant EMA filter on ensembling weights is highly effective.
* **Low System Overhead:** Because the recurrence is applied sample-by-sample and layer-by-layer, it requires $O(1)$ additional storage (storing only the routing weights of the previous layer) and zero extra training, making it highly suitable for production serving.

## Analysis of Technical Flaws and Limitations
The authors are extremely thorough and honest in identifying and evaluating potential limitations and edge cases:
1. **Ecological Validity (Synthetic Sandbox):** The primary limitation is that the main evaluation is situated within the coordinate-aligned Analytical Coordinate Sandbox (ICS). While the sandbox is highly controlled and standardized, it is a simplified model of a real-world Transformer. Crucially, the authors fully address this in Appendix B by outlining a concrete mathematical scaling trajectory (incorporating layer-wise centroid anchoring, layer-wise temperature scaling, and depth-wise momentum modulation) and proposing an actionable experimental protocol on LLaMA-7B/Mistral-7B.
2. **Recurrence Trapping (Appendix B.5):** The authors identify a key vulnerability in the Advanced Momentum-Merge variant when calibration data is extremely scarce ($|\mathcal{C}_k| \le 8$). In this regime, noisy centroids lead to inaccurate initial routing weights. Because the stateful system possesses temporal momentum memory, this initial boundary error propagates through network depth and traps ensembling coefficients in highly sub-optimal states throughout the forward pass (collapsing joint accuracy). This is a highly insightful and mathematically sound analysis, establishing that stateful systems require a minimum calibration size ($|\mathcal{C}_k| \ge 16$) to avoid trapping.
3. **Task-Asymmetric Noise Regimes (Appendix F):** The authors stress-test their uniform-inertia assumption against ChemMerge's state-dependent, dynamic-inertia rates. They honestly demonstrate that under extreme noise asymmetry, ChemMerge's dynamic kinetics offer a minor joint accuracy buffer of $+0.15\%$ to $+0.30\%$ absolute, because it dynamically applies heavier localized smoothing to the noisy task. However, they also demonstrate that this minor accuracy benefit comes at a catastrophic cost in routing stability (causing ChemMerge's routing jitter to surge rapidly), whereas Momentum-Merge maintains near-zero jitter while remaining highly competitive.

## Reproducibility
The reproducibility of this paper is outstanding:
* All mathematical equations are fully defined with clear variable declarations.
* The physical parameters of the Analytical Coordinate Sandbox (ICS) are explicitly specified: 14 layers, representation dimension $D = 192$, shared layers $L_{\text{frozen}} = 3$, rank $r = 8$, task manifolds $K = 4$, 1000 sequential samples, noise scales, and standard deviations.
* Optimal hyperparameters for all evaluated methods are clearly provided (e.g., $\beta = 0.60$ and Softmax temperature $\tau = 0.005$ for Momentum-Merge).
* Systematic sweeps over key parameters (momentum $\beta$, Softmax temperature $\tau$, calibration subset size $|\mathcal{C}_k|$, layer noise scales $\sigma$) are provided in the appendices with detailed tabular data, making it straightforward for an expert reader to replicate and verify the findings.
