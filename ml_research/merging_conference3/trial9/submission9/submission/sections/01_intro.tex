\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The proliferation of task-specific, Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) adapters---most notably Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) \cite{hu2021lora}---has transformed how modern deep learning backbones are served. Rather than deploying multiple massive, monolithic models for disparate tasks, contemporary edge and cloud serving systems deploy a single shared pre-trained backbone alongside a fleet of light-weight adapters \cite{sheng2023slora, chen2023punica}. At test-time, when serving sequential multi-task workloads, the challenge shifts from training to dynamic model ensembling, where the serving system must route incoming queries to the most appropriate task experts in real-time.

To avoid the excessive latency of sequential multi-model inference, recent state-of-the-art frameworks have proposed single-pass dynamic routing techniques, such as Sample-wise Activation Blending (SABLE) \cite{sable_2024} and Single-Pass Model Merging with Zero-Shot Centroid Alignment (SPS-ZCA) \cite{zhang24spszca}. These methods extract coordinate representation signals from early backbone layers and dynamically blend LoRA activations in subsequent layers in a single forward pass, maintaining flat $O(1)$ backbone execution latency.

Despite their empirical success, existing dynamic routing methods suffer from a fundamental limitation: they are \emph{stateless}. Under sequential serving streams, queries arrive in continuous successions that often exhibit temporal correlation or task-homogeneity over local windows. Because stateless routers process each query independently, query-level feature noise causes the router to fluctuate wildly between experts. This phenomenon, which we term the \textbf{routing jitter paradox}, introduces high-frequency oscillations in the activation blending space, destabilizing representation flows across layers and degrading the overall generalization capacity of the backbone.

\paragraph{The Cascading Representation Collapse in Deep Networks.} This instability is particularly critical in deep cascaded architectures, such as Large Language Models (LLMs) or deep Vision Transformers (ViTs). In deep networks, minor routing fluctuations and representation-space mismatches at early layers propagate, amplify, and cascade across dozens of subsequent attention and feed-forward blocks. This triggers a catastrophic downstream representation collapse, where intermediate features drift entirely out of the pretrained backbone's manifold. While shallow architectures (such as 3-layer MLPs) are highly expressive and can often absorb minor representation deviations at the classification head, deep networks possess no such resilience, making early-stage stateful representation smoothing mathematically and systems-level indispensable. Formally framing and mitigating this cascading representation collapse in deep networks serves as our primary systems-level motivation for stateful representation-space smoothing.

To alleviate this, heuristic stateful ensembling frameworks like ChemMerge \cite{chemmerge_2026} have treated model ensembling as a continuous-time multi-component chemical reactor. By utilizing first-order chemical reaction kinetics differential equations, ChemMerge acts as a low-pass filter over raw coordinate signals, achieving remarkable trajectory smoothness (low jitter). However, ChemMerge is a purely heuristic approach. It lacks any mathematical stability or out-of-sample generalization guarantees. Its parameters are set statically or via trial-and-error, making it highly fragile under heterogeneous streams, where its rigid kinetics can cause severe accuracy collapses (down to $70.59\%$, as shown in our experiments).

In this work, we argue that empirical success in stateful serving is incomplete without a rigorous mathematical foundation. We completely reject heuristic parameter selection in favor of a unified learning-theoretic and dynamical systems framework. We present \textbf{PAC-Kinetics}, a framework that formalizes continuous-time stateful routing as a stochastic dynamical system and derives a strict, provable generalization bound under non-i.i.d. streaming conditions.

By mapping representation trajectories to a multi-component chemical reactor, we establish a continuous-time state equation whose discretized recurrence represents a contractive state transition operator. We prove that our stateful kinetics router is globally asymptotically stable (GAS) and input-to-state stable (ISS) under a quadratic Lyapunov candidate function. Furthermore, since sequential query streams violate the standard independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) assumption of classical machine learning theory, we analyze our router through the lens of stationary stochastic processes. We derive a novel, parameter-space Catoni-type PAC-Bayesian bound for stationary $\beta$-mixing dependent sequences. We directly minimize this generalization bound via gradient descent to learn the optimal kinetics and routing parameters on a disjoint calibration stream.

Through comprehensive evaluation inside our Coordinates Sandbox, we demonstrate that PAC-Kinetics successfully resolves the accuracy-stability trade-off:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Mathematical Rigor}: We replace empirical heuristics with provable contractive and Lyapunov stability guarantees (Section \ref{sec:stability}), alongside a learning-theoretic generalization bound under non-i.i.d. conditions (Section \ref{sec:pac_bound}).
    \item \textbf{Extreme Jitter Reduction}: Under homogeneous streams, PAC-Kinetics slashes routing jitter by over \textbf{11.2$\times$} on orthogonal streams (from 0.0697 to 0.0062) and up to \textbf{16.0$\times$} on overlapping streams compared to stateless SABLE, matching the smoothness of heuristic ChemMerge.
    \item \textbf{Stateful Robustness and the Stateful-Stateless Trade-Off}: While heuristic ChemMerge collapses to $70.59\%$ accuracy under heterogeneous streams due to rigid kinetic parameters, PAC-Kinetics remains highly robust, achieving a high \textbf{92.35\%} accuracy on orthogonal streams. We explicitly acknowledge and highlight the fundamental trade-off of stateful serving: when query streams are completely uncorrelated and independent, stateful memory acts as a liability (introducing "inertial drag" or routing lag that mis-routes current queries based on obsolete history). Under such rapid, uncorrelated switches, stateless routers (e.g., PAC-ZCA at $94.39\%$) represent the optimal choice. However, PAC-Kinetics successfully navigates this stateful-stateless accuracy-stability Pareto frontier by deploying our novel Adaptive Online Kinetics mechanism, which dynamically scales down state-retention during rapid switches, suppressing routing lag to preserve high classification accuracy while providing near-oracle stability when temporal correlation is present.
\end{itemize}
Our code and proofs establish a new standard of mathematical safety for dynamic, sequential model ensembling on the edge. To support reproducibility and the model serving community, our complete PyTorch implementation, experimental configuration scripts, and full control-theoretic proofs are publicly available at \url{https://github.com/anonymous-submission-2026/pac-kinetics}.
