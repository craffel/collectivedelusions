## Major Strengths
1. **Rigorous Control-Theoretic Stability Guarantees:** Proving Global Asymptotic Stability (GAS) and Input-to-State Stability (ISS) using Lyapunov candidate functions for both the autonomous recurrence and the time-varying Adaptive Online Kinetics system adds an exceptional layer of mathematical safety.
2. **Mathematically Sound PAC-Bayesian Framework:** Formulating a Catoni-type PAC-Bayesian bound specifically for stateful sequential ensembling on non-i.i.d. mixing streams. Utilizing the Even/Odd Block Splitting technique is mathematically rigorous, successfully avoiding the exploding Total Variation penalty multiplier that often cripples dependent-sequence bounds.
3. **Adaptive Online Kinetics Mechanism:** The differentiable, elegant cosine-similarity scaling ($a_{k,t} = a_k \cdot Sim_t$) beautifully resolves the accuracy-stability trade-off, eliminating "inertial drag" or routing lag during rapid workload shifts.
4. **Empirical Rigor and Simulation-to-Physical Validation:** The authors go far beyond a simple toy sandbox simulation by validating their method on actual PyTorch networks (incorporating task-specific heads to resolve the Uniform Merging Paradox) and physical MNIST/Fashion-MNIST datasets, demonstrating substantial accuracy and smoothness gains.
5. **Exhaustive Sensitivity Sweeps and Systems Profiling:** The authors provide high-impact profiling of systems latency and memory footprints across different fleet sizes $K$ on both CPU and GPU (under concurrent batching), showing microsecond-level updates ($<3.5 \mu s$ on GPU under batch size 128). They also conduct detailed sweeps over prior variance ($\sigma_0^2$), calibration sequence length ($T$), expert fleet sizes up to $K=16$ (reporting spectral condition numbers), and provide empirical comparisons with high-capacity gated recurrences (GRUs and LSTMs).
6. **Outstanding Transparency:** The authors openly present and analyze the performance collapse of the randomized router, conduct a follow-up ablation sweep over smaller perturbation variances ($\sigma_{\text{pert}}^2 \le 0.01$) to bridge the gap, and honestly frame the stateful-stateless trade-off under completely uncorrelated streams.

## Areas for Improvement (Scholarly Contextualization)
While the paper is technically excellent, it would be significantly strengthened by addressing the following scholarly and positioning dimensions:
1. **Contextualization with Online Learning Literature:** The paper claims to propose "the first learning-theoretic framework for stateful, sequential model ensembling." However, the rich literature on **Online Learning and Prediction with Expert Advice** (e.g., the Hedge algorithm, Multiplicative Weights Update, and Online Convex Optimization) has studied stateful, sequential ensembling with rigorous, non-asymptotic regret bounds under arbitrary, adversarial (non-i.i.d.) streams for decades. Acknowledging and contrasting PAC-Kinetics with this classical body of work is crucial for proper scholarly attribution.
2. **Contextualization with State-Space Models (SSMs) in Deep Learning:** Since the stateful recurrence $s_t = \mathbf{A}s_{t-1} + W\mathbf{e}_t$ is mathematically equivalent to a linear State-Space Model, the paper should explicitly discuss how its routing architecture relates to the modern trend of using SSMs (such as S4 and Mamba) to model sequential dependencies.
3. **Explicit Attributions of Classical Control Concepts:** Foundational control-theory concepts utilized in the stability proofs (Section 3.7), such as Lyapunov's Direct Method and Input-to-State Stability, should explicitly cite classic reference literature (e.g., Khalil 2002, Sontag 1989).
4. **Physical Validation Scale Limitations:** While the systems-level latency profiling on GPU demonstrates scalability up to $K=16$ experts under a batch size of 128, the physical validation is currently limited to a 3-layer MLP on image data. Validating PAC-Kinetics on massive Transformer backbones (such as LLMs or deep ViTs with dozens of layers) under larger fleet scales remains a critical open systems-level challenge.

## Overall Presentation Quality
- **Excellent:** The writing is highly professional, direct, and dense with scientific insights. 
- **Mathematical Accessibility:** The mathematical notation is rigorous and consistent, with key variables, perturbations, and bounds summarized in Table 1, making the theoretical proofs highly accessible to reader.
- **Visual Aids:** The conceptual comparison of Stateless vs. Stateful routing response in Figure 3 is highly illustrative, helping build strong physical intuition for the accuracy-stability Pareto frontier.

## Potential Impact / Significance
- **High Significance:** Multi-tenant serving of parameter-efficient adapters (such as LoRA) is a dominant paradigm in edge and cloud AI serving. By providing a mathematically sound and control-theoretically stable framework for dynamic model ensembling with microsecond-scale execution overhead, PAC-Kinetics has the potential to significantly influence the design of production-level model-serving engines (such as S-LoRA, Punica, and light-weight edge deployments).
