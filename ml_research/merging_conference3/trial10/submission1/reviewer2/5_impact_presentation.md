# 5. Impact and Presentation

## Major Strengths
1. **Elegant Mathematical Foundation:** The paper introduces a rigorous, elegant theoretical framework by modeling deep network layers as a 1D lattice and formulating ensembling as a global energy-minimization problem. Using Pearl's sum-product algorithm on a chain-structured Markov Random Field (MRF) provides mathematically exact, globally optimized marginals in $O(L K^2)$ time.
2. **Clever Spatio-Temporal Decoupling:** Decoupling spatial inter-layer smoothing from temporal sample tracking is a highly valuable conceptual breakthrough. Solving spatial jitter entirely within a single sample's depth lattice allows the model to remain completely stateless across sequence samples, eliminating serving lag and temporal hysteresis.
3. **Rigorous and Practical Simplifications:** Transitioning from the exact bidirectional two-pass algorithm to the single-pass, on-the-fly `QPathMerge-Single` controller is a highly practical and well-justified step. Grounding the Truncated Backward Horizon ($H=4$) in Dobrushin's contraction theorem to prove exponential convergence is exceptionally rigorous.
4. **Exhaustive Experimental Validation and Ablations:** The authors evaluate their method against seven diverse baselines (including post-hoc filters and stateful kinetics models) across multiple sandbox configurations and a physical ResNet-18 model. The inclusion of systematic sweeps over the transition leakage $M$ and the horizon $H$ is a testament to the paper's high scientific standards.
5. **High Reproducibility:** Providing a complete, production-grade PyTorch implementation in the appendix dramatically enhances the paper's practical utility and reproducibility.

## Areas for Improvement (Weaknesses)
1. **Address the Power-Iteration Degeneracy of the Constant Future Potential Assumption:** Under the speculative constant future potential assumption ($\psi_{l'} = \psi_l$), the backward recurrence in the single-pass variant is mathematically equivalent to running a power iteration on the matrix $A = \phi \operatorname{diag}(\psi_l)$. This means the backward message $\beta_l$ converges to the dominant eigenvector of $A$, carrying zero predictive information about future layers' actual representations. The authors should discuss this degeneracy and explain why simulating $H$ steps of recurrence is superior to simply solving for the dominant eigenvector in closed form.
2. **Clarify the Single-Pass Limit Behavior as $M \to 0$:** The "Symmetric Cancellation of Forward-Backward Drift" (proving 0.0 layer jitter at $M \to 0$) only holds for the exact bidirectional solver. In the single-pass version, because of the constant potential assumption, $M \to 0$ actually amplifies the local potential of the current layer by a factor of $H+1$, which can increase spatial jitter if local potentials are noisy. The authors should mathematically clarify this limit for the single-pass version.
3. **Validate on Modern Autoregressive Transformers with Fine-Tuned PEFT:** The physical validation, while appreciated, is conducted on ResNet-18 (a CNN) using a training-free channel-modulation surrogate. Evaluating QPathMerge on actual autoregressive large language models (LLMs) with mathematically optimized fine-tuned LoRA adapters (where the Oracle baseline defines the true upper bound) would strengthen the physical generalizability claims.
4. **Acknowledge Temporal Context in Correlated Streams:** Under realistic user serving streams (e.g., multi-turn dialogs), task signatures are often temporally correlated. In such settings, stateful models could outperform completely stateless ones by leveraging historical context to filter local activation noise. The authors should discuss this trade-off.

## Overall Presentation Quality
The presentation quality is **excellent**. The paper is beautifully written, logically structured, and uses highly precise technical terminology. The figures are clean, expressive, and directly support the narrative. The transition from abstract physical metaphors to concrete probabilistic graphical models is handled smoothly and transparently.

## Potential Impact and Significance
The potential impact is **high**. As Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) and parameter-efficient adapter-merging systems become the standard paradigm for scalable deep learning on resource-constrained edge hardware, low-overhead, stable, and zero-lag serving controllers are crucial. QPathMerge shows that global physical and graphical principles can be mapped directly to serving-time controls, opening a highly promising avenue of research for state-free, mathematically optimal trajectory control in deep multi-task networks.
