\begin{abstract}
Serving multi-task parameter-efficient experts (e.g., LoRA) on dynamic edge streams is challenging due to the simultaneous presence of representation noise across model depth and temporal noise across sequence history. Prior state-of-the-art stateful serving methods attempt to stabilize ensembling trajectories using complex dynamical formulations, such as non-equilibrium chemical reaction kinetics (ChemMerge) or learning-theoretic state-space optimization (PAC-Kinetics). In this work, we apply Occam's razor to stateful serving and demonstrate that these highly parameterized frameworks can be surpassed by a simple, mathematically pure localized recursive filter. We introduce \textbf{2D-STEM} (2D Spatio-Temporal Exponential Moving Average), a training-free, highly parameter-efficient, and analytically simplex-preserving bilinear filter. 2D-STEM resolves the dual-noise problem by smoothing ensembling trajectories simultaneously across backbone depth and sequence history using a single, unified 2D recurrence. To prevent inertial transition lag under abrupt task switches, we propose \emph{Adaptive Temporal Gating} (ATG), which measures stream homogeneity on-the-fly to scale down temporal momentum instantly when a transition is detected. Empirically, 2D-STEM achieves near-oracle routing stability, reducing excess routing noise by up to $15.8\times$ above the physical boundary transition limit (and absolute jitter by up to $2.75\times$) compared to stateless baselines, while outperforming the constant-inertia ChemMerge proxy by up to a massive $51.88\%$ absolute accuracy on heterogeneous switching streams—all with zero extra parameters and zero online backpropagation or optimization overhead.
\end{abstract}
