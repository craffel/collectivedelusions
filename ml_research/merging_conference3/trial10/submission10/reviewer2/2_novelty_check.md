# 2. Novelty Check

## Assessment of Key Novel Aspects
The paper introduces several distinct novel elements that advance the state-of-the-art in dynamic multi-task model merging and serving:
1. **Unification of Spatial and Temporal State:** Rather than treating depth-wise (spatial) and sample-wise (temporal) smoothing as separate problems, 2D-STEM integrates them into a single, unified 2D bilinear recurrence filter (Equation 8).
2. **Analytical Simplex-Preserving Proof:** The authors mathematically prove (Theorem 1) that under a simple linear inequality constraint on the momentum coefficients ($\beta_{\text{depth}} + \beta_{\text{temp}, t} \le 1$), the ensembling coefficients are analytically guaranteed to reside on the probability simplex $\Delta^{K-1}$ at all steps and layers. This removes the need for expensive projection or Softmax re-normalization steps during inference.
3. **Power-Law Gating (ATG-PL) for Bias Resolution:** Linear gating ($\beta_{\text{temp}, t} = \beta_{\text{temp}, 0} \cdot Sim_t$) fails to collapse momentum during task transitions because non-negative coordinate vectors have overlapping manifolds, causing transition similarity to be biased above zero ($Sim_{\text{switch}} > 0$). The introduction of the power-law sharpening exponent ($\gamma \ge 2$) is a novel mathematical resolution that squashes this residual momentum, collapsing transition lag while preserving strong smoothing on stable blocks.
4. **Coordinate-Prior Spatial Boundary Condition:** The formulation of the virtual boundary state at layer $L_{\text{frozen}}$ using an early task-coordinate representation is a novel boundary condition that resolves spatial momentum cancellation at the entry layer without introducing the accuracy drag characteristic of uniform boundaries.
5. **Top-$k$ Coordinate Masking:** This sparse gating extension is a novel addition that decouples transition detection complexity from the expert pool size $K$, guaranteeing $O(1)$ scaling and zero cumulative overlap.

## The 'Delta' from Prior Work
- **Momentum-Merge (Spatial-only):** Momentum-Merge applies a spatial EMA to the ensembling weights across layers but resets its state completely for every new sample. The delta of 2D-STEM is the integration of sequence-level temporal propagation, allowing the system to buffer against inter-sample stream-level noise.
- **PAC-Kinetics (Temporal-only):** PAC-Kinetics propagates state temporally but forces a single static routing vector across the entire model depth, losing depth-wise local representational alignment. It also requires heavy offline PAC-Bayesian optimization loops and online backpropagation. The delta of 2D-STEM is its local layer-wise routing, zero training/backpropagation requirement, and analytical simplex preservation.
- **ChemMerge (Biochemical Stateful Merging):** ChemMerge smooths trajectories in both depth and time but models them as continuous-time chemical reactions governed by biochemical rate equations and ODEs, requiring expensive online numerical ODE integration and five or more sensitive hand-tuned hyperparameters. The delta of 2D-STEM is its discrete-time, single-line bilinear formulation which requires zero online numerical solvers, is mathematically transparent, and achieves superior noise reduction on homogeneous streams by isolating transition detection at an early frozen layer (thereby avoiding the vulnerability of ChemMerge Dynamic ODE, which misinterprets representation noise as task switches).

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this work is **significant**, particularly through the lens of a **practitioner**. While Exponential Moving Averages (EMA) and 2D bilinear recursive filters are classical mathematical concepts, their surgical adaptation, mathematical optimization, and application to dynamic PEFT model-serving is highly creative. The authors do not merely apply a standard filter; they identify and solve critical physical constraints (simplex preservation, first-layer spatial momentum cancellation, upward bias of linear cosine gating on non-negative spaces, and scalability to massive expert pools). This systematic resolution of engineering bottle-necks, combined with a training-free and projection-free formulation, represents a highly impactful contribution to practical deep learning systems.
