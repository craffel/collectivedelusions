# 2. Novelty Check and Delta from Prior Work

## Key Novel Aspects
1. **First-Principles Derivation of Bayesian Smoothing for Expert Serving:** While empirical low-pass filters (like Momentum-Merge) or continuous reaction-rate models (like ChemMerge) exist, they rely on rigid, heuristic temporal integration. AIR is the first framework to derive sequential expert gating from first-principles **Variational Free Energy (VFE) minimization**, providing a robust, theoretically grounded basis for dynamic ensembling.
2. **Exact Closed-Form Solution:** Unlike prior control-theoretic or recurrent methods (such as PAC-Kinetics) that require iterative optimization steps, unrolled gradient descent, or learning rate schedules during serving, AIR simplifies the perception objective to a quadratic convex landscape. It solves this objective **exactly in a single closed-form step** ($\mathbf{H}\mathbf{\mu}_t^* = \mathbf{b}_t$).
3. **Cholesky Precomputation:** By recognizing that the Hessian matrix $\mathbf{H}$ of the Free Energy landscape is completely constant at test-time under static variational covariance, the authors precompute its Cholesky factorization once. This allows optimal belief state calculation to be executed via simple, highly optimized forward-backward substitutions.

## Delta from Prior Work
- **vs. Stateless Routers (e.g., SABLE):** Stateless models evaluate each incoming query in absolute isolation, ignoring sequence history. AIR introduces a stateful Gaussian variational belief that acts as an adaptive temporal prior, suppressing high-frequency noise while allowing prediction errors to trigger rapid adaptation.
- **vs. Stateful Temporal Filters (e.g., ChemMerge, Momentum-Merge):** Heuristic smoothers apply a fixed integration rate regardless of the input stream, creating a severe delay (representational lag) at task boundaries. AIR's precision-weighted prediction errors act as an information-driven mechanism: a large prediction error spike at task boundaries instantly overrides the temporal prior, enabling adaptation within 1--2 steps.
- **vs. Recurrent/Optimization-Based Routers (e.g., PAC-Kinetics):** PAC-Kinetics uses unrolled recurrent updates or gradient approximations, which are computationally expensive and sensitive to step-size or initialization. AIR retrieves the mathematically exact global minimum of the free energy in a single step with a tiny, deterministic computational cost ($\mathcal{O}(K^2)$ substitution complexity).

## Characterization of Novelty (Minimalist Perspective)
- **The Elegance of Simplification:** From a minimalist perspective, the true novelty of AIR lies in its **exceptional mathematical simplification**. The authors take a highly complex neuroscientific paradigm (Active Inference)—which typically requires heavy computational simulation, continuous ODE solvers, or iterative gradient unrolling—and reduce it under static covariance to an elegant, classic **linear state observer (Kalman filter)**. 
- **Conceptual Over-Engineering vs. Core Simplicity:** While the connection to the Free Energy Principle is intellectually stimulating, the actual mechanism is remarkably simple and direct. The heavy cognitive science jargon ("self-organizing cognitive agent", "active perception", "inhibitory pathways") somewhat obfuscates this simplicity. The core technique is essentially a beautifully tuned linear state observer. The paper would be stronger and more accessible if it celebrated this simplicity directly, rather than cloaking it in extensive neuroscience terminology.
- **The Value of AIR (Diagonal):** The authors present a highly simplified variant, **AIR (Diagonal)**, where the generative coordinate mapping $\mathbf{W}$ is constrained to be diagonal. This reduces the parameter space to $5K$ (linear $\mathcal{O}(K)$ complexity) and requires almost no calibration data ($T_{\text{cal}} = 32$). This minimalist model achieves nearly the same performance as the dense model while being structurally far simpler and highly resistant to overfitting. This represents a highly practical and elegant novelty that is extremely valuable for resource-constrained systems.
