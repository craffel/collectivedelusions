# Active Inference Routing (AIR): Stateful Model serving via Variational Free Energy Minimization

## 1. Persona Alignment
In strict alignment with **The Visionary** persona (described in `persona.md`), **Active Inference Routing (AIR)** rejects incremental, purely empirical tweaks to existing dynamic ensembling heuristics. Instead, we propose a radical, brain-inspired paradigm shift that draws direct inspiration from theoretical neuroscience and cognitive science. 

Rather than viewing the router as a passive, feed-forward mathematical filter (like an Exponential Moving Average or a discretized biochemical ODE), AIR rethinks the fundamental routing paradigm: we model the multi-expert serving system as an **active, self-organizing agent** that performs test-time perception and action in a dynamic, non-i.i.d. environment. By modeling the routing weights as an internal "belief" state and updating them via the minimization of **Variational Free Energy** (balancing top-down temporal expectations and bottom-up sensory prediction errors), we establish a highly novel and theoretically rich foundation for stable test-time model ensembling.

---

## 2. Core Techniques
The core algorithms and mechanisms introduced in AIR include:
* **Active Inference Formulation of Serving Streams:** We formalize the sequence of task coordinates as a sensory stream generated by a latent state-space model representing the active task context.
* **Stateful Variational Belief State:** The routing state is represented as a Gaussian variational belief $q(\mathbf{s}_t) = \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{s}_t; \mathbf{\mu}_t, \mathbf{\Sigma}_t)$, which tracks the latent log-probabilities of experts.
* **Precision-Weighted Prediction Errors:** Bottom-up sensory prediction errors (discrepancies between expected and actual coordinate observations) are weighed against top-down prior transition expectations. This balance is governed by learnable sensory and prior precision matrices (inverse covariances), enabling the router to dynamically filter out high-frequency representation noise.
* **Test-Time Free Energy Minimization (Perception):** Instead of using static recurrence equations, the router dynamically updates its belief vector at test-time by executing a small number of gradient steps to minimize the Variational Free Energy.
* **Gibbs Policy Mapping (Action):** The converged belief state smoothly translates to active ensembling coefficients (actions) via a multi-temperature Gibbs Softmax policy, preserving Lipschitz continuity and preventing routing weight oscillations.

* *Free Energy Principle:* Friston, K. (2010). "The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?". *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*.
* *PAC-Kinetics / SABLE / ChemMerge:* Drawing upon the coordinate sandbox paradigms and mathematical deconstructions from previous trials.

## 3. Mathematical Formulation

### 3.1 The Generative Model
Let $\mathbf{s}_t \in \mathbb{R}^K$ be the latent task belief state at time step (or network layer) $t$. Let $\mathbf{e}_t \in [0, 1]^K$ be the observed coordinate projection vector. We define a linear-Gaussian state-space generative model:
1. **Transition Prior:**
   $$p(\mathbf{s}_t | \mathbf{s}_t-1) = \mathcal{N}\left(\mathbf{s}_t; \mathbf{A} \mathbf{s}_{t-1}, \mathbf{\Sigma}_s\right)$$
   where $\mathbf{A} = \text{diag}(a_1, \dots, a_K) \in (0, 1)^K$ is the diagonal state retention matrix, and $\mathbf{\Sigma}_s = \text{diag}(\sigma_{s, 1}^2, \dots, \sigma_{s, K}^2)$ is the diagonal process covariance.
2. **Observation Likelihood:**
   $$p(\mathbf{e}_t | \mathbf{s}_t) = \mathcal{N}\left(\mathbf{e}_t; \mathbf{W} \mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{\Sigma}_e\right)$$
   where $\mathbf{W} \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times K}$ is the generative coordinate mapping matrix, and $\mathbf{\Sigma}_e = \text{diag}(\sigma_{e, 1}^2, \dots, \sigma_{e, K}^2)$ is the diagonal observation covariance.

### 3.2 Variational Free Energy
At time step $t$, the agent receives a sensory observation $\mathbf{e}_t$. We define the Variational Free Energy $\mathcal{F}_t$ under the Gaussian variational posterior $q(\mathbf{s}_t) = \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{s}_t; \mathbf{\mu}_t, \mathbf{\Sigma}_t)$ as:
$$\mathcal{F}_t = \mathbb{E}_{q(\mathbf{s}_t)} \left[ \ln q(\mathbf{s}_t) - \ln p(\mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{e}_t | \mathbf{s}_{t-1}) \right]$$
Expanding and discarding entropy terms that do not depend on the mean parameter $\mathbf{\mu}_t$, the free energy simplifies analytically to the sum of precision-weighted squared prediction errors:
$$\mathcal{F}_t = \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{e}_t - \mathbf{W} \mathbf{\mu}_t)^T \mathbf{\Pi}_e (\mathbf{e}_t - \mathbf{W} \mathbf{\mu}_t) + \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{\mu}_t - \mathbf{A} \mathbf{\mu}_{t-1})^T \mathbf{\Pi}_s (\mathbf{\mu}_t - \mathbf{A} \mathbf{\mu}_{t-1})$$
where:
* $\mathbf{\Pi}_e = \mathbf{\Sigma}_e^{-1} = \text{diag}(\Pi_{e, 1}, \dots, \Pi_{e, K})$ is the diagonal **sensory precision** matrix.
* $\mathbf{\Pi}_s = \mathbf{\Sigma}_s^{-1} = \text{diag}(\Pi_{s, 1}, \dots, \Pi_{s, K})$ is the diagonal **prior precision** matrix.

### 3.3 Belief Update via Gradient Descent
To perceive the environment (i.e., update its latent belief), the agent minimizes $\mathcal{F}_t$ with respect to $\mathbf{\mu}_t$ by executing $N_{\text{steps}}$ of gradient descent:
$$\mathbf{\mu}_t^{(i+1)} = \mathbf{\mu}_t^{(i)} - \eta \nabla_{\mathbf{\mu}_t} \mathcal{F}_t$$
where the analytical gradient is:
$$\nabla_{\mathbf{\mu}_t} \mathcal{F}_t = - \mathbf{W}^T \mathbf{\Pi}_e (\mathbf{e}_t - \mathbf{W} \mathbf{\mu}_t) + \mathbf{\Pi}_s (\mathbf{\mu}_t - \mathbf{A} \mathbf{\mu}_{t-1})$$
We initialize the gradient descent loop using the prior temporal expectation: $\mathbf{\mu}_t^{(0)} = \mathbf{A} \mathbf{\mu}_{t-1}$.

### 3.4 Ensembling Policy
The active ensembling weights $\alpha_t \in \Delta^{K-1}$ are generated via a multi-temperature Gibbs Softmax policy:
$$\alpha_{k, t} = q_k(\mathbf{\mu}_t; \Theta) = \frac{\exp(\mu_{k, t}/\tau_k)}{\sum_{j=1}^K \exp(\mu_{j, t}/\tau_j)}$$
where $\tau_k = e^{w_k} + \tau_{\min}$ is the temperature for task expert $k$.

## 4. Architecture Specifications

### 4.1 System Inputs and Outputs
* **Inputs:** Raw intermediate representation $z_t \in \mathbb{R}^D$ extracted at the designated routing layer $l_{\text{route}}$.
* **Intermediate Representations:**
  1. Unit-norm normalized activation: $\tilde{z}_t = \frac{z_t}{\|z_t\|_2 + \epsilon}$.
  2. Coordinate projection vector: $\mathbf{e}_t = [e_{1, t}, \dots, e_{K, t}]^T \in [0, 1]^K$, where $e_{k, t} = \|V_{k, d}^T \tilde{z}_t\|_2$.
  3. Variational belief state vector: $\mathbf{\mu}_t \in \mathbb{R}^K$.
  4. Ensembling coefficients: $\alpha_t \in \Delta^{K-1}$.
* **Outputs:** Blended activation $h_t^{(l)}$ at all adapted layers $l > l_{\text{route}}$:
  $$h_t^{(l)} = h_t^{(l-1)} W_{\text{base}}^{(l)} + \sum_{k=1}^K \alpha_{k, t} \left( h_t^{(l-1)} A_k^{(l)} B_k^{(l)} \right)$$

### 4.2 Trainable Parameters
The complete parameter set $\Theta = \{ \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{W}, \mathbf{p}_e, \mathbf{p}_s, \mathbf{w} \}$ is low-dimensional, consisting of exactly $4K + K^2$ parameters:
1. **Log-Retention Parameters** $\mathbf{u} \in \mathbb{R}^K$: mapped to $a_k = \sigma(u_k) \in (0, 1)$.
2. **Generative Projection Matrix** $\mathbf{W} \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times K}$.
3. **Log-Sensory Precisions** $\mathbf{p}_e \in \mathbb{R}^K$: mapped to $\Pi_{e, k} = e^{p_{e, k}} > 0$.
4. **Log-Prior Precisions** $\mathbf{p}_s \in \mathbb{R}^K$: mapped to $\Pi_{s, k} = e^{p_{s, k}} > 0$.
5. **Log-Temperatures** $\mathbf{w} \in \mathbb{R}^K$: mapped to $\tau_k = e^{w_k} + \tau_{\min} \ge \tau_{\min}$.

These parameters are optimized end-to-end via gradient descent on a tiny calibration subset using standard backpropagation, minimizing cross-entropy loss under the Gibbs policy.

## 5. Baselines
We evaluate AIR against the following competitive baselines to verify its performance:
1. **Stateless SABLE (Centroid Similarity):** A stateless router serving as the upper ceiling for joint classification accuracy under static streams, but highly susceptible to massive routing jitter.
2. **ChemMerge (Biochemical ODE):** The state-of-the-art stateful routing framework modeling expert activation as chemical species in a continuous reactor.
3. **Momentum-Merge (Constant EMA):** A training-free, single-parameter EMA baseline that represents the minimalist limit of stateful smoothing.
4. **PAC-Kinetics (Theoretical SOTA):** An optimized stateful diagonal linear recurrence with PAC-Bayesian complexity regularizations under stationary mixing assumptions.

```
[Input Sample x_t]
        │
        ▼
[Frozen shared layers up to l_route]
        │
        ▼
[Raw activation z_t] ───► [Unit Normalization] ───► [Unit-norm activation z_tilde_t]
                                                                  │
                                                                  ▼
                                                      [Task Subspace Projections]
                                                                  │
                                                                  ▼
[Previous Belief μ_t-1] ──► [Prior Expectation (A * μ_t-1)]   [Sensory Coordinate e_t]
              │                            │                      │
              └────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────┘
                                           ▼
                            [Free Energy Minimization Loop]
                            (Minimize F_t w.r.t. μ_t via N_steps)
                                           │
                                           ▼
                                 [Converged Belief μ_t]
                                           │
                                           ▼
                                    [Gibbs Softmax]
                                           │
                                           ▼
                                [Ensembling Weights α_t]
                                           │
                                           ▼
                             [Single-Pass Activation Blending]
                             (At all adapted layers l > l_route)
                                           │
                                           ▼
                                 [Final Output Prediction]
```

### 6.1 Calibration and Initialization
1. **PCA Extraction:** Extract the top-$d$ principal components $V_{k, d}$ for each task expert $k$ over a small, disjoint calibration dataset $\mathcal{C}^{\text{sub}}$ to construct coordinate projection matrices.
2. **Parameter Optimization:** Train $\Theta = \{ \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{W}, \mathbf{p}_e, \mathbf{p}_s, \mathbf{w} \}$ on a short, synthesized sequential calibration stream.
3. **Inference Initialization:** Set the initial belief state at $t=1$ directly to the first coordinate observation: $\mathbf{\mu}_1 = \mathbf{e}_1$.

### 6.2 Test-Time Serving Loop (For each incoming query step $t$)
1. **Coordinate Extraction:** Forward pass the sample $\mathbf{x}_t$ up to $l_{\text{route}}$. Normalize $z_t$ to $\tilde{z}_t$, and compute coordinate vector $\mathbf{e}_{k, t} = \|V_{k, d}^T \tilde{z}_t\|_2$.
2. **Predictive Prior:** Compute the prior temporal prediction: $\mathbf{\mu}_t^{(0)} = \mathbf{A} \mathbf{\mu}_{t-1}$.
3. **Perception (Belief Refinement):** Initialize $\mathbf{\mu}_t = \mathbf{\mu}_t^{(0)}$. Run $N_{\text{steps}}$ iterations of gradient descent:
   $$\mathbf{\mu}_t^{(i+1)} = \mathbf{\mu}_t^{(i)} + \eta \left( \mathbf{W}^T \mathbf{\Pi}_e (\mathbf{e}_t - \mathbf{W} \mathbf{\mu}_t^{(i)}) - \mathbf{\Pi}_s (\mathbf{\mu}_t^{(i)} - \mathbf{\mu}_t^{(0)}) \right)$$
   This step acts as a dynamic Kalman-like filter: if sensory coordinate noise is high (low sensory precision $\mathbf{\Pi}_e$), updates are small, and the prior temporal prediction dominates. If task transition is rapid, bottom-up prediction error is large, overcoming the prior and shifting the belief state immediately.
4. **Action (Gating):** Map the final converged belief $\mathbf{\mu}_t$ to ensembling weights $\alpha_t$ via the Gibbs Softmax. Store $\mathbf{\mu}_t$ as the state history for the next step.
5. **Single-Pass Blending:** Linearly blend intermediate LoRA expert activations across all deep layers $l > l_{\text{route}}$ in a single forward pass, producing the final task prediction.
