\section{Architectural Guarantee of Exponential Recurrence}
\label{app:positivity_proof}

In this section, we outline the structural property of the discrete-time Lotka-Volterra Ricker competition formulation that guarantees the strict positivity of routing states (populations) across all network layers. This architectural guarantee is highly desirable because it naturally ensures that the routing states remain mathematically well-defined and confined to the interior of the probability simplex, completely avoiding the need for ad-hoc mathematical clamping or projection operations to enforce model physical boundaries (as required by continuous biochemical solvers), though standard wide-boundary numerical stabilizers are still employed to prevent systems-level float32 underflow or overflow.

The strict positivity of the routing states is an elegant, direct structural property of the Ricker formulation. Let us outline this inductive guarantee:

For the initial layer, let the starting population vector at the routing layer $l_{\text{route}}$ be strictly positive, i.e., $x_{k, t}^{(l_{\text{route}})} > 0$ for all $k \in \{1, \dots, K\}$. Then, for any finite growth rates $r_{k, t} \in \mathbb{R}$ and finite competition coefficients $c_{kj, t} \in \mathbb{R}$, the population density $x_{k, t}^{(l)}$ is strictly positive ($x_{k, t}^{(l)} > 0$) for all layers $l > l_{\text{route}}$ and all $k \in \{1, \dots, K\}$.

We proceed by simple induction on the layer index $l$.

\textbf{Base Case:} For $l = l_{\text{route}} + 1$, the Ricker recurrence (Eq. \ref{eq:ricker_recurrence}) defines the population as:
\begin{equation}
    x_{k, t}^{(l_{\text{route}} + 1)} = x_{k, t}^{(l_{\text{route}})} \exp\left( r_{k, t} - \sum_{j=1}^K c_{kj, t} x_{j, t}^{(l_{\text{route}})} \right)
\end{equation}
By definition, $x_{k, t}^{(l_{\text{route}})} > 0$. Since the exponential function $\exp(z) > 0$ for any real-valued argument $z \in \mathbb{R}$, we have:
\begin{equation}
    \exp\left( r_{k, t} - \sum_{j=1}^K c_{kj, t} x_{j, t}^{(l_{\text{route}})} \right) > 0
\end{equation}
The product of two strictly positive real numbers is strictly positive. Therefore, $x_{k, t}^{(l_{\text{route}} + 1)} > 0$ for all $k \in \{1, \dots, K\}$.

\textbf{Inductive Step:} Assume that for some layer $l \ge l_{\text{route}} + 1$, the population vector is strictly positive, i.e., $x_{k, t}^{(l)} > 0$ for all $k$. We show that $x_{k, t}^{(l+1)} > 0$.

Applying the Ricker recurrence for layer $l+1$:
\begin{equation}
    x_{k, t}^{(l+1)} = x_{k, t}^{(l)} \exp\left( r_{k, t} - \sum_{j=1}^K c_{kj, t} x_{j, t}^{(l)} \right)
\end{equation}
By the inductive assumption, $x_{k, t}^{(l)} > 0$. Since $r_{k, t}$ and $c_{kj, t}$ are finite real numbers, and the sum $\sum_{j=1}^K c_{kj, t} x_{j, t}^{(l)}$ is a finite real sum, the argument to the exponential function:
\begin{equation}
    z = r_{k, t} - \sum_{j=1}^K c_{kj, t} x_{j, t}^{(l)}
\end{equation}
is a well-defined real number $z \in \mathbb{R}$. Thus, $\exp(z) > 0$. Since both $x_{k, t}^{(l)}$ and $\exp(z)$ are strictly positive, their product is strictly positive:
\begin{equation}
    x_{k, t}^{(l+1)} > 0 \quad \forall k \in \{1, \dots, K\}
\end{equation}
This completes the inductive step.

Therefore, the virtual population densities $x_{k, t}^{(l)}$ are guaranteed to remain strictly positive ($x_{k, t}^{(l)} > 0$) for all layers $l > l_{\text{route}}$ and all $k \in \{1, \dots, K\}$ as a direct structural consequence of the exponential multiplier formulation.

Since the ensembling weights $\alpha_{k, t}^{(l)}$ are obtained via simple normalization:
\begin{equation}
    \alpha_{k, t}^{(l)} = \frac{x_{k, t}^{(l)}}{\sum_{j=1}^K x_{j, t}^{(l)}}
\end{equation}
and since $x_{k, t}^{(l)} > 0$ for all $k$, the denominator is strictly positive ($\sum_{j=1}^K x_{j, t}^{(l)} > 0$), meaning the division is mathematically well-defined and guarantees that $\alpha_{k, t}^{(l)} \in (0, 1)$ and $\sum_{k=1}^K \alpha_{k, t}^{(l)} = 1$. This maps the population vector perfectly onto the interior of the probability simplex $\Delta^{K-1}$ at every layer.

\section{Ecosystem Prior Hyperparameters and Initialization Stability}
\label{app:initialization}

The initial parameters of our Lotka-Volterra Competitive Serving (LVCS) system are modeled as a stable, balanced ecological system prior to gradient-based training. This prevents initial optimization instability and guarantees that the system starts with high-quality noise-suppressive smoothing behavior.

We summarize the key hyperparameter parameters and their corresponding biological interpretation in Table \ref{tab:hyperparams}.

\label{tab:hyperparams}

\begin{center}
\begin{small}
\begin{sc}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Parameter & Value & Biological Interpretation \\
\midrule
$d$ (PCA dimensions) & 10 & Dimensionality of task resource niche \\
$K$ (Experts) & 4 & Number of competing species \\
$l_{\text{route}}$ & 3 & Habitat boundary / routing entry layer \\
$s_{0, k}$ & 0.0 & Unit growth scaling ($w_k^{\text{grow}} = 1.0$) \\
$b_{0, k}^{\text{grow}}$ & 0.0 & Neutral baseline growth bias \\
$u_{0, k}$ & $\ln(0.9) \approx -0.105$ & Balanced diagonal self-limitation carrying capacity ($c_{kk} = 1.0$) \\
$v_{0, kj}$ ($k \neq j$) & $\sigma^{-1}(0.1) \approx -2.197$ & Low initial niche overlap / inter-species competition ($c_{kj} = 0.1$) \\

Under this prior configuration, if a query is highly aligned with task $k$, its resource coordinate $R_{k, t}$ approaches 1.0 while other resource coordinates remain low. The exponential growth rate $r_{k, t}$ of species $k$ will dominate, allowing it to rapidly reach its carrying capacity ($1/c_{kk} = 1.0$), while other species decay due to inter-species competition. The Adaptive Niche Plasticity scaling factor $Sim_t$ dynamically adjusts these interactions under sequential streaming shifts, preserving stability and responsiveness throughout training.
