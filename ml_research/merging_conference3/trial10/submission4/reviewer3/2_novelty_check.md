# Novelty Check: QA-Merge (Quantization-Aware Merge)

## Assessment of Key Novel Aspects
The paper introduces several highly novel elements tailored specifically to the problem of low-precision model merging and dynamic coordinate-space ensembling:
1. **Error Diffusion in Latent Coordinates (EF-Smooth & AEF):** Applying noise-shaping and error-diffusion concepts (classically used in digital signal processing, like halftoning) to neural network ensembling trajectories is a creative and highly novel approach. Tracking rounding errors layer-by-layer and adding them back recursively to the routing weights (EF-Smooth) and activations (AEF) effectively bounds the cumulative discretization noise. This addresses a deep architectural problem with a simple, high-impact systems solution.
2. **Permutation-Invariant Single-Pass Apportionment (PI-SPA):** Standard simplex projections or apportionment algorithms (like Hamilton's method) require sorting the remainders, which has $O(K \log K)$ complexity and introduces expensive pipeline branches on specialized vector hardware. PI-SPA is a novel, branchless, sorting-free projection with $O(K)$ complexity that uses static expert ID perturbations to break ties deterministically. This resolves compilation fragility (e.g., in TensorRT or TVM) and represents a significant microarchitectural innovation.
3. **Quantized Centroid Calibration (QCC) & Gating-Register Scale Alignment:** While Post-Training Quantization (PTQ) is widely studied, calibrating centroids directly inside the target quantized coordinate representation space (QCC) combined with register-level scale alignment for mixed gating (adding unscaled integer logits to scale-invariant fixed-point cosine similarities) is a highly practical, novel systems mechanism that ensures integer-only execution.

## The 'Delta' from Prior Work
- **From Static Model Merging (e.g., Model Soups, Git Re-Basin, TIES-Merging):** These techniques average weights statically offline and compile them into a single high-precision model. QA-Merge enables *dynamic ensembling* during inference under extreme INT8/INT4 quantization.
- **From Dynamic Ensembling (SABLE, ChemMerge, Momentum-Merge):** Prior activation ensembling works assume Float32 precision and collapse completely under quantization. QA-Merge provides the critical "delta" that makes these algorithms robust and deployable in low-precision edge environments.
- **From Standard Quantization (PTQ, QAT, SmoothQuant):** Traditional quantization targets individual networks. While QA-Merge leverages existing primitives like the Straight-Through Estimator (STE) and SmoothQuant-style scale migration, its primary novelty lies in *how* these are combined and adapted for *routing networks* and *coordinate-space representation trajectories*.
- **From Task Vector Quantization (TVQ):** TVQ and other ultra-low precision merging methods (like 1bit-Merging) focus on compressing checkpoint storage. QA-Merge focuses on accelerating on-device inference and reducing physical runtime latency/power on edge chips (e.g., ARM Cortex-M7).

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this paper is **significant and highly practical**. Rather than introducing heavy theoretical frameworks or high-order abstractions, the paper addresses the physical constraints of edge deployment by combining well-known concepts (STE, SmoothQuant) with highly innovative engineering designs (EF-Smooth, AEF, and PI-SPA). The "delta" from existing ensembling and quantization literature is clear, and the resulting systems speedups (5.2x latency reduction, 42% power reduction) highlight that the proposed novelty is highly actionable and valuable for real-world deployments.
