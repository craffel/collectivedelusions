# Impact and Presentation Quality Assessment

## 1. Major Strengths
1. **Mathematical Rigor and Completeness**: The paper provides a very solid theoretical foundation. It derives exact, closed-form empirical Rademacher complexity bounds for both Fourier and DCT ensembling classes over network depth coordinates. The step-by-step mathematical proofs are detailed and correct.
2. **Downward Generalization Bridge via Covering Numbers**: Instead of settling for a standard contraction-based bound that exhibits dimensional mismatch, the authors provide a formal covering-number derivation. This establishes an explicit $\widetilde{\mathcal{O}}(1/\sqrt{N})$ decay rate over data samples that is completely independent of the underlying network's parameter count, mathematically justifying the robustness of low-dimensional trajectory tuning.
3. **Identification and Mitigation of Polynomial Runaway**: The paper clearly identifies **boundary runaway** (Runge's-like phenomenon) in prior quadratic polynomial ensembling trajectories (RBPM, $d=2$). It offers an elegant solution via half-period cosine functions (RB-DCTM), which use implicit homogeneous Neumann boundary conditions ($h'(0) = h'(1) = 0$) to stabilize the critical feature-extraction and classification boundaries.
4. **Principled Optimization Design (Spectral Lasso)**: Applying the $L_1$ penalty strictly to the harmonic (non-DC) coefficients ($\theta_{k,\text{harm}}$) while leaving the uniform baseline coefficient ($a_{k,0}$) unpenalized is a clever and highly effective optimization choice. It preserves the optimal uniform activation scale across layers while constraining trajectory fluctuations.
5. **Self-Awareness and Transparency regarding Limitations**: The authors are highly transparent and proactive. Throughout the paper and appendix, they include detailed remarks addressing standard machine learning theory-practice gaps, Lipschitz scaling pathologies in deep networks, representational flexibility trade-offs of Neumann conditions, and the need for auxiliary data in coordinate alignment.

## 2. Areas for Improvement
1. **Resolve Empirical Contradictions**: The authors must explain or resolve why unregularized models on CLIP ViT-B/16 achieve much higher accuracy (83.35% at $F=3$ with $\gamma=0$) than their regularized counterparts (72.70% with $\gamma=0.01$ and $F=2$ in Table 1), and why increasing cutoff frequency $F$ under $\gamma=0$ improves accuracy on CLIP while their theory claims it should overfit.
2. **True Few-Shot Validation without Auxiliary Data**: The authors should run the coordinate alignment phase using a regularized covariance estimator (such as Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage) to prove that the entire pipeline can execute successfully strictly on a 10-shot calibration budget, without relying on an auxiliary 100-sample unlabeled dataset for ZipIt! alignment.
3. **Large-Scale Real-World Benchmarks**: The paper would be significantly strengthened by expanding the evaluation beyond a 2-task CIFAR-10/100 merge on a single ViT-B/16 network. Evaluating on standard benchmarks (e.g., merging 8 CLIP task vectors or merging multiple LLaMA instruction-following checkpoints) is necessary to validate the claimed scalability of the method to ultra-deep decoder architectures.
4. **Include State-of-the-Art Adaptive Baselines**: Include AdaMerging (Yang et al., 2024) or other layer-wise adaptive ensembling methods from the recent literature as experimental baselines to prove the superiority of the proposed spectral trajectory regularization.
5. **De-emphasize the Synthetic Sandbox**: Since the "Analytical Coordinate Sandbox" is an environment where any parameter adaptation is counterproductive (Static Uniform consistently outperforms all adaptive methods by over 10%), the authors should de-emphasize its role in the main paper and focus more on realistic, non-aligned scenarios where adaptive merging is actually needed.

## 3. Overall Presentation Quality
- **Clarity and Narrative**: The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow. The progression from weight-space merging to Fourier trajectory parameterization, complexity bounds, the covering-number bridge, and empirical validation is seamless.
- **Notation and Mathematical Formatting**: The mathematical notation is precise and consistent throughout the paper. The appendix is highly detailed and well-contextualized.
- **Visualizations**: The inclusion of trajectory profiles and accuracy comparisons is helpful to build intuition.

## 4. Potential Impact and Significance
- **Theoretical Contribution**: Establishing tight empirical Rademacher complexity bounds and a rigorous downstream generalization bridge via covering numbers for continuous trajectory classes is a valuable contribution to the statistical learning theory of deep neural networks.
- **Practical Significance**: The concept of continuous, low-dimensional spectral trajectories for model ensembling across depth is highly general and elegant. If scaled and validated on large-scale models (such as deep LLMs with 32 to 80 layers), this method could have a substantial impact on the machine learning community. It decouples the ensembling parameter space from network depth, dramatically reducing the dimensionality of layer-wise tuning and providing a robust post-hoc ensembling mechanism for edge and multi-task model assembly.
