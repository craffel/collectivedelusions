\section{Calibrated Representation Simulation Specifications}
\label{app:simulation}

To establish a controlled environment for testing model ensembling routing behaviors under perfect task isolation, we designed a high-fidelity calibrated representation simulator. The parameters governing this environment are defined as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Feature Dimension:} $D = 192$, representing a compact but expressive representation space.
    \item \textbf{Expert Registry Size:} $K = 4$ experts, corresponding to the MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN specialized classifiers.
    \item \textbf{Subspace Dimensionality:} Each task is allocated a disjoint $D_{sub} = D / K = 48$ dimensional subspace. Features for Task $k$ are strictly active within their respective subspace coordinates, preventing arbitrary feature leakage and establishing perfect task boundaries.
    \item \textbf{Calibrated Noise Scales:} Each task is simulated with a distinct Gaussian noise level added to the feature embeddings:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item MNIST ($k=0$): $\sigma_0 = 0.05$ (highly clean representation)
        \item FashionMNIST ($k=1$): $\sigma_1 = 0.25$ (moderate noise)
        \item CIFAR-10 ($k=2$): $\sigma_2 = 0.40$ (moderate-to-high noise)
        \item SVHN ($k=3$): $\sigma_3 = 1.95$ (extremely high noise)
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Oracle Classifiers:} The expert classifiers are modeled as unit-norm weight matrices aligned with class prototypes in the representation space. The oracle accuracy achieved under perfect gating is exactly 74.18\%, reflecting the impact of the simulated noise on classification boundaries.
\end{itemize}

\section{Mathematical Proof of Orthonormalization and Symmetry}
\label{app:proof}

In this section, we formally verify that the L{\"o}wdin Symmetric Orthogonalization yields a perfectly orthonormal basis, and prove its symmetric order-invariance properties.

\subsection{Proof of Orthonormality}
Let $\bar{V} \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times D}$ be the matrix of original normalized centroids:
\begin{equation}
\bar{V} = [\bar{v}_1, \dots, \bar{v}_K]^T
\end{equation}
The symmetric Gram overlap matrix $S \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times K}$ is defined as:
\begin{equation}
S = \bar{V} \bar{V}^T
\end{equation}
By computing the eigendecomposition $S = U \Lambda U^T$ (where $U U^T = U^T U = I_K$), the symmetric inverse square root is given by:
\begin{equation}
S^{-1/2} = U \Lambda^{-1/2} U^T
\end{equation}
The orthogonalized directions are defined in matrix form as:
\begin{equation}
Q = S^{-1/2} \bar{V}
\end{equation}
We verify the orthonormality of the basis by computing the inner product matrix of $Q$:
\begin{align}
Q Q^T &= \left( S^{-1/2} \bar{V} \right) \left( S^{-1/2} \bar{V} \right)^T \\
&= S^{-1/2} \bar{V} \bar{V}^T \left( S^{-1/2} \right)^T \\
&= S^{-1/2} S S^{-1/2}
\end{align}
Because $S^{-1/2}$ and $S$ share the same eigenvectors $U$, they commute. Therefore:
\begin{align}
Q Q^T &= S^{-1/2} S^{1/2} S^{1/2} S^{-1/2} \\
&= I_K
\end{align}
Thus, the task coordinate vectors $\{q_k\}$ are guaranteed to be perfectly orthonormal: $q_i \cdot q_j = \delta_{ij}$.

\subsection{Proof of Symmetry and Order-Invariance}
Unlike Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization, which is sequentially asymmetric, L{\"o}wdin orthogonalization is order-invariant. Let $P \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times K}$ be an arbitrary permutation matrix representing a reordering of our experts. The permuted centroids are given by $\bar{V}_{\text{perm}} = P \bar{V}$, and the corresponding permuted Gram matrix is:
\begin{equation}
S_{\text{perm}} = \bar{V}_{\text{perm}} \bar{V}_{\text{perm}}^T = P \bar{V} \bar{V}^T P^T = P S P^T
\end{equation}
The symmetric inverse square root of the permuted Gram matrix is:
\begin{equation}
S_{\text{perm}}^{-1/2} = (P S P^T)^{-1/2} = P S^{-1/2} P^T
\end{equation}
The resulting orthogonalized directions under permutation are:
\begin{align}
Q_{\text{perm}} &= S_{\text{perm}}^{-1/2} \bar{V}_{\text{perm}} \\
&= \left( P S^{-1/2} P^T \right) \left( P \bar{V} \right) \\
&= P S^{-1/2} \bar{V} \\
&= P Q
\end{align}
This proves that permuting the order of the experts merely permutes the resulting orthogonal basis in the exact same manner. The task-space coordinates are completely independent of the arbitrary index order of the expert registry, ensuring perfect mathematical symmetry.

\subsection{Proof of Equivalence under Constant Symmetric Task Correlation}
\label{app:equivalence}

Under symmetric task correlation evaluated in our experiments, the task centroid directions $\{\bar{v}_k\}_{k=1}^K$ share a constant pairwise overlap $s \in [0, 1)$ for all $i \neq j$. Consequently, the Gram overlap matrix $S \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times K}$ has the form:
\begin{equation}
S = (1 - s) I_K + s \mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^T
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{1} = [1, 1, \dots, 1]^T \in \mathbb{R}^K$ is the vector of ones. 

Because $S$ is a symmetric matrix with constant off-diagonals, any analytic function of $S$, such as the symmetric inverse square root $S^{-1/2}$, preserves this algebraic structure. Therefore, we can express $S^{-1/2}$ in the form:
\begin{equation}
S^{-1/2} = d_1 I_K + d_2 \mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^T
\end{equation}
where $d_1 > 0$ and $d_2$ are scalar constants. The L{\"o}wdin-orthogonalized basis vectors are given in matrix form by $Q = S^{-1/2} \bar{V}$, where $\bar{V} = [\bar{v}_1, \dots, \bar{v}_K]^T \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times D}$. Writing this coordinate-wise:
\begin{equation}
q_k = d_1 \bar{v}_k + d_2 \sum_{j=1}^K \bar{v}_j
\end{equation}
During dynamic ensembling, let $\tilde{z}_b$ be the normalized feature representation of sample $b$. The L{\"o}wdin coordinate projection $u'_{k,b} = q_k \cdot \tilde{z}_b$ is:
\begin{align}
u'_{k,b} &= \left( d_1 \bar{v}_k + d_2 \sum_{j=1}^K \bar{v}_j \right) \cdot \tilde{z}_b \\
&= d_1 (\bar{v}_k \cdot \tilde{z}_b) + d_2 \sum_{j=1}^K (\bar{v}_j \cdot \tilde{z}_b) \\
&= d_1 u_{k,b} + C_b
\end{align}
where $u_{k,b} = \bar{v}_k \cdot \tilde{z}_b$ is the raw unorthogonalized PFSR projection coordinate, and $C_b = d_2 \sum_{j=1}^K (\bar{v}_j \cdot \tilde{z}_b)$ is a scalar constant that is identical for all tasks $k$ given sample $b$.

Crucially, because $C_b$ is a constant shift applied to all coordinate dimensions, it does not alter the relative ordering or the argmax of the projection coordinates:
\begin{equation}
\arg\max_k u'_{k,b} = \arg\max_k (d_1 u_{k,b} + C_b) = \arg\max_k u_{k,b}
\end{equation}
Under near-hard temperature scaling ($\tau = 0.001$), the dynamic ensembling coefficients are completely determined by the coordinate argmax. Thus, OTSP and PFSR are mathematically forced to make \textbf{exactly identical ensembling decisions} for all samples. Under smoother temperatures, L{\"o}wdin orthogonalization behaves simply as a uniform temperature-rescaling factor ($\tau_{\text{eff}} = \tau / d_1$), demonstrating that the orthogonalization framework is practically and mathematically redundant under symmetric constant task correlation.
