\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this paper, we introduced \textbf{Rademacher-Bounded Fourier Trajectory Merging (RB-FTM)} and its non-periodic variant \textbf{RB-DCTM}, a mathematically rigorous and structurally stable ensembling paradigm for layer-wise model merging. By parameterizing trajectories as low-frequency continuous sinusoids, we establish tight empirical Rademacher complexity guarantees that decay with depth and are independent of model parameter sizes. This trigonometric formulation successfully mitigates boundary runaway near first and last layers. Our evaluations in our Analytical Coordinate Sandbox across CNN and Transformer backbones, alongside a real-world validation on actual Vision Transformers, demonstrate that our spectral trajectory models serve as exceptionally robust regularized optimizers when adaptation of ensembling weights is required, significantly outperforming all other tuned baselines. Future work includes extending spectral merging trajectories to large language models (LLMs) and investigating automated frequency-cutoff selection mechanisms based on task-specific alignment complexity. By introducing statistical learning theory to the geometry of model merging, this work paves the way for provably robust, post-hoc multi-task architectures.
