# 2. Novelty Check

## Assessment of Key Novel Aspects
From a theoretical and conceptual perspective, the novelty of the proposed **Sparsity-Guided Task Arithmetic (SG-TA)** framework is **incremental**. The core idea of applying magnitude-based pruning to task-specific updates prior to merging is not fundamentally new, nor does it represent a significant mathematical departure from existing paradigms:
- **Pruning in Model Merging**: Pre-merging weight sparsification is already well-established. For instance, **TIES-Merging** (Yadav et al., 2023) utilizes magnitude-based pruning as its very first step, before performing sign election and consensus averaging. SG-TA simply removes the subsequent sign election and consensus steps, proposing a stripped-down, deterministic version. While the paper argues that this simplification isolates the causal impact of magnitude pruning, removing existing algorithmic components is a simplification rather than a conceptually novel contribution.
- **Equivalence to Decoupled Prune-then-Merge (P-then-M)**: The authors explicitly acknowledge that **SG-TA (LQ)** (Layer-wise Quantile masking) is mathematically equivalent to the existing **Decoupled Prune-then-Merge (P-then-M)** baseline when both are fully optimized. The empirical performance of the two is nearly identical (57.81% vs. 57.11%), which means the layer-wise version of the proposed method provides zero algorithmic delta over known baselines.
- **Global Quantile (GQ) Masking**: The primary algorithmic distinction of SG-TA (GQ) is the use of a *global* magnitude threshold across the entire model instead of independent thresholds per layer. While this budget-allocation flexibility yields improved empirical results, it lacks any rigorous theoretical formulation. No mathematical analysis is provided to explain *why* or *how* global quantile selection alters the representation capacity or spectral properties of the merged weight matrices, leaving the mechanism purely heuristic.
- **Sigmoid-Gated Soft Masking (SG-TA-Soft)**: Applying a continuous sigmoid gate to smooth out thresholding boundaries is a standard mathematical trick used extensively across deep learning (e.g., in routing gates, soft-thresholding operators, and gating mechanisms). The paper does not provide any new mathematical insights or optimization guarantees regarding the landscape smoothness or convergence properties of this formulation.
- **Task Vector Magnitude Normalization (TV-Norm)**: Scaling vector updates by the inverse of their L1-norm (mean absolute magnitude) is a straightforward vector normalization technique. It is highly intuitive, but conceptually straightforward.
- **Non-Uniform Calibration (Coordinate Search)**: Coordinate descent is a standard textbook optimization technique. Applying coordinate descent sequentially task-by-task to optimize $2T$ scalar hyperparameters is a direct application of standard optimization heuristics and does not introduce any novel theoretical optimization algorithms.

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this submission is **incremental and predominantly empirical**. Rather than establishing a new theoretical foundation, a novel mathematical framework, or proving new guarantees for model merging, the paper systematically studies a set of simplified, deterministic heuristics (magnitude pruning, global vs. layer-wise budgeting, L1 vector scaling, sigmoid gating, and coordinate search). 

While the empirical exploration of these components is thorough and provides valuable practical insights (such as the importance of global budget flexibility over rigid layer budgets), the lack of formal theoretical delta or mathematical novelty limits its contribution to the scientific understanding of *why* weight-space consolidation works under these specific conditions.
