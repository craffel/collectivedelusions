# Evaluation Phase 1: Summary of the Paper

## 1. Main Topic and Scope
This paper addresses the problem of post-hoc model merging, which consolidates multiple task-specific expert models (fine-tuned from a shared base initialization) into a single multi-task network without joint retraining. Specifically, it focuses on parameterized model merging, where layer-wise or parameter-wise combining coefficients are optimized using a small set of data (either test-time data streams or small offline validation sets). The paper challenges the standard assumption that these coefficients should be optimized as independent variables in the physical, spatial depth coordinate space. It argues that this spatial paradigm leads to high-frequency spatial noise and severe overfitting under data scarcity—a phenomenon the authors term the *Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox*.

## 2. Proposed Approach (SpectralMerge)
The paper proposes **SpectralMerge**, a framework that maps the 1D spatial sequence of layer-wise merging coefficients into the frequency domain using the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT-II). By leveraging the orthonormal trigonometric basis of the DCT, SpectralMerge models merging trajectories in an orthogonal spectral space. 

The authors propose two frequency-domain regularization variants:
1. **SpectralMerge-LP (Low-Pass Hard Cutoff):** Restricts the trainable parameters to the first $F$ lowest-frequency components (e.g., $F \in \{1, 2, 3\}$), setting all higher-frequency components to zero. This acts as a hard low-pass filter, reducing the optimization search space and eliminating high-frequency degrees of freedom.
2. **SpectralMerge-Reg (Soft Spectral Regularization):** Optimizes all frequency components but adds a quadratic **Spectral Decay Penalty** to the objective function, penalizing high-frequency coordinates with a weight that scales quadratically with the frequency index: $\lambda_j = \mu \cdot j^2$.

The framework also naturally generalizes to handle architectural heterogeneity through *Block-wise and Layer-type Spectral Merging*, which partitions layers into homogeneous functional subsets (e.g., MHA vs. MLP) and applies independent spectral transformations to each.

## 3. Key Findings
* **Standard Sequential Streams:** On a calibrated Vision Transformer (ViT-B/32) simulation landscape, Online SpectralMerge-LP ($F=3$) and Online SpectralMerge-Reg achieve state-of-the-art multi-task average accuracies of $85.32\%$ and $85.17\%$, outperforming Uniform merging ($84.44\%$) and Online AdaMerging ($79.15\%$). Under the offline few-shot validation tuning (OFS-Tune) regime with $M=10$ samples, SpectralMerge-LP ($F=3$) and SpectralMerge-Reg achieve $86.46\%$ and $86.44\%$ respectively, outperforming unconstrained spatial search ($83.81\%$) and the polynomial baseline Poly-Val ($85.67\%$).
* **Adversarial Stream Conditions:** Under extreme label shift, bursty task streams, and small batch noise, online adaptation methods suffer from adaptation drift and collapse, while OFS-Tune SpectralMerge maintains a robust $86.46\%$ accuracy across all conditions, showing complete immunity to non-stationary environments.
* **Resolution of Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox:** Under extreme data scarcity ($M \in [5, 50]$), unconstrained spatial search suffers from severe overfitting, falling below the static Uniform baseline. SpectralMerge completely resolves this overfitting, showing steady scaling behavior as $M$ increases.
* **Resilience to Validation Selection Bias:** When validation sets suffer from isotropic or structured selection bias (up to $30\%$), SpectralMerge degrades gracefully, maintaining $>85\%$ accuracy, whereas unconstrained spatial optimization collapses.
* **Empirical Validation on Physical Networks:**
  * **Heterogeneous MLP:** A physical 12-layer MLP on a synthetic 3-task dataset shows that SpectralMerge-Reg ($\mu=1.0$) improves multi-task accuracy by $10\%$ absolute over unconstrained spatial search ($60.42\%$ vs $50.42\%$). Block-wise SpectralMerge outpeforms global spectral merging by $2.92\%$, validating the handling of architectural heterogeneity.
  * **Pre-trained ResNet-18:** Evaluated on real CIFAR-10 tasks, unconstrained spatial search and PolyMerge overfit catastrophically and collapse to majority-class guessing ($29.00\%$). SpectralMerge-Reg ($\mu=1.0$) prevents this collapse, achieving a blowout multi-task test accuracy of $54.00\%$ ($+25.00\%$ over spatial/PolyMerge and $+13.00\%$ over Uniform).
  * **PEFT-Induced Step-Function Discontinuity:** The authors observe that when merging networks with localized updates (e.g., fine-tuning only the classification head and `layer4` of ResNet-18 while keeping other layers frozen), a sharp step-function discontinuity in parameter sensitivity is introduced. This discontinuity requires infinite frequency support, causing hard-cutoff spectral methods (SpectralMerge-LP and LP-Adaptive) to fail ($29.00\%$), while soft regularization (SpectralMerge-Reg) successfully adapts and thrives ($54.00\%$).

## 4. Explicitly Claimed Contributions
1. **Conceptual Paradigm Shift:** Challenging the spatial coordinate paradigm of model merging and proposing frequency-domain re-parameterization via the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT-II).
2. **Mathematical Framework:** Designing SpectralMerge-LP and SpectralMerge-Reg, which provide robust structural regularization and perfect numerical conditioning ($\kappa \approx 1.0$) across all network depth scales.
3. **Rigorous Empirical Verification:** Demonstrating outstanding performance on a standard multi-task ViT-B/32 simulation benchmark across 30 random seeds, and stress-testing under multiple severe adversarial corruptions and validation biases.
4. **Resolution of Overfitting and Real-World Checkpoints:** Empirically refuting the Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox under extreme sample complexity, and validating on physical PyTorch networks (Heterogeneous MLP and pre-trained ResNet-18) using real image data.
5. **Architectural and PEFT Insights:** Proposing Block-wise Spectral Merging to handle architectural heterogeneity, identifying PEFT-induced step discontinuities, and introducing Adaptive Bandwidth SpectralMerge (LP-Adaptive).
