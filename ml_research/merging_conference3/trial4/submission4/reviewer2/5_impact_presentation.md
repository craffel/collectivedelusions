# Evaluation Phase 5: Impact and Presentation Quality

## 1. Major Strengths
* **Mathematical Elegance:** The mathematical foundation of SpectralMerge is exceptionally solid and elegant. Mapping layer-wise combining coefficients onto an orthonormal trigonometric basis (DCT-II) guarantees perfect numerical conditioning ($\kappa = 1.0$) across all network depths. The implicit even symmetric boundary condition provides a beautiful boundary derivative flattening effect (zero slope) at the virtual edges, smoothly protecting critical input and classification head layers from gradient spikes.
* **Effective Overfitting Prevention:** The soft spectral decay penalty (SpectralMerge-Reg) provides a highly effective regularizer that prevents catastrophic validation overfitting under extreme data scarcity. The blowout accuracy boost on physical ResNet-18 checkpoints ($+25.00\%$ over spatial and PolyMerge) is a compelling empirical demonstration of this benefit.
* **Deep Architectural Analysis:** The paper does not simply present a method; it provides a deep analysis of failure modes. The identification of the **PEFT-Induced Step-Function Discontinuity** and the explanation of why hard low-pass filters collapse on localized fine-tuned weights is an excellent and highly insightful addition to the literature.
* **Comprehensive Simulation:** The multi-axial simulation experiments (incorporating 30 seeds, extreme label shift, bursty streams, small batch noise, and varying validation selection biases) provide a thorough stress-test of the optimization dynamics.

## 2. Areas for Improvement (Constructive Critique)
* **Scale Up the Physical Evaluation:** The physical experiments are currently restricted to toy-scale configurations (a 12-layer MLP on synthetic data, and a ResNet-18 fine-tuned on only 120 samples per task on two binary CIFAR-10 tasks). The authors should evaluate SpectralMerge on standard, large-scale model-merging benchmarks (e.g., merging 5-8 full-scale vision expert models on diverse datasets, or merging pre-trained LLMs on NLP tasks) to demonstrate real-world scalability.
* **Address Missing Key Literature:** The authors must cite and discuss **FREE-Merging (ICCV 2025)**, which is a concurrent and highly relevant frequency-domain model-merging framework. They must clarify the distinction: FREE-Merging filters high-dimensional model parameters, whereas SpectralMerge regularizes low-dimensional combining trajectories.
* **Implement the Missing Spatial Smoothness Baseline:** The authors must compare their spectral approach against direct **spatial smoothness regularization** (adding a first-order or second-order finite difference penalty on layer coefficients directly in the spatial coordinate space). This is a critical baseline omission; without it, they cannot prove that optimizing in the spectral domain is superior to simple spatial smoothing in the spatial domain.
* **Resolve the Global DCT Contradiction:** The authors argue that global smoothing across heterogeneous layer types is restrictive and propose Block-wise Spectral Merging. However, in their physical ResNet-18 evaluation, they apply a global DCT-II across convolutional filters, batch norm parameters, and classification heads. They should resolve this contradiction by evaluating Block-wise Spectral Merging on the ResNet-18 architecture.
* **De-hype the Narrative:** The paper overuses hyperbolic and academic-hype language ("paradigm-shifting", "visionary", "spectacular", "blowout"). The authors should adopt a more sober, objective, and professional tone.

## 3. Overall Presentation Quality
* **Writing and Structure:** The writing quality is excellent, and the manuscript is logically structured. The mathematical formulas are complete and correctly formatted.
* **Visuals:** The figures (including the condition number comparison, sample complexity sweeps, validation bias curves, and convergence trajectories) are exceptionally clear, professional, and do a great job of conveying the key results.
* **Conciseness:** The text is highly repetitive, particularly in explaining the boundary derivatives and the simulation environment. Compressing these repetitive explanations would streamline the paper and leave more space for full-scale evaluations.

## 4. Potential Impact and Significance
* **Theoretical Impact:** High. The paper introduces a highly elegant mathematical framework that bridges digital signal processing and deep model merging, offering a superior alternative to spatial polynomial smoothing.
* **Practical Impact:** Currently Moderate. While SpectralMerge-Reg achieves outstanding results on ResNet-18, the absolute multi-task accuracy ($54\%$) is still very poor, and the hard-cutoff variants (SpectralMerge-LP) collapse completely on physical checkpoints. Unless the authors can demonstrate high performance on large-scale benchmarks and resolve the PEFT-induced step-function discontinuity through hybrid designs, practitioners may find its utility limited in complex real-world pipelines.
