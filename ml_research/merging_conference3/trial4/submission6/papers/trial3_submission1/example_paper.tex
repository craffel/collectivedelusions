\section{Sensitivity Analysis of Elastic Spatial Regularization}
\label{appendix:alpha_sensitivity}

In Section 3.3, we introduced Elastic Spatial Regularization (ESR, modeled as Total Variation over adjacent layer coefficients) scaled by a regularization weight $\alpha$:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{R}_{\text{TV}}(\Lambda) = \sum_{k=1}^K \sum_{l=1}^{L-1} (\lambda_k^{l+1} - \lambda_k^l)^2
\end{equation}
To investigate the sensitivity of Q-Merge optimization to this spatial smoothing parameter, we performed a sweep over $\alpha \in \{0.05, 0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 5.0\}$ under 4-bit Symmetric Per-Channel source optimization ($Q_{\text{opt}}$) and evaluated performance on both the source and the mismatched Symmetric Per-Tensor target schema ($Q_{\text{eval}}$).

The results are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:alpha_sensitivity}. As analyzed in Section 4.3, weak spatial regularization ($\alpha \in \{0.05, 0.1\}$) fails to smooth the coefficient landscape sufficiently to override discrete rounding thresholds, leaving target performance near random-guess levels. Conversely, strong regularization ($\alpha \ge 1.0$) over-regularizes the merging coefficients, forcing them toward uniform linear ensembling and significantly damaging source accuracy without providing any target mitigation. This empirical sweep verifies that continuous spatial regularizers are fundamentally limited in their ability to bridge discontinuous quantization boundaries.

\section{Formalization of the Proposed Hybrid Optimization Pipeline}
\label{appendix:hybrid_optimizer}

To address the trade-off between the rapid basin-finding capability of gradient-based optimization and the robust, non-smooth boundary navigation of black-box search, we formalize the proposed \textbf{Hybrid Optimization Pipeline}. 

The pipeline operates in two distinct, sequential phases:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Phase 1: Coarse STE Search.} We execute standard gradient-based Straight-Through Estimator (STE) optimization under the unsupervised prediction prediction entropy minimization objective $\mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}}(\Lambda)$ for $T_{\text{coarse}}$ steps. This allows the optimizer to rapidly navigate the continuous weight space and locate a low-loss basin.
    \item \textbf{Phase 2: Fine-Grained Derivative-Free Search under Spatial Smoothing.} Upon satisfying a transition criterion (either reaching a fixed step count $T_{\text{coarse}}$ or when the moving average of gradient magnitudes falls below a threshold $\epsilon_{\text{grad}}$), we freeze the gradient path and switch to a 1+1 Evolution Strategy (1+1 ES). In this phase, we optimize the joint objective $\mathcal{L}_{\text{total}}(\Lambda) = \mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}}(\Lambda) + \alpha_{\text{hybrid}} \mathcal{R}_{\text{TV}}(\Lambda)$ using purely derivative-free, zero-order perturbations. The TV spatial regularizer prevents candidate proposals from overfitting to the micro-rounding boundaries of the source quantization operator, ensuring the learned coefficients are spatially smooth and robust.
\end{enumerate}

The algorithmic layout of this hybrid optimization pipeline is detailed in Algorithm~\ref{alg:hybrid_opt}.

\begin{algorithm}[tb]
\caption{Proposed Hybrid Optimization Pipeline for Robust Low-Bit Model Merging}
\label{alg:hybrid_opt}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE {\bfseries Input:} Initial merging coefficients $\Lambda^{(0)} \in [0, 1]^{K \times L}$, calibration dataset $D_{\text{cal}}$, learning rate $\eta$, step size $\sigma$, coarse steps $T_{\text{coarse}}$, total steps $T_{\text{total}}$, regularization weight $\alpha_{\text{hybrid}}$, transition threshold $\epsilon_{\text{grad}}$.
\STATE {\bfseries Output:} Optimized, deployment-robust merging coefficients $\Lambda^*$.
\STATE Initialize Adam moments $m^{(0)} \leftarrow 0$, $v^{(0)} \leftarrow 0$.
\STATE \COMMENT{\emph{Phase 1: Coarse STE Search}}
\FOR{$t = 0$ {\bfseries to} $T_{\text{coarse}} - 1$}
    \STATE Compute predictions $p(c)$ on $D_{\text{cal}}$ under quantized weights $Q_{\text{opt}}(\theta_{\text{merged}}(\Lambda^{(t)}), b)$.
    \STATE Compute prediction entropy $\mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}}(\Lambda^{(t)})$.
    \STATE Compute STE gradient $g^{(t)} = \nabla_{\Lambda} \mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}}(\Lambda^{(t)})$.
    \STATE Update Adam moments and compute bias-corrected step $s^{(t)} = \text{Adam}(g^{(t)})$.
    \STATE Update coefficients: $\Lambda^{(t+1)} \leftarrow \text{clamp}\left(\Lambda^{(t)} - \eta \cdot s^{(t)}, [0, 1]\right)$.
    \IF{$\|s^{(t)}\|_2 < \epsilon_{\text{grad}}$}
        \STATE {\bfseries break} \COMMENT{Early transition to derivative-free search}
    \ENDIF
\ENDFOR
\STATE Let $T_{\text{trans}}$ be the step at which Phase 1 terminates.
\STATE Initialize ES parent: $\bar{\Lambda} \leftarrow \Lambda^{(T_{\text{trans}})}$.
\STATE Initialize parent loss: $\mathcal{L}_{\text{parent}} \leftarrow \mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}}(\bar{\Lambda}) + \alpha_{\text{hybrid}} \mathcal{R}_{\text{TV}}(\bar{\Lambda})$.
\STATE \COMMENT{\emph{Phase 2: Fine-Grained Derivative-Free Search under Spatial Smoothing}}
\FOR{$t = T_{\text{trans}}$ {\bfseries to} $T_{\text{total}} - 1$}
    \STATE Propose candidate: $\Lambda' \leftarrow \text{clamp}\left(\bar{\Lambda} + \sigma \cdot Z, [0, 1]\right), \quad Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$.
    \STATE Compute candidate loss: $\mathcal{L}_{\text{cand}} \leftarrow \mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}}(\Lambda') + \alpha_{\text{hybrid}} \mathcal{R}_{\text{TV}}(\Lambda')$.
    \IF{$\mathcal{L}_{\text{cand}} \le \mathcal{L}_{\text{parent}}$}
        \STATE Accept proposal: $\bar{\Lambda} \leftarrow \Lambda'$
        \STATE Update parent loss: $\mathcal{L}_{\text{parent}} \leftarrow \mathcal{L}_{\text{cand}}$
    \ENDIF
\ENDFOR
\STATE \textbf{return} $\Lambda^* \leftarrow \bar{\Lambda}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
