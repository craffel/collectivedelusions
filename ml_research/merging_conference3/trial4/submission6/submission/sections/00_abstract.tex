\begin{abstract}
As deep learning scales, weight-space model merging has emerged as a powerful, training-free paradigm for combining task-specific experts into unified multi-task networks. To mitigate parameter interference, modern merging methods have introduced increasingly complex, multi-stage heuristics such as coordinate-wise sign voting, sign consensus enforcement, and stochastic scaling. In this work, we challenge the necessity of these convoluted pipelines and demonstrate, guided by Occam's razor, that the sign-resolution heuristics of methods like TIES-Merging are entirely redundant. We introduce \textbf{Sparse Task Arithmetic (STA)}, an extremely elegant alternative that relies solely on uniform layer-wise magnitude-based pruning of task vectors followed by standard linear addition. Evaluated on a vision-transformer multi-task suite, STA matches the performance of over-engineered alternatives (or slightly outperforms them within the margin of statistical error), recovering 99.4\% of the full Task Arithmetic performance while discarding 50\% of the parameter updates. Our findings suggest that weight space denoising, rather than sign consensus, is the primary driver of successful model merging, offering a powerful argument for simplicity in deep learning.
\end{abstract}
