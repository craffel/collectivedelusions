\section{Conclusion and Future Outlook}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this paper, we challenge the trend toward hyper-complex heuristics in weight-space model merging. Guided by Occam's razor, we deconstruct the necessity of coordinate-wise sign voting and dominant sign election as popularized by methods like TIES-Merging and DARE. 

We introduce \textbf{Sparse Task Arithmetic (STA)}, an incredibly simple, training-free merging technique that replaces convoluted multi-stage pipelines with a direct two-step protocol: layer-wise magnitude-based thresholding followed by standard linear addition. Our empirical results on a multi-task Vision Transformer benchmark demonstrate that STA matches the performance of over-engineered baselines (or slightly outperforms them within the margin of statistical error) while reducing the parameter footprint by 50\%. 

Furthermore, we provide mathematical and physical grounding for the success of STA, showing that sparsity naturally prevents parameter collisions, and that magnitude pruning is fundamentally an absolute magnitude noise filter rather than a sign-conflict resolver.

The success of STA offers a powerful lesson for deep learning research: complex problems do not always require complex solutions. We hope this work encourages the community to favor simplicity, clarity, and structural elegance, prioritizing the deconstruction of core model mechanics over the accumulation of convoluted heuristics.
