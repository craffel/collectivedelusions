# 1. Summary of the Paper

## Main Topic
The paper addresses the challenge of **spatial weight-space interference** in weight-space model merging. Model merging aims to combine multiple task-specific expert models (fine-tuned from a shared pre-trained base model) into a single unified network without incurring additional training costs. However, when these expert models are fine-tuned on highly orthogonal or conflicting domains, standard linear averaging of their parameter updates (such as in Task Arithmetic or TIES-Merging) leads to representation dilution, signal erasure, and catastrophic representational collapse.

## Proposed Approach
To mitigate weight-space interference, the authors propose **Exclusive Parameter Merging (EPM)**, which combines two core techniques:
1. **Soft Exclusive Parameter Allocation (Soft-EPA):** A training-free coordinate-level routing operator. For each coordinate in the model, Soft-EPA identifies the "dominant" expert model based on its **Task Vector Standardization** (global or layer-wise). The update from this dominant expert is applied at full strength, while the updates from all non-dominant experts are attenuated by a coherence retention factor $\gamma$ (default $\gamma = 0.2$ for dense merging). To prevent capacity starvation under high-sparsity merging, the authors introduce **Dynamic Coherence Scheduling (DCS)**, which dynamically scales $\gamma$ upward as a quadratic function of target sparsity $p$.
2. **Task-Level Coefficient Tuning (TLC-Tune):** A gradient-free optimization framework that calibrates only $K$ global scaling factors (one per expert model) using a (1+1) Evolution Strategy on a small validation set (128 samples per task). TLC-Tune directly optimizes a minimax validation metric to prevent any single task from collapsing, thereby raising the worst-case multi-task performance floor.

## Key Findings and Claims
1. **Mitigation of Representational Collapse:** The authors claim that EPM, combined with Task Vector Standardization, successfully levels the playing field under severe domain conflicts, elevating the accuracy of simple tasks (MNIST, FashionMNIST) from near-random levels (under standard average-based merging) to functional states.
2. **Empirical Outperformance:** The authors present empirical results on a Vision Transformer (ViT-Tiny) backbone across four datasets (MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, SVHN). Under dense merging, EPM with TLC-Tune is reported to achieve a joint mean accuracy of **46.19%**, outperforming Task Arithmetic (40.96%) and TIES-Merging (20.55%).
3. **Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox vs. Optimization Failure:** The authors argue that SOTA layer-wise test-time adaptation methods (such as AdaMerging and ZipMerge) suffer from absolute optimization failure (under-convergence) under zero-order search, while being highly vulnerable to transductive noise if continuously tuned on small calibration splits. In contrast, they claim TLC-Tune's 4-dimensional global scaling space is easily optimized and generalizes flawlessly.
4. **Coordinate Exclusivity as a Shield:** Under moderate sparsity ($p=0.5$), the authors claim that while standard average-based weight sharing (Standardized TA + Pruning) can achieve a slightly higher joint average performance, Soft-EPA acts as a crucial spatial safeguard that prevents individual expert collapse, raising the worst-case performance floor by over 43% absolute.

- **Introduction of EPM and Soft-EPA:** A novel coordinate-level routing protocol that utilizes Task Vector Standardization to resolve task scale imbalances. *Evidence:* Table 1, Table 2, and the "scale override" analysis showing that SVHN/CIFAR-10 would physically overpower MNIST/FashionMNIST at 13.8% of coordinates without standardization.
- **Dynamic Coherence Scheduling (DCS):** A schedule that dynamically scales $\gamma$ under sparse merging to prevent capacity starvation. *Evidence:* Table 3 and Table 4 showing improved joint mean at $p=0.8$.
- **Task-Level Coefficient Tuning (TLC-Tune):** A stable, low-dimensional optimization method using (1+1)-ES on 128 samples. *Evidence:* An optimization study showing rapid convergence (Figure 2, Table 5) and calibration size sensitivity sweeps (Table 6).
