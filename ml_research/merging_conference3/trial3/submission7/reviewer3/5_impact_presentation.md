# 5. Impact and Presentation Quality

## Major Strengths
* **Pragmatic Honesty & High-Signal Negative Results**: The paper is exceptionally honest and objective. Rather than inventing a complex heuristic to force a positive result, the authors report a highly significant negative result: that no test-time adaptive merging method beats a zero-overhead static uniform weight blend in low-fidelity settings. This is of immense value to practitioners, as it prevents waste on computationally heavy test-time optimization loops that degrade performance in real deployments.
* **Deconstruction of ES "Robustness"**: The deep analysis of zero-order optimization (1+1 ES) dynamics in high-dimensional spaces is a brilliant contribution. Deconstructing its apparent robustness as "optimization sluggishness" (failure to optimize under the curse of dimensionality, keeping weights close to the robust uniform initialization) is a refreshing and highly insightful contribution to the literature.
* **Comprehensive Unification of Granularity**: Unifying prior isolated merging methods (task arithmetic, AdaMerging, SplineMerge) into a nested structural granularity spectrum (Level 1 to 5) is an excellent, logical contribution that brings order to a fragmented field.
* **Excellent Presentation and Reproducibility**: The writing is crisp, technically precise, and highly structured. The inclusion of a comprehensive, transparent Appendix with exhaustive architectural parameters and training schedules is outstanding.

## Areas for Improvement
* **Evaluate on Fully-Converged, High-Fidelity Experts**: The most significant weakness is the reliance on poorly converged, extremely weak experts (e.g., 24.93% on CIFAR-10 and 17.50% on SVHN). Merging models that are barely better than random guessing introduces high-frequency parameter noise that is highly uncharacteristic of real-world production models. The authors should validate their findings on standard, fully-converged experts (e.g., standard fine-tuned models with high classification accuracies) to confirm if the same trade-off holds.
* **Evaluate on Standard Production-Scale Backbones**: While evaluating on a custom `ViTTiny` ($d_{\text{model}}=64$) is a useful stress-test for edge deployment, evaluating on at least one standard production-scale backbone (such as a standard ViT-Base, ResNet-50, or a small language model like LLaMA-1B) would massively boost the practical weight and industrial significance of the findings.
* **Beyond Entropy Minimization**: Since entropy minimization is highly prone to "confident but incorrect" predictions under transductive shift, evaluating semantically richer self-supervised surrogate losses (such as Centered Kernel Alignment drift or representation consistency) would strengthen the paper's practical recommendations.

## Overall Presentation Quality
The presentation quality is **excellent**. The narrative flows logically from the introduction of model merging, through the formulation of the granularity hierarchy, to the deep analytical discussion of the empirical results. The figures and tables are exceptionally clear, and the authors are highly careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and limitations of their study.

## Potential Impact and Significance
The potential impact on practical ML engineering and deployment is **significant**. 
Many teams are looking for cheap, training-free ways to achieve multi-task capabilities on the edge. Test-time adaptive model merging has been proposed as a promising approach. This paper serves as a vital cautionary tale for engineers, showing that fine-grained test-time adaptation is extremely fragile, highly prone to transductive overfitting, and often underperforms a simple, zero-overhead static uniform blend. By establishing the "Generalization-Granularity Trade-off" and pointing out the limitations of soft regularizations, this paper provides highly valuable guidelines that can save engineers massive amounts of time, complexity, and computational overhead.
