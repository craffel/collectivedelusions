# 2. Novelty Check

## Key Novel Aspects
* **Unified Structural Granularity Spectrum**: Instead of analyzing a single proposed heuristic, this paper provides the first comprehensive, systematic unification of model-merging parameter resolutions into a single nested hierarchy of five distinct levels (Global, Layer-wise, Block-wise, Component-wise, and Tensor-wise). This allows for a clean deconstruction of the generalization-granularity curve.
* **The "Optimization Sluggishness" Hypothesis**: The paper offers a highly original and honest scientific deconstruction of zero-order optimization dynamics in high dimensions. It demonstrates that 1+1 Evolution Strategies (ES)'s apparent robustness is not just due to "implicit regularization" but is heavily driven by optimization sluggishness (underfitting) under the curse of dimensionality. This prevents the optimizer from moving far from the initialization (the robust uniform baseline scale), thereby preserving generalizability.
* **Elastic Spatial Regularization (ESR) and depth-wise Total Variation (TV) for Merging**: The application of localized average-pulling spatial regularizers and layer-wise smoothness penalties specifically to model merging coefficients at test time represents a novel combination of established regularization concepts applied to a new, highly practical problem.

## Delta from Prior Work
* **Task Arithmetic / AdaMerging / SplineMerge**: Task arithmetic operates at the global scalar level (Level 1), AdaMerging at the layer-wise level (Level 2), and SplineMerge uses continuous splines as low-frequency filters over layers. This paper bridges these isolated works, showing that as resolution increases, unconstrained adaptation deteriorates rapidly.
* **Negative Results and Pragmatic Honesty**: Rather than claiming to beat all existing state-of-the-art methods, the paper highlights that **no adaptive merging configuration outperforms the static, zero-overhead Uniform Task Arithmetic baseline of 30.41%** in this low-resource setting. Pointing out the severe misalignment between the unsupervised surrogate loss (prediction entropy) and true classification accuracy under small calibration budgets ($N=256$) is a major delta from typical adaptive-merging papers that only report positive results on high-capacity models.

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this work is **significant**. While the individual components (e.g., Vision Transformers, 1+1 ES, Adam, L2 regularization) are standard, their combination and systematic evaluation are highly novel. Rather than introducing a speculative heuristic, the authors provide a deep, honest, and mathematically grounded analysis of a critical deployment challenge. From a practitioner's perspective, this systematic study provides extremely useful guidance on the limitations of test-time adaptation, helping prevent over-engineered, fragile deployments.
