\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The deployment of deep neural networks on edge devices—such as autonomous vehicles, mobile sensors, and outdoor robotic platforms—is fundamentally constrained by real-world physical limitations including limited on-chip memory, strict thermal envelopes, and battery capacity. 
In practice, these systems must concurrently handle diverse specialized tasks (e.g., classification, segmentation, and detection across various domains). 
Storing and executing separate full-parameter expert backbones for each individual task is highly impractical, as it leads to unsustainable storage costs and excessive DRAM-to-SRAM weight-transfer latency \cite{fedus2022switch}. 
On the other hand, multi-task joint retraining from scratch is computationally expensive, requires simultaneous access to all private training datasets, and is highly prone to catastrophic forgetting or sub-optimal task interference \cite{wortsman2022model, yadav2023resolving}.

To address these deployment challenges, \emph{model merging} has emerged as an exceptionally elegant, zero-inference-overhead paradigm. 
By performing a linear or non-linear combination of fine-tuned task-specific expert weights, model merging constructs a single unified backbone capable of multi-task execution without any retraining \cite{ilharco2022editing, matena2022merging, jin2023regmean}. 
To adjust blending coefficients dynamically on-device, modern frameworks utilize unsupervised Test-Time Adaptation (TTA) via entropy minimization on local unlabeled target-task streams, a technique pioneered by AdaMerging \cite{yang2024adamerging}. 
TTA allows the merged model to autonomously fine-tune its layer-wise coefficients to align with the active task distribution at runtime.

However, in real-world physical environments, edge devices consistently encounter highly non-ideal input streams. 
Cameras, LiDARs, and edge sensors suffer from physical degradation, including Gaussian sensor noise, defocus blur, dust occlusion, weather shifts (rain, snow, lighting changes), and heavy lossy compression artifacts. 
We discover that when adaptive model merging is subjected to these realistic corruptions, it experiences a devastating failure mode we term \textbf{Noise-Entropy Collapse}. 
Because prediction entropy under noise becomes highly distorted, standard first-order optimizers (like Adam) easily minimize the transductive loss but catastrophically damage the merged model's actual out-of-distribution generalization. 
This is a severe expression of the \emph{Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox} \cite{yang2024adamerging}: the optimizer fits high-frequency transductive noise in the adaptation batch, yielding highly jagged, oscillating layer-wise coefficient profiles that drift far from the optimal configuration. 
Under moderate noise, unconstrained AdaMerging collapses joint multi-task accuracy on a calibrated simulation of a Vision Transformer (ViT-B/32) from $84.44\%$ to $74.67\%$ (and to a catastrophic $59.05\%$ under extreme noise), with the out-of-distribution SVHN accuracy collapsing to $60.47\%$.

While conventional regularizers such as Total Variation (TV) roughness penalties or weight decay ($L_2$) can offer marginal relief, they are highly sensitive and require extensive continuous hyperparameter sweeps across tasks and noise levels to find a stable trade-off. 
This level of tuning is completely impractical for autonomous edge systems operating in dynamic, unpredictable physical environments.

To resolve this critical bottleneck, we propose \textbf{FlatMerge}, a highly robust and memory-efficient dual-regularized test-time model merging framework. 
FlatMerge is designed with the primary engineering philosophy that a deployed model must be robust to environmental noise without exceeding strict edge-hardware memory boundaries. 
We achieve this through a synergistic dual-regularization approach that trades off a manageable latency overhead for complete protection against activation memory overflow:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Subspace-Constrained Blending (PolyMerge):} Instead of optimizing $L$ independent layer-wise coefficients per task, we project and restrict the optimization search space to a low-degree polynomial of normalized layer depth ($d=2$) \cite{polymerge}. This mathematically forces depth-wise smoothness and reduces parameter dimensionality by over $90\%$, effectively filtering out high-frequency transductive noise.
    \item \textbf{Zeroth-Order Flatness-Aware Randomized Smoothing:} To prevent low-frequency transductive noise from causing the learned polynomial trajectory to drift, we introduce a Zeroth-Order (gradient-free) flatness-aware randomized smoothing formulation directly inside the compact polynomial coefficient space. Instead of minimizing entropy at a single sharp point estimate (which requires full backpropagation and activation caching), FlatMerge evaluates predictive entropy over randomized coefficient perturbations. By optimizing this smoothed objective, FlatMerge actively guides the adaptation toward flat entropy valleys that are robust to input corruptions, using only forward passes.
\end{enumerate}

Critically, because FlatMerge's optimization is Zeroth-Order and occurs within a highly compact coefficient space (e.g., just $3$ parameters per task, totaling $12$ parameters for $4$ tasks) rather than the millions of parameters of the underlying weight space, it completely eliminates the need to run any backpropagation through the model backbone or cache massive activation maps. Consequently, the peak memory during adaptation is identical to standard forward inference, and backpropagation is completely bypassed, satisfying the strict hardware budgets of resource-limited edge accelerators. While Zeroth-Order optimization increases execution latency due to forward perturbations, it provides complete protection against activation memory overflows on edge platforms.

Through exhaustive evaluation inside a highly calibrated numerical simulation of a 12-layer Vision Transformer (ViT-B/32) loss landscape, supplemented by physical empirical validation on real physical MLP and 5-layer CNN experts fine-tuned on MNIST, FashionMNIST, and KMNIST, we demonstrate that FlatMerge consistently out-performs existing baselines. 
Under moderate-to-severe input corruptions, FlatMerge achieves state-of-the-art robust joint accuracy, while simultaneously \textbf{reducing seed variance by more than $60\%$} in simulation and completely preventing the catastrophic transductive collapse of standard first-order TTA on physical weights.

In summary, our key practical contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item We expose and analyze the severe threat of physical test-time noise on adaptive model merging, characterizing the phenomenon of \textbf{Noise-Entropy Collapse} caused by the Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox.
    \item We propose \textbf{FlatMerge}, a plug-and-play dual-regularization TTA framework that combines low-degree polynomial subspace constraints with zeroth-order flatness-aware randomized smoothing in the compact coefficient space.
    \item We demonstrate that FlatMerge is highly resource-efficient, requiring \textbf{exactly zero activation memory caching} and \textbf{zero backbone backpropagation}, making it uniquely suited for edge accelerators with strictly bounded on-chip SRAM, while characterizing its exact execution latency trade-offs.
    \item Through extensive evaluations on a calibrated Vision Transformer simulation and physical validations on real MLP and 5-layer CNN models, we show that FlatMerge achieves state-of-the-art out-of-distribution accuracy under varying levels of physical noise, significantly outperforming standard AdaMerging and PolyMerge while dramatically stabilizing convergence and reducing seed variance.
\end{itemize}
