# 2. Novelty Check

## Key Novel Aspects
The paper introduces a novel application and synergistic integration of existing concepts to solve a critical, newly identified vulnerability in on-device test-time model merging:
1. **Identification and Analysis of Noise-Entropy Collapse:** While previous works like AdaMerging and RegCalMerge identified the "Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox" on clean transductive batches, this work is the first to systematically investigate and characterize the catastrophic "Noise-Entropy Collapse" of test-time model merging under physical input noise (sensor noise, corruptions, etc.).
2. **Coefficient-Space Flatness-Aware Minimization (SAM):** Standard Sharpness-Aware Minimization (SAM) is applied to network weights, which is computationally expensive and requires caching massive intermediate activations for double-backward passes. This paper's core novelty is applying flatness-aware minimization **exclusively inside the compact, low-dimensional polynomial coefficient blending space** ($D = K \times (d+1)$ parameters, e.g., $D=12$).
3. **Backpropagation-Free Zeroth-Order Randomized Smoothing:** To make this flatness optimization viable for edge accelerators, the authors formulate it using a gradient-free, Zeroth-Order (ZO) randomized smoothing gradient estimator. This completely eliminates backpropagation through the heavy backbone and avoids caching intermediate activation maps entirely, achieving a peak activation memory of 0 MB during adaptation.

## Delta from Prior Work
- **Delta from AdaMerging / RegCalMerge:** AdaMerging optimizes unconstrained blending coefficients layer-by-layer, which is extremely sensitive to test-time input noise. RegCalMerge introduces continuous regularizers (like Elastic Spatial Regularization) to smooth layer-wise variation, but these require task-specific hyperparameter sweeps and are highly sensitive. FlatMerge replaces these unconstrained spaces with a hard polynomial subspace constraint and a flatness optimizer, making it plug-and-play and robust without manual, task-specific tuning.
- **Delta from PolyMerge:** PolyMerge introduced the concept of restricting blending coefficients to a low-degree polynomial of layer depth to enforce depth-wise smoothness. However, PolyMerge assumes clean test-time data. The paper demonstrates that under physical noise, PolyMerge still suffers from low-frequency transductive drift where the polynomial trajectory overfits the systematic noise bias. FlatMerge adds zeroth-order flatness-aware optimization to actively stabilize this residual drift, which is a major robustness advancement.
- **Delta from Weight-Space SAM:** Standard SAM operates over millions of network weights, necessitating high-overhead backward passes. FlatMerge's ZO randomized smoothing operates in the coefficient space (e.g., 12 parameters), making the mathematical computation of flatness-aware gradients extremely lightweight and backpropagation-free.

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this paper is **significant and highly pragmatic**. 
While the individual building blocks—polynomial parameterization (PolyMerge), Sharpness-Aware Minimization (SAM), and Zeroth-Order randomized smoothing—are known in the machine learning literature, their unique, synergistic combination to solve the on-device model merging robustness bottleneck is highly creative and technically well-grounded. The paper does not merely transplant SAM; it re-engineers it as a Zeroth-Order coefficient-space optimizer to satisfy the strict, physical hardware constraints of edge deployment. This structural alignment between mathematical formulation and physical hardware limits elevates the work from an incremental combination to a highly cohesive and practical engineering solution.
