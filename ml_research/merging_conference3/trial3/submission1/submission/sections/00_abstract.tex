\begin{abstract}
Recent advances in model merging, such as Q-Merge, claim near-lossless performance when combining task-specific expert weights under post-training quantization (PTQ) constraints using Straight-Through Estimators (STE). In this work, we present a rigorous methodological deconstruction and independent robustness audit of Q-Merge, exposing critical, previously unstudied vulnerabilities in this paradigm. Through a multi-axial evaluation across diverse quantization operators, calibration characteristics, and optimization backends, we reveal that learned coefficient configurations overfit catastrophically to their source quantization operator, collapsing to random-guess performance (sub-10\%) when deployed under hardware-relevant target schemas. Furthermore, we show that standard unsupervised entropy minimization is highly fragile under non-idealized calibration streams, particularly under class imbalance, and that derivative-free 1+1 Evolution Strategy outperforms STE optimization on the source schema while suffering from severe generalization collapse under schema shift. Our findings highlight the severe risks of over-optimistic evaluation protocol trends and advocate for a transition toward more rigorous, cross-operator verification in weight-space fusion research.
\end{abstract}
