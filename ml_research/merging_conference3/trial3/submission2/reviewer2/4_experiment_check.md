# 4. Experimental Setup and Baselines Evaluation

## Critical Evaluation of the Experimental Setup
The experimental setup of the paper is highly rigorous and shows an exemplary commitment to statistical soundness:
- **Multi-Seed Evaluations:** Executing all continuous simulation experiments across **30 independent random seeds (42 to 71)** is an outstanding standard in machine learning research. It guarantees that the reported results are statistically representative and not the result of seed-cherrypicking. Similarly, the physical CNN validation uses **5 independent random seeds (42 to 46)**, which is standard and robust for physical deep training runs.
- **Calibrated Simulation:** The simulation is calibrated on empirical Vision Transformer (ViT-B/32) classification statistics across MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN, ensuring realistic range and domain behaviors.
- **Stress-Testing via Adversarial Streams:** To expose the vulnerabilities of online methods, the authors introduced Extreme Label Shift, Bursty Task Streams, and Small Batch Sizes. This is a highly appropriate and realistic experimental design.
- **Validation Selection Bias Sweeps:** Sweeping systematic validation domain shifts up to $30\%$ (isotropic Gaussian and structured late-layer) is an exceptionally strong way to verify the robustness of offline tuning.

---

## Baselines Evaluation
The paper evaluates a highly comprehensive set of baselines:
1. **Model Merging Baselines:** Task Arithmetic (Uniform), Online AdaMerging (Layer-wise), Online RegCalMerge, and Online PolyMerge ($d=2$). This represents the direct state-of-the-art in TTA model merging.
2. **Few-Shot Supervised Baselines (Physical CNN):** Few-Shot Joint Fine-Tuning (FT-Val) and Few-Shot Head-Only Tuning (Head-Val) on the validation set. These are essential baselines to compare against weight-space coefficient validation tuning.
3. **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The authors swept the base learning rate of Adam ($lr \in [10^{-4}, 10^{-1}]$) and elastic regularization weight ($\lambda$) to ensure the online TTA baselines were evaluated in their optimal configurations, making the comparisons highly fair and credible.

---

## Analysis of Whether the Claims are Supported by the Data
The empirical evidence provided in the paper strongly and convincingly supports its central claims:
- **OFS-Tune Superiority under Standard Streams (Supported):** Table 1 shows that OFS-Tune ($d=1, M=10$) achieves **85.89%** average accuracy, outperforming Uniform Task Arithmetic (84.44%) and completely dominating Online AdaMerging (79.72%) and Online RegCalMerge (80.70%), while outperforming Online PolyMerge (85.25%).
- **Fragility of Online TTA under Stream Shifts (Supported):** Table 2 documents that under Extreme Label Shift, Bursty Task Stream, and Small Batch Sizes, Online AdaMerging collapses significantly (dropping to 77.99%, 79.56%, and 79.90% respectively). Online RegCalMerge and PolyMerge also experience noticeable performance drops. In contrast, OFS-Tune maintains its static, deterministic accuracy of **85.89%** across all conditions.
- **The Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox (Supported):** Table 4 shows that under PyTorch Adam with scarce validation data ($M=5$), unconstrained 48-D layer-wise tuning overfits severely, getting only **80.78%** accuracy, whereas the 12-D Poly-Val ($d=2$) achieves **87.24%** (a 6.46% absolute improvement). Under Nelder-Mead, Layer-wise tuning gets **84.48%** (virtually identical to the starting Uniform baseline of 84.44%), exposing that Nelder-Mead's apparent resistance to overfitting in high dimensions is merely an optimization failure due to simplex-search stalling.
- **Catastrophic Dimensionality Collapse of Nelder-Mead (Supported):** Figure 3 and Appendix D document that Nelder-Mead on Poly-Val ($d=2$) completely stalls and fails to improve over the baseline when $K \ge 16$ tasks. In contrast, PyTorch Adam scales smoothly up to $K=64$ tasks ($768$ parameters).
- **Physical Validation (Supported):** Table 5 shows that physical experiments on deep convolutional networks mirror the simulation findings. Directly fine-tuning the classification head (Head-Val, 47.97%) or the entire network (FT-Val, 43.77%) on $M=10$ samples performs significantly worse than Uniform TA (55.27%) due to severe overfitting, whereas OFS-Tune Poly-Val ($d=1$) generalizes successfully, achieving **56.31%**. Under 30% label noise, Head-Tuning and Joint FT collapse to 38.34% and 35.87% respectively, while OFS-Tune Poly-Val remains perfectly robust at **56.35%**. Online AdaMerging collapses to **42.94%** (clean) and **42.30%** (noisy).

## Technical Critiques & Missing Empirical Details (Empiricist Perspective)
Despite the outstanding empirical rigor, an **empiricist** reviewer must raise the following critiques regarding the experimental design and presentation:

1. **Massive Discrepancy in Physical AdaMerging Performance:** In Table 5 (physical CNN experiments), Online AdaMerging collapses catastrophically to **42.94%** under a *clean, standard* test stream, which is significantly below the Uniform TA baseline of **55.27%**. However, in the simulated Standard Stream (Table 1), Online AdaMerging gets **79.72%** (closer to Uniform's 84.44%), and under sterile simulation conditions, it is shown to beat Uniform. This massive physical collapse suggests that prediction entropy minimization is fundamentally broken on this DeepCNN architecture, or that the learning rate used ($lr=10^{-3}$ as reported in Appendix A) was too high/unstable for the physical model. A physical evaluation on a pre-trained Vision Transformer (ViT-B/32) under standard clean streams would have clarified whether AdaMerging's SOTA claims are fragile solely on small CNNs or if they fail on large models under standard conditions too.
2. **Inductive Bias vs. Sample Complexity:** In Table 4 (Empirical Control), even when validation data is abundant ($M=50$ samples per task, 200 samples total), PyTorch Adam on Poly-Val ($d=2$) achieves **87.69%** simulated accuracy, outperforming the unconstrained 48-D Layer-wise search which gets **87.22%**. At $M=50$, validation noise should be highly minimized, yet the lower-dimensional polynomial space still generalizes better. This implies that the polynomial trajectory across depth is not merely a filter against small-sample noise, but serves as a highly beneficial **inductive bias** for weight-space model merging. The paper should have highlighted and discussed this distinction between structural inductive bias and sample-size regularization.
3. **Toy-Scale Task Cardinality in Physical Validation:** The physical CNN experiment is restricted to merging only 2 experts (MNIST and FashionMNIST). This represents a highly simplified, low-cardinality task setup ($K=2$). Given that the simulation highlights catastrophic dimensionality collapse for $K \ge 16$ tasks under Nelder-Mead, a physical validation scaling to at least 4 or 8 tasks (e.g., adding CIFAR-10 and SVHN to the CNN setup) would have been highly valuable to empirically verify if the scalability bottlenecks and PyTorch Adam scaling properties hold in physical weight-space model merging.
4. **Lack of Baseline Variance in Table 1:** In Table 1, the Uniform Task Arithmetic baseline is reported as $84.44\% \pm 0.00\%$, and the OFS-Tune methods have extremely low variance (e.g., $85.89\% \pm 0.01\%$). While the 30 independent seeds vary the underlying loss landscapes, the lack of variance in these static baselines suggests that the simulation landscape's optimal coefficients are highly concentrated, or that the coupled Model II sensitivity surfaces are extremely stable across seeds. In physical neural networks (Table 5), we see much more realistic variance (Uniform TA is $55.27\% \pm 6.60\%$ and OFS-Tune Poly-Val is $56.31\% \pm 5.17\%$). This discrepancy should be discussed to maintain transparency about the idealized nature of the continuous simulation.
