\begin{abstract}
Dynamic model merging (test-time expert blending) has emerged as a powerful paradigm to adapt neural networks dynamically to multi-task streams without expanding the physical footprint of the deployed model. However, its real-world performance is plagued by two critical vulnerabilities: (1) calibration data scarcity, which causes standard parametric routers to overfit and suffer from transductive collapse, and (2) deployment stream batch heterogeneity, where mixed-task batches cause routers to average representation dynamics across tasks, leading to ``heterogeneity collapse.'' In this work, we present a rigorous, large-scale empirical investigation into these failure modes and propose Confidence-Gated Hybrid Routing (CGHR) combined with Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH) to restore robustness. CGHR is a dual-pathway system that dynamically routes high-confidence samples using a flexible parametric router, while falling back to a robust, zero-shot parameter-free subspace router (PFSR) when parametric confidence is low. MBH isolates heterogeneous stream batches on the fly, preventing routing-logit smoothing. Conducting massive empirical sweeps across three critical scenarios (gating threshold sensitivity, data scarcity sample complexity, and deployment batch stream scales) over five independent random seeds, we show that CGHR + MBH consistently outperforms state-of-the-art baselines. Under extreme calibration data scarcity ($N=16$), CGHR maintains exceptional stability where standard parametric models fail. Under mixed-task streams, MBH ensures perfectly flat, robust, collapse-free performance curves across all batch sizes (up to $B=512$). Our exhaustive empirical analyses establish a robust, deployment-ready framework for test-time dynamic model merging.
\end{abstract}
