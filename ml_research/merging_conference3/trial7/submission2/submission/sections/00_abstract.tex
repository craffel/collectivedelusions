\begin{abstract}
Test-time model merging has emerged as a powerful paradigm for dynamically ensembling specialized domain-expert models without the need for intensive retraining. However, existing dynamic routing frameworks typically utilize over-parameterized parametric routers or unweighted parameter-free projections, which mathematically assume a flat, isotropic Euclidean weight space. This misspecification leads to catastrophic failure modes under extreme data scarcity, namely the \emph{Dynamic Routing Paradox} (overfitting under few-shot scenarios) and \emph{Vectorization Collapse} (instability under single-sample sequential streams). In this work, we propose \textbf{Fisher-Information Optimal Subspace Routing (FIOSR)}, a training-free and parameter-free framework that treats the parameter space as a Riemannian manifold. By utilizing the empirical diagonal Fisher Information Matrix (dFIM) of the expert heads, we construct an analytical local Riemannian metric tensor that warps the representation space based on parameter sensitivity, smoothly suppressing noisy dimensions while amplifying key task features. When combined with Class-Size Scaling Calibration (CSC) and Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH), FIOSR completely bypasses the need for test-time optimization. Our mathematical formulation achieves near-perfect specialized routing stability, matching the oracle ceilings of individual experts ($\approx 100\%$ on MNIST and FashionMNIST) and outperforming complex, highly parameterized routing baselines by up to $40.7\%$ under extreme, non-stationary heterogeneous stream batching.
\end{abstract}
