# 1. Summary

## Main Topic and Approach
The paper introduces **Fisher-Information Optimal Subspace Routing (FIOSR)**, a training-free and parameter-free dynamic ensembling framework designed for test-time model merging. In a modular parameter ensembling scenario (e.g., merging specialized expert adapters like LoRAs sharing a common pre-trained backbone), existing methods either utilize over-parameterized parametric routers that overfit under few-shot calibration data ("The Dynamic Routing Paradox") or suffer from high instability when transitioning to single-sample, low-latency sequential streams ("Vectorization Collapse"). Alternatively, prior parameter-free subspace routing (PFSR) methods project representations onto expert class prototypes using unweighted cosine similarity, which implicitly assumes a flat, isotropic weight space—geometrically and information-theoretically misspecifying the warped manifolds of fine-tuned expert models.

To resolve these issues, FIOSR treats the parameter space as a Riemannian manifold, using a smoothed and power-scaled diagonal empirical Fisher Information Matrix (dFIM) as an analytical local Riemannian metric tensor. Calculated over a microscopic calibration split, this dFIM warps the projection coordinate space during inference, suppressing noisy task-irrelevant feature dimensions (which exhibit high variance / low Fisher information) and amplifying highly informative task features (low variance / high Fisher information). 

To ensure stability against statistical biases and stream non-stationarity, FIOSR incorporates two additional mechanics:
1. **Class-Size Scaling Calibration (CSC):** Normalizes the similarity coordinates to correct for statistical maximum bias when experts have asymmetric class vocabulary sizes.
2. **Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH):** An unsupervised batch-partitioning algorithm that groups incoming heterogeneous samples into homogeneous micro-batches based on their dominant task coordinates, avoiding "heterogeneity collapse" without requiring multiple independent forward passes for every sample.

## Key Findings
- **High-Fidelity Routing and Accuracy in Synthetic Settings:** Across 10 random seeds inside a 192-dimensional synthetic "Analytical Coordinate Sandbox" modeling MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN, FIOSR achieves a mean joint ensembling accuracy of **76.86%**, outperforming the unweighted PFSR+MBH baseline by **8.56%** absolute (68.30%). It successfully recovers individual expert performance ceilings ($\approx 100\%$ for MNIST/FashionMNIST, near-ceiling for CIFAR-10, and exceeding the baseline ceiling by 27% for SVHN).
- **Immunity to Overfitting and Collapses:** Under heterogeneous stream batch sizes ranging from $B=1$ to $512$, FIOSR combined with MBH retains absolute performance stability (flatline around **76.83%**), while parametric baselines (Linear Router, QWS-Merge, L3-Softmax) catastrophically collapse to near-uniform accuracy ($\approx 36-39\%$) due to few-shot overfitting on the calibration set ($N=64$).
- **Real-World LoRA Activation Space Validation:** Under a 64-dimensional simulated LoRA setup modeling MNIST, FashionMNIST, and CIFAR-10 with anisotropic, correlated features from real activation variances, FIOSR achieves **95.00%** routing accuracy (a $+16.67\%$ absolute improvement over flat cosine) and **77.00%** joint ensembling accuracy (a $+6.67\%$ improvement), recovering **98.3%** of the oracle routing ceiling.
- **End-to-End Physical Validation (ResNet-18):** On a physical pre-trained ResNet-18 backbone evaluated on MNIST, FashionMNIST, and SVHN, FIOSR achieves a **59.00%** routing accuracy ($+2.67\%$ absolute gain over flat cosine PFSR) and a **52.00%** joint ensembling accuracy ($+1.33\%$ gain).

## Claimed Contributions and Evidence
1. **Information-Geometric Perspective on Test-Time Model Merging:** Treat parameter/representation spaces as Riemannian manifolds using the Fisher Information Metric. (Supported by mathematical formulations in Section 3 and robustness derivations in Appendix A.2).
2. **Smoothed and Power-Scaled Diagonal Fisher Regularizer:** Formulate dFIM as an analytical coordinate filter to suppress task-irrelevant noise in parameter-free routing. (Supported by ablation studies in Section 4.5 showing a performance drop of 8.56% when collapsing back to the flat Euclidean space).
3. **Robustness to Overfitting and Vectorization Collapse:** Bypassing test-time optimization using a parameter-free approach to survive low-latency streaming ($B=1$) and extreme data scarcity ($N_c \ge 8$). (Supported by mixed-task heterogeneous streaming experiments in Section 4.4 and $N_c$ sensitivity sweeps in Appendix B.3).
4. **Rigorous Empirical Evaluation against Baselines:** Evaluating against five major ensembling baselines over 10 random seeds in synthetic, LoRA-simulated, and physical ResNet-18 environments. (Supported by extensive tables and figures in Sections 4.3, 4.4, 4.6, 4.7, and 4.8).
