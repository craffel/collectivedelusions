# Evaluation Component 4: Experimental Evaluation and Claims Support

## Critical Evaluation of the Experimental Setup
A rigorous, empiricist review requires scrutinizing the experimental setup to determine if the findings are statistically sound, reproducible, and robustly validated.

1. **Heavy Over-Reliance on Synthetic Sandbox:** The primary quantitative evaluation is conducted on a closed, simulated "Isolating Coordinate Sandbox" ($L=14$ layers, $D=192$ hidden dimension, $K=4$ tasks) where task data and representations are generated with manually tuned coordinate scrambling and Gaussian noise. Expert adapters in this sandbox are analytically constructed via Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to mathematically reconstruct the identity matrix (un-scrambling the representations). While this sandbox functions as a valuable controlled laboratory to isolate representation-space mechanics, it is highly artificial. It completely bypasses the complex, non-linear, stochastic, and intertwined representations of real-world trained networks, making it a weak foundation for proving real-world systems-ML applicability.
2. **Suspiciously Perfect Results and Lack of Statistical Variance:**
   - In Table 1 (homogeneous streams) and Table 2 (heterogeneous streams), the proposed PFAB-BOP matches the absolute "Expert Ceiling" and the SOTA "PFSR+MBH" perfectly at **81.50% Joint Mean accuracy** (with exactly identical task-by-task scores of MNIST: 100.00%, F-MNIST: 100.00%, CIFAR-10: 96.00%, and SVHN: 30.00%). While hitting the expert ceiling indicates 100% correct routing, there is no discussion of routing error rates, confidence intervals, or statistical variance.
   - More critically, in Table 6 (DomainNet pilot with a pre-trained ViT-B/16), the paper reports that PFAB-BOP matches the Expert Ceiling *perfectly* at **78.80% Joint Mean accuracy**, down to the two-decimal-place precision for every single domain (Real: 84.80%, Sketch: 71.60%, Painting: 79.20%, Clipart: 79.60%). In a real-world visual domain dataset like DomainNet, achieving 100% routing accuracy on every single validation sample using a zero-parameter cosine similarity projection of penultimate activations is highly implausible. A single misclassified routing decision would alter the accuracy. This suspiciously perfect match suggests the "DomainNet pilot" may also be highly idealized, or the routing classification was deterministic and error-free, raising major questions about the realism and rigor of the organic validation.
   - No standard deviations, confidence intervals, or number of random seeds/runs are reported for any of the accuracy or latency metrics. This lack of error margins is a severe deficiency in scientific reporting.
3. **Fairness and Tuning of Baselines:**
   - Standard dynamic routing baselines (such as the Linear Router, L3-Linear, and QWS SOTA) are shown to collapse catastrophically to the static Uniform Merging baseline (28.60% or 29.10%) under heterogeneous streams. However, these baselines are evaluated under their default batch-pooling configurations. The paper does not explore whether these baseline routers could be executed sample-wise (similar to standard MoEs) to provide a fairer, more competitive comparison.
   - The "Jointly Trained Multi-Task Adapter" is a strong baseline that achieves 64.10% Joint Mean accuracy, illustrating the benefits of specialized expert model isolation. However, more advanced multi-adapter serving baselines are only discussed qualitatively.
4. **Subspace Entanglement Stress Test (Section 4.5):**
   - The paper shows that under severe leakage ($\epsilon = 0.5$), PFAB-BOP collapses to 51.30%. The proposed solution of SVD-orthogonalization restores performance to 80.50%. However, this SVD projection is evaluated as a post-hoc patch. There is no thorough ablation of how SVD orthogonalization scales with different numbers of tasks ($K$) or different leakage thresholds, nor is its decentralized counterpart (DSCP) quantitatively evaluated.
5. **Generative LLM Sequence Simulation (Section 4.6):**
   - The token-by-token sequence simulation on $T=50$ tokens shows that TSVHA with DGR achieves 100% Gating Synchrony. However, a 50-token simulation is extremely small and represents a toy setup. Evaluating this on long, complex, real-world text generation tasks with organic LLMs (e.g., LLaMA) is necessary to substantiate claims of viability for modern language modeling.

## Do the Results Support the Claims?
- **Claim: PFAB achieves SOTA routing quality with flat constant-time latency ($O(1)$ backbone complexity).**
  - **Support:** Partially supported. On the synthetic sandbox, PFAB-BOP matches SOTA accuracy while achieving flat latency profiles (2.52x speedup at $G=4$ active tasks). However, the results are limited to simulated scenarios and a single, highly idealized DomainNet pilot. The absolute speedup claims are heavily dependent on the PyTorch sequential-loop execution of the MBH baseline; production serving layers using compiled SGMV kernels may exhibit different latency crossover behaviors.
- **Claim: PFAB-ELC achieves single-pass execution with zero trainable parameters and constant-time complexity.**
  - **Support:** Supported, but with severe caveats. The single-pass ELC pathway experiences a massive 36% absolute accuracy drop on DomainNet compared to the expert ceiling due to semantic representation mismatch and fragility under organic covariate shifts. This indicates that while systems-efficient, the single-pass pathway is highly unreliable in organic settings.
- **Claim: Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC) eliminates scale drift.**
  - **Support:** Supported by the ablation study on the sandbox, where omitting UNC drops Joint Mean accuracy to 53.40%. However, this is not validated across a wide variety of organic model architectures.
