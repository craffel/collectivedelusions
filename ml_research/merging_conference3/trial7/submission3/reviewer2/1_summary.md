# Part 1: Comprehensive Summary

## 1. Main Topic and Scope
This paper addresses the problem of dynamic model merging in modular deep learning, where specialized expert neural networks (e.g., task-specific adapters, PEFT modules) are blended on-the-fly at inference time on a per-sample or per-layer basis. The core goal is to consolidate multiple specialized expert models into a single architecture without experiencing destructive parameter interference.

## 2. Core Methodological Approach: GP-DR and MBH
To resolve two primary limitations of standard dynamic routers—namely the **Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox** (catastrophic overfitting of gradient-based routers to tiny validation splits) and **Heterogeneity Stream Collapse** (loss of localized routing in heterogeneous streaming batches due to feature averaging)—the authors propose:
1. **Gaussian Process Dynamic Routing (GP-DR):** A training-free, non-parametric Bayesian framework. It treats a small set of frozen calibration samples (e.g., $N=64$ samples) as spatial landmarks in a low-dimensional representation space. Using a Gaussian Process (GP) prior with a Radial Basis Function (RBF) kernel (or other alternative kernels), it analytically computes dynamic parameter-merging coefficients in a single, closed-form forward pass. This completely eliminates backpropagation and the need to train routing network parameters.
2. **Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH):** A systems-level streaming buffer dispatch mechanism. It partitions incoming heterogeneous streaming batches into homogeneous micro-batches based on the router's task preferences. This isolates representation spaces during the forward pass and prevents representation-averaging collapse.
3. **Uncertainty-Guided Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Rejection:** GP-DR utilizes its closed-form posterior predictive variance as a measure of epistemic uncertainty. When high uncertainty is detected (indicating an OOD input), the system falls back to a uniform static blend across all experts to safeguard performance.

## 3. Key Findings
* **Zero-Optimization Stability:** On a 14-layer, 192-dimensional synthetic "Isolating Coordinate Sandbox," GP-DR achieves a **72.40% Joint Mean test accuracy** with zero training loops. This represents a $+42.40\%$ absolute improvement over standard unregularized parametric linear baselines which collapse under extreme data scarcity.
* **Stream-Level Recovery:** Under heterogeneous mixed-task evaluations, streaming dynamic routers without batch partitioning suffer from representation collapse, dropping to uniform merging performance (~25%-27%). Pairing GP-DR with MBH recovers performance to **70.20% accuracy** ($+42.80\%$ recovery margin).
* **Robustness Under Feature Coupling:** In scenarios with severe representational coupling (where task representation manifolds overlap), GP-DR's smooth Bayesian non-parametric prior maintains stable generalization, achieving **77.90% Joint Mean accuracy** (highly comparable to PFSR SOTA's 77.80%).
* **Real-World Validation:** On real-world text classification datasets (SST-2, CoLA, MRPC from the GLUE benchmark) using a pre-trained BERT-Tiny backbone, GP-DR achieves **45.78% Joint Mean accuracy** under low-data calibration ($N=48$). Pairing with MBH recovers streaming accuracy to **45.76%** ($+31.70\%$ recovery margin), and the system achieves a 100.00% orthogonal OOD detection rate.
* **OOD Rejection Limitations:** While the GP posterior variance achieves 100% AUROC on OOD samples mapped to the origin, it exhibits severe **unit-sphere variance collapse** under random unit-sphere noise. Under representational coupling and overlap, simpler distance-based heuristics (such as 5-NN Euclidean distance) vastly outperform GP-DR's posterior variance.

## 4. Explicitly Claimed Contributions and Supporting Evidence
* **Exposing Key Vulnerabilities:** The paper characterizes and empirically proves the existence of the Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox and Heterogeneity Stream Collapse. *Evidence:* Tables 1 and 3 demonstrate the collapse of parametric linear baselines at test-time under low calibration data and mixed-task streams.
* **Proposed GP-DR Framework:** Bypasses gradient updates, computing analytical closed-form posterior mean blending weights. *Evidence:* Formal derivations of the posterior mean and Proposition 3.1, proving that GPR posterior mean routing weights sum to exactly 1.0 (Sum-to-One Consistency) for any arbitrary input.
* **Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH):** Prevents vectorization collapse in streaming batches. *Evidence:* Table 3 and Figure 1b, showing massive recovery margins across multiple models when MBH is integrated.
* **Theoretical and Empirical OOD Analysis:** Exposing limitations and analyzing alternative kernels. *Evidence:* Detailed derivations of numerical safeguards, alternative kernels (Cosine, Mahalanobis, vMF), and empirical sweeps in Section 4.7 showing GPR posterior variance's vulnerability to unit-sphere noise and representational overlap.
