# 3. Soundness and Methodology Check

## Mathematical Rigor and Correctness
The mathematical formulation of the layer-wise dynamic model-merging framework is exceptionally clear, rigorous, and correct:
- **Interpolation Formulation:** Equations (1) to (3) clearly define the dynamic batch-averaging and physical weight-space parameter interpolation process, addressing how the latency of sample-by-sample model replication is avoided.
- **SVD Collinearity Diagnostic:** Section 3.4 defines the construction of the Batch-Averaged Layer-wise Coefficient Matrix $A \in \mathbb{R}^{L \times K}$ and uses SVD to compute the Collinearity Ratio $\rho_{collinear} = \sigma_1/\sum_i \sigma_i$. This is mathematically clean and provides a rigorous, scalar-based metric for auditing routing dimensionality.
- **Inter-Layer Cosine Similarity:** The pairwise inter-layer cosine similarity matrix formulation $S_{l, l'} = (A_l \cdot A_{l'}) / (\|A_l\|_2 \|A_{l'}\|_2)$ is correct and acts as a strong visual diagnostic.
- **Statistical Robustness:** SVD is performed on each random seed's batch-averaged matrix $A$ independently, and the mean and standard deviation of $\rho_{collinear}$ are reported across the 5 independent seeds. This incorporates stochastic optimization variations and ensures statistical soundness.

## Bounded Sigmoid (BSigmoid) Router Design & Decoupled Gradients
The design of the Bounded Sigmoid (BSigmoid) router is mathematically and conceptually solid, and the authors have added a strong explanation to address the "Normalization Paradox":
- **Dimensionality Reduction:** The grayscale input is projected from $D=784$ to a low-dimensional space $d=8$ using a random frozen Gaussian matrix $P_{proj}$. The choice of $d=8$ is well-rationalized as a regularizer to control parameters under tight few-shot calibration budgets.
- **The Normalization Paradox:** The author exposes a deep, critical paradox: independent Sigmoids are used to avoid the zero-sum competitive constraint of Softmax, but sum-to-1 normalization (Equation 10) must be applied to prevent exponential activation drift across $L$ layers. This normalization mathematically re-introduces the very competitive constraints the Sigmoid was designed to bypass!
- **Decoupled Gradient Paths Explanation:** In response to previous feedback, the authors have added an elegant mathematical explanation for why the normalized BSigmoid heavily outperforms Softmax (e.g., $52.52\%$ vs $28.33\%$ on TinyCNN-4 Cross-Domain). They show that while standard Softmax couples logits at the exponential level (leading to premature optimization collapse when steep gradients of easy tasks suppress harder tasks), the BSigmoid applies independent gates first. This decouples the gradient paths during backward propagation, protecting individual expert updates from being overridden.
- **Remaining Gap:** While this theoretical explanation is mathematically sound and highly compelling, it is presented as a pure theoretical narrative. The paper lacks any empirical validation of this gradient decoupling hypothesis—such as a plot of gradient norms or loss curves comparing Softmax and BSigmoid during the 40 calibration steps—which would make the argument empirically watertight.

## The Routing Noise Hypothesis under MLP Collapse
To address the concern of whether the observed drop in SVD Collinearity Ratio on deep MLPs represents true semantic depth-specialization or is merely a symptom of optimization noise in a functionally collapsed network, the authors have conducted a controlled ablation study and analyzed the learned routing trajectories under artificial noise.
- **The Noise Test:** They find that the $L_2$ regularizer ($\gamma=10^{-4}$) restricts the parameter space, keeping coefficients smooth across contiguous blocks. Furthermore, their inter-layer cosine similarity maps (Figure 2, right) show highly structured block-diagonal clusters rather than chaotic, noisy patterns, confirming that the multi-dimensional trajectory represents true layer-group specialization rather than overfitting to random noise.
- **Remaining Limitation:** This analysis successfully refutes the Routing Noise Hypothesis. However, we must highlight that on DeepMLP-12 under Cross-Domain task conflict, the absolute accuracy of the Layer-wise Router ($16.15\% \pm 5.60\%$) remains extremely close to the random guessing barrier of $12.5\%$. In a network where coordinate alignment is completely broken across 12 dense non-linear layers, the forward activations are essentially corrupted. Analyzing routing trajectories in a functionally collapsed regime is of limited practical use, and the spectral findings on this specific architecture should be interpreted with caution.

## The Batch-Averaged Multi-Task Inference Paradox
Section 3.5 formalizes the **Batch-Averaged Multi-Task Inference Paradox**, showing that dynamic merging under a physical serving environment is subject to a logical dilemma:
1. **Heterogeneous Streams (Mixed-Batch Collapse):** Batch-averaging dynamic routing coefficients over heterogeneous batches mathematically smooths the coefficients toward a uniform compromise, collapsing the "dynamic" system back to static uniform merging.
2. **Homogeneous Streams (Homogeneous-Batch Redundancy):** If the serving engine has the task labels a priori to group inputs into homogeneous batches to prevent collapse, the entire model-merging system becomes redundant because the system could simply route the homogeneous batches directly to the specialized expert models (the Oracle).
This paradox represents a profound conceptual contribution that challenges the core utility of dynamic merging on full weights under standard deployments and guides future research toward PEFT-level serving (such as LoRA with sample-specific forward passes).

Overall, while the theoretical and empirical grounding has been significantly strengthened with the decoupled gradient paths and the routing noise analysis, the paper still faces small remaining gaps in empirical validation for its gradient theories and functional limitations under dense MLP collapse.
