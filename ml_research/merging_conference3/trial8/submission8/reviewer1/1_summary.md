# Paper Evaluation: 1. Summary

## Main Topic of the Paper
The paper investigates the robustness and sample complexity of out-of-distribution (OOD) task rejection modules within dynamic model merging and serving frameworks on resource-constrained edge hardware. Specifically, it focuses on Single-Pass Sample-Wise Routing architectures (such as SPS-ZCA and SABLE) that project early-layer hook activations (e.g., from Layer 3 of a Vision Transformer) onto task centroids to obtain a low-dimensional coordinate representation space. Standard approaches fit diagonal Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) over these coordinates during an offline calibration phase and use log-likelihood thresholds to reject OOD queries. The paper exposes a critical overfitting and variance collapse vulnerability when these GMMs are trained on tiny calibration sets ($N \le 64$), leading to unstable density boundaries and high false-rejection/false-acceptance rates under realistic serving noise or representation-level covariate shift.

## Proposed Approach
To rectify low-resource variance collapse without introducing static, non-adaptive regularization bias, the authors propose **SRC-DE** (Shrinkage-Regularized Coordinate Density Estimation). 
Key components of the approach include:
1. **Coordinate-Space Projection:** Projecting early hidden states (specifically from Layer 3 of a frozen backbone) using cosine similarity against pre-computed task centroids to map queries into an interpretable $\mathbb{R}^K$ coordinate space.
2. **Post-Fit Ledoit-Wolf Covariance Shrinkage:** Applying an analytical, parameter-free Ledoit-Wolf-style covariance shrinkage immediately following Expectation-Maximization (EM) convergence. The empirical diagonal covariance matrix is regularized by shrinking it toward a target matrix:
   - A **Global Coordinate-Wise Diagonal Target** ($T = \text{diag}(\sigma^2_{\text{global}})$) is used for small registries ($K=4$) to preserve coordinate scales and avoid sphericity bias.
   - A **Spherical Diagonal Target** ($T = \nu I$) is used for high-dimensional task registries ($K \ge 16$).
3. **Analytical Estimation of Shrinkage Intensity ($\alpha_{\text{opt}}$):** Deriving the optimal shrinkage strength $\alpha_{\text{opt}}$ dynamically per mixture component based on the local sample size and coordinate variance of variance estimators.
4. **Scikit-Learn Cholesky Bug Fix:** Overwriting the cached `precisions_cholesky_` in standard python GMM libraries to ensure that post-fit manual covariance adjustments are actually utilized during density scoring.
5. **Distribution-Free Threshold Calibration:** A percentile-based safety threshold heuristic ($\eta_k$) that uses only in-distribution calibration samples to bound the False Positive Rate (FPR) of in-distribution data.

## Key Findings
1. **Symmetric Evaluation Necessity:** Prior OOD evaluation pipelines suffered from an *unequal noise confounder* where representation-level noise was applied only to in-distribution samples, artificially collapsing their similarity coordinates toward zero and dropping the OOD rejection AUC below 0.50. Symmetrizing the evaluation setup resolves this experimental confounder.
2. **Raw Cosine Dominance and the Curse of Dimensionality:** In a clean independent coordinate setup, non-parametric 1D Raw Cosine thresholding consistently and significantly outperforms multi-dimensional GMM models under noise (e.g., a gap of up to ~14% to 18% AUC). This is due to the curse of dimensionality (where noise accumulates across the $K-1$ inactive coordinate dimensions with tiny variances, burying the active coordinate's signal) and monotonicity (GMM penalizes deviations in both directions, whereas Raw Cosine only penalizes low similarities).
3. **U-Shaped GMM Sample Complexity Curve:** Under unregularized GMMs with $M=2$ components, the sample complexity performance follows a non-monotonic U-shaped curve. Under extreme data scarcity ($N \in [8, 16]$), EM fails to split components, reverting to a stable $M=1$ model. At $N=32$, EM succeeds in splitting but lacks sample abundance to estimate parameters stably, causing variance collapse and dragging down the AUC, which recovers only when $N \to 256$.
4. **Stabilizing Effect of SRC-DE:** Covariance shrinkage successfully mitigates local variance collapse, significantly improving the AUC of multi-component models ($M=2$) across noise levels (e.g., a +3.57% absolute AUC gain at $\sigma^2 = 0.05$ and +4.28% absolute AUC gain at $\sigma^2 = 0.20$ over unregularized GMMs).
5. **Architectural Crossover under Overlapping Task Registries:** In overlapping registries (where OOD queries have high similarity on multiple registered tasks simultaneously), Raw Cosine and Independent 1D GMMs suffer from severe routing false positives (AUC drops to ~0.75 and ~0.72, respectively) because they evaluate coordinates in isolation. In low dimensions ($K=4$), a full joint GMM with Noise-Adapted SRC-DE achieves an outstanding AUC of $0.8137$ (+5.57% over Raw Cosine) by capturing joint coordinate dependencies. However, in high dimensions ($K \ge 16$), the curse of dimensionality dominates, and independent 1D models (which bypass scaling noise) are preferred.

## Explicitly Claimed Contributions (and Evidence)
1. **Comprehensive Methodological Audit:** Exposing the vulnerability of unregularized GMMs to representation-level covariate shift and low-resource variance collapse (evidenced by Experiments 1 & 2 showing catastrophic AUC drops for unregularized models).
2. **Identification and Resolution of the Unequal Noise Confounder:** Establishing a symmetric, scientifically valid evaluation protocol for OOD task rejection (evidenced by the symmetric noise evaluation setup detailed in Section 4.4 and visual plots).
3. **SRC-DE Pipeline:** A training-free, mathematically rigorous, and adaptive covariance-shrunk coordinate density estimator (formulated in Section 3 and detailed in Algorithm 1).
4. **Empirical Superiority & bug fixes:** Demonstrating up to +4.63% absolute AUC improvement in high-dimensional registries and documenting/resolving the scikit-learn covariance caching bug (evidenced by Table 3, Table 4, and Section 4.3).
5. **On-Device Profiling and Systems Benchmarks:** Demonstrating that diagonal coordinate GMM routing is computationally lightweight, requiring <0.33 ms for single-query inference and <3.2 KB of RAM, making it highly compatible with physical resource-constrained microcontrollers (evidenced by Table 11).
