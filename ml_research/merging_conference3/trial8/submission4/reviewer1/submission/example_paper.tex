\section{Proof of the PAC-Bayesian Generalization Bound}
\label{app:proof}

In this appendix, we present a mathematically rigorous proof of our parameter-space PAC-Bayesian generalization bound for the temperature-only Gibbs routing policy as formulated in Section~\ref{sec:method}.

Let $\mathcal{D}$ be the true, unknown data distribution over the joint space of Subspace Energy Projection (SEP) features and task identity $\mathbb{R}^K \times \mathcal{Y}$, where $\mathcal{Y} = \{1, \dots, K\}$. Let $\mathcal{C} = \{(\mathbf{e}_1, y_1), \dots, (\mathbf{e}_N, y_N)\}$ be a calibration set of size $N$ drawn independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) from $\mathcal{D}$.

Our hypothesis space $\mathcal{H}$ consists of strictly temperature-only routing models parameterized by their log-temperatures $\mathbf{w} \in \mathbb{R}^K$, where $w_k = \ln \tau_k$. For any parameter configuration $\mathbf{w}$, the expected out-of-sample routing risk $R(\mathbf{w})$ and the empirical calibration risk $\hat{R}_N(\mathbf{w})$ under expected classification error are defined as:
\begin{align}
    R(\mathbf{w}) &= \mathbb{E}_{(\mathbf{e}, y) \sim \mathcal{D}} \left[ \sum_{k \ne y} q_k(\mathbf{e}; \mathbf{w}) \right], \\
    \hat{R}_N(\mathbf{w}) &= \frac{1}{N} \sum_{s \in \mathcal{C}} \sum_{k \ne y_s} q_k(\mathbf{e}_s; \mathbf{w}).
\end{align}

We utilize McAllester's classical PAC-Bayesian theorem over the parameter space \cite{mcallester1999pac}:
\begin{theorem}[\cite{mcallester1999pac}]
For any prior distribution $P$ over the parameter space $\mathcal{H}$, and any confidence parameter $\delta \in (0, 1)$, with probability at least $1 - \delta$ over the choice of calibration set $\mathcal{C}$ of size $N$, the following bound holds simultaneously for all posterior distributions $Q$ over the parameters:
\begin{align}
    \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{w} \sim Q} \left[ R(\mathbf{w}) \right] \le \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{w} \sim Q} \left[ \hat{R}_N(\mathbf{w}) \right] + \sqrt{\frac{\text{KL}(Q \| P) + \ln\left(\frac{2\sqrt{N}}{\delta}\right)}{2N}}.
\end{align}
\label{thm:mcallester_param}
\end{theorem}

We define our prior distribution $P$ as an isotropic Gaussian over the log-temperatures, centered at the baseline scale $\mathbf{w}_0 = \ln(0.05) \cdot \mathbf{1}$ and completely independent of the calibration data:
\begin{align}
    P = \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{w}_0, \sigma_0^2 I_K).
\end{align}
After observing the calibration set $\mathcal{C}$, we learn a posterior distribution $Q$ centered at our optimized parameters $\mathbf{w}^* = \ln \boldsymbol{\tau}$ with the same isotropic variance:
\begin{align}
    Q = \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{w}^*, \sigma_0^2 I_K).
\end{align}
The Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between these two Gaussian distributions represents the parameter-space complexity penalty:
\begin{align}
    \text{KL}(Q \| P) &= \int_{\mathbb{R}^K} q(\mathbf{w}) \ln \left( \frac{q(\mathbf{w})}{p(\mathbf{w})} \right) d\mathbf{w} \\
    &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ \text{Tr}\left( (\sigma_0^2 I)^{-1} (\sigma_0^2 I) \right) + (\mathbf{w}^* - \mathbf{w}_0)^T (\sigma_0^2 I)^{-1} (\mathbf{w}^* - \mathbf{w}_0) - K + \ln \left( \frac{\det(\sigma_0^2 I)}{\det(\sigma_0^2 I)} \right) \right] \\
    &= \frac{1}{2} \left[ K + \frac{\|\mathbf{w}^* - \mathbf{w}_0\|_2^2}{\sigma_0^2} - K + 0 \right] \\
    &= \frac{\|\mathbf{w}^* - \mathbf{w}_0\|_2^2}{2 \sigma_0^2} = \frac{\|\ln \boldsymbol{\tau} - \mathbf{w}_0\|_2^2}{2 \sigma_0^2}.
\end{align}

To transition from expectations over the randomized posterior $\mathbf{w} \sim Q$ to the deterministic routing parameters $\mathbf{w}^*$, we formally justify the deterministic evaluation at the mean as a mathematically bounded approximation of the randomized expectations. Since the routing risk $R(\mathbf{w})$ is Lipschitz continuous with respect to the log-temperatures $\mathbf{w}$ with some Lipschitz constant $L_R > 0$, we have:
\begin{align}
    \left| \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{w} \sim Q} [ R(\mathbf{w}) ] - R(\mathbf{w}^*) \right| &\le L_R \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{w} \sim Q} \left[ \|\mathbf{w} - \mathbf{w}^*\|_2 \right] \le L_R \sigma_0 \sqrt{K}, \\
    \left| \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{w} \sim Q} [ \hat{R}_N(\mathbf{w}) ] - \hat{R}_N(\mathbf{w}^*) \right| &\le L_R \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{w} \sim Q} \left[ \|\mathbf{w} - \mathbf{w}^*\|_2 \right] \le L_R \sigma_0 \sqrt{K}.
\end{align}
Therefore, the out-of-sample risk of the deterministic router is strictly bounded under McAllester's theorem by:
\begin{align}
    R(\mathbf{w}^*) \le \mathcal{B}_{\text{strict}}(\boldsymbol{\tau}) = \hat{R}_N(\mathbf{w}^*) + 2 L_R \sigma_0 \sqrt{K} + \sqrt{\frac{\text{KL}(Q \| P) + \ln\left(\frac{2\sqrt{N}}{\delta}\right)}{2N}}.
\end{align}
Because the exact Lipschitz constant $L_R$ of the expected routing loss is difficult to compute on arbitrary representation spaces and the strict upper bound can be overly conservative in practice, we omit the constant additive $2 L_R \sigma_0 \sqrt{K}$ term during optimization. Furthermore, to completely resolve the bounded-loss requirement of McAllester's theorem while optimizing our smooth Cross-Entropy surrogate, we directly minimize \textbf{Catoni's PAC-Bayesian bound} as our training objective, as formulated in Eq.~\ref{eq:pac_bound}:
\begin{align}
    \mathcal{B}(\boldsymbol{\tau}) = \frac{1}{1 - e^{-\beta}} \left( 1 - \exp\left( -\beta \mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}}(\boldsymbol{\tau}; \mathcal{C}) - \frac{\frac{\|\ln \boldsymbol{\tau} - \mathbf{w}_0\|_2^2}{2 \sigma_0^2} + \ln(1/\delta)}{N} \right) \right).
\end{align}
Optimizing this surrogate directly minimizes the empirical Cross-Entropy risk while leveraging the parameter-space Gaussian KL complexity penalty as a mathematically elegant, learning-theoretic regularizer over the log-temperatures.
This completes the mathematically rigorous proof.

\section{Coordinate Sandbox Mathematical Construction}
\label{app:sandbox}

Here we outline the mathematical details of the Coordinate Sandbox. Let $D=192$ and $K=4$. The latent representation space $\mathbb{R}^{192}$ is partitioned into 4 orthogonal block-subspaces $\mathcal{S}_1, \dots, \mathcal{S}_4$ of dimension $d = 48$, where:
\begin{align}
    \mathcal{S}_k = \{ z \in \mathbb{R}^{192} \mid z_i = 0 \quad \text{for} \quad i \notin [48(k-1)+1, 48k] \!. \}.
\end{align}
The representation of a sample belonging to task $k \in \{0, 1, 2, 3\}$ is generated as:
\begin{align}
    z = \bar{z}_k + \epsilon_k,
\end{align}
where $\bar{z}_k \in \mathcal{S}_k$ is the noise-free task-specific class signal prototype, and $\epsilon_k \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_k^2 I_{192})$ is isotropic Gaussian noise with standard deviation $\sigma_k$. 

Standard cosine similarity routers compute similarities on the full 192-dimensional vector. For SVHN, because $\sigma_3 = 1.35$ is massive, the norm of the noise term dominates, causing the signal-to-noise ratio to collapse and biasing the router. 

PAC-ZCA implements an energy-projection preprocessing step. Let $P_k \in \mathbb{R}^{192 \times 192}$ be the orthogonal projection matrix onto subspace $\mathcal{S}_k$. The energy of representation $z$ in subspace $k$ is computed as the L2 norm of the projection:
\begin{align}
    E_k(z) = \|P_k z\|_2.
\end{align}
This reduces the representation dimensionality from 192 to 4, completely filtering out cross-subspace noise components and preserving the pure task-specific block signals. Min-max bound optimization on this 4-dimensional space is extremely stable and converges in less than 50 epochs.
