# Intermediate Review Evaluation 1: Summary of the Paper

## Main Topic and Objective
The submission addresses the operational bottleneck in dynamic test-time model ensembling of Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) experts, where state-of-the-art methods (such as SPS-ZCA and SABLE) rely on pre-computing task-routing centroids from offline, labeled calibration splits. The paper aims to develop a completely unsupervised and calibration-free framework for dynamic model merging at test time.

## Proposed Approach and Methodology
The authors propose and evaluate two primary paradigms for zero-shot calibration-free model merging:
1. **Zero-Shot Expert Entropy Routing (EER):** This is an accuracy-focused direct-routing paradigm that extracts intermediate representations from early, shared backbone layers, passes them through all specialized experts in parallel, and routes the sample to the expert that minimizes the **Normalized Shannon prediction entropy** ($\bar{H}(p_k(x_b)) = \frac{H(p_k(x_b))}{\log(Y_k)}$). This normalization is designed to guarantee scale-invariance when vocabulary sizes $Y_k$ differ across tasks.
2. **Entropy-Pseudo-Labeled Online Centroid Adaptation (EPL-OCA):** This is an efficiency-focused ensembling paradigm. It maintains running task centroids updated on-the-fly via exponential moving averages using pseudo-labels generated by EER. It uses cosine similarity to the running centroids to compute ensembling coefficients and blends expert activations in a single pass using Single-Pass Activation-Space Dynamic Blending (SPS).
3. **Centroid-Gated Entropy Routing (CG-EER):** A hybrid, semi-supervised framework developed to address out-of-distribution (OOD) overconfidence on real embeddings by gating out experts whose offline-computed centroids have low cosine similarity to the input.
4. **Unsupervised Centroid-Gated Entropy Routing (UCG-EER):** A fully unsupervised version of CG-EER where the gating centroids are updated online on-the-fly.

To optimize serving latency under post-activation divergence (as LoRA adapters swap in at Layer 4 and diverge activations), the authors propose **Amortized Pseudo-Labeling**, where expert entropy calculations and centroid updates are executed once every $N_{\text{amortize}} = 10$ steps, caching and reusing routing decisions in between.

## Key Findings and Explicit Claims
- **EER Sandbox Performance:** In a synthetic 192-dimensional representation space sandbox (composed of four orthogonal 48-dimensional task subspaces, modeling MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN), EER achieves **71.38%** Joint Mean accuracy under heterogeneous shuffled streams, outperforming the supervised SPS-ZCA baseline by $+4.62\%$.
- **Robustness to Drift:** Under gradual linear representation drift ($d=0.45$), EER maintains **71.18%** Joint Mean accuracy, preserving robustness completely calibration-free.
- **EPL-OCA Degradation (Representational Sparsity Paradox):** EPL-OCA achieves a lower Joint Mean accuracy of **49.88%** in the sandbox. The authors attribute this to class orthogonality within task subspaces creating spatial dispersion, which causes centroids to jitter and degrades cosine similarity routing.
- **EPL-OCA Collapse on Real Embeddings:** On real 512-dimensional ResNet-18 embeddings, EPL-OCA collapses to **27.45%** (hard routing) and **31.52%** (soft ensembling), failing to beat static Uniform Weight Merging (31.66%). This collapse is attributed to the **Entropy Calibration Discrepancy** where simpler experts (like MNIST) exhibit OOD overconfidence, causing a self-referential pseudo-label feedback loop that corrupts running centroids.
- **CG-EER Resolution:** CG-EER achieves **61.50%** Joint Mean accuracy on real features, outperforming supervised SPS-ZCA by $+0.70\%$, by utilizing pre-computed offline task centroids to restrict routing.
- **UCG-EER Collapse:** Fully unsupervised gating (UCG-EER) collapses to **28.45%** due to the self-referential loop, motivating the need for hybrid semi-supervised designs on real embeddings.
- **Systems Profiling:** Amortized EER reduces CPU latency on an AMD EPYC single core to **0.2211 ms per sample** ($1.57\times$ overhead compared to a single-pass expert) and reduces energy consumption by $4.14\times$ ($0.11\ \mu\text{J}$ per sample).

## Explicitly Claimed Contributions (with Evidence)
1. **First study of completely calibration-free, zero-shot dynamic model ensembling:** Tested against static Uniform Merging, PFSR, and supervised SPS-ZCA.
2. **Proposing and evaluating EER:** Shown to yield 71.38% sandbox accuracy, surviving linear domain drift.
3. **Chronological data leakage fix:** Fixing centroid updates to happen after routing and prediction, evaluated in EPL-OCA.
4. **Validation on real ResNet-18 embeddings & identifying Entropy Calibration Discrepancy:** Formulation of CG-EER to resolve overconfidence.
5. **Evaluating UCG-EER & identifying self-referential corruption loops:** Empirically showing collapse across various warm-up windows $T_{\text{warmup}} \in [10, 50, 100, 200]$.
6. **Mathematical complexity formulation & profiling:** FLOP analysis showing $0.25+0.75K$ passes and CPU latency benchmarks.
