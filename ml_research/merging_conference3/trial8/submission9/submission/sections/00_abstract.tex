\begin{abstract}
While dynamic test-time model ensembling of Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) experts enables highly flexible multi-task on-device serving, state-of-the-art frameworks (such as SPS-ZCA and SABLE) require pre-computing task-routing centroids from offline, labeled calibration splits. This requirement introduces a severe operational bottleneck in privacy-restricted, zero-downtime, or streaming edge environments where labeled data is completely unavailable. To resolve this bottleneck, we present a comprehensive, technically rigorous, and honest systems-ML study of \textbf{Zero-Shot Calibration-Free Model Merging}, evaluating and comparing two primary paradigms under heterogeneous streams and extreme continuous covariate shift: (1) \textbf{Zero-Shot Expert Entropy Routing (EER)} [Accuracy-First] and (2) \textbf{Entropy-Pseudo-Labeled Online Centroid Adaptation (EPL-OCA)} [Efficiency-First].

EER maps incoming samples on-the-fly to the expert exhibiting the minimum scale-invariant Normalized Shannon prediction entropy (maximum confidence). Evaluating these paradigms inside a synthetic 192-dimensional representation space sandbox (modeling MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN using Gaussian manifolds), we show that EER achieves an outstanding \textbf{71.38\%} Joint Mean accuracy under heterogeneous shuffled streams, outperforming the supervised SOTA baseline (SPS-ZCA) by \textbf{+4.62\%} absolute accuracy, and maintains complete robustness under extreme representation drift ($d=0.45$, delivering \textbf{71.18\%} Joint Mean). Conversely, we highlight a critical methodological limitation of EPL-OCA: while it performs reasonably in our synthetic sandbox, it \textbf{completely collapses on real 512-dimensional ResNet-18 embeddings} ($27.45\%$ for hard routing, and $31.52\%$ for soft blending), failing to outperform a simple static \textbf{Uniform Weight Merging} baseline ($31.66\%$). This total collapse occurs because the Entropy Calibration Discrepancy corrupts the online running centroids via a self-referential pseudo-label loop.

To resolve this on real features, we evaluate \textbf{Centroid-Gated Entropy Routing (CG-EER)}, achieving \textbf{61.50\%} accuracy (outperforming supervised SPS-ZCA by \textbf{+0.70\%}). We explicitly and prominently delineate CG-EER as a hybrid, semi-supervised framework, rather than a purely calibration-free one, since it relies on pre-computed offline task centroids to restrict entropy routing to candidate experts near the sample. Attempting to make this gating completely calibration-free via unsupervised online centroids (\textbf{UCG-EER}) results in a catastrophic accuracy collapse to \textbf{28.45\%} across all warm-up windows, due to a self-referential pseudo-label corruption loop. This formally demonstrates the inherent challenges and fundamental limits of fully unsupervised test-time adaptation on uncalibrated real embeddings, mathematically and empirically motivating our hybrid semi-supervised design. Finally, we analyze the physical edge-device serving overhead under post-activation divergence, demonstrating that Amortized EER reduces CPU latency to a highly practical $1.57\times$ overhead and slashes edge energy footprint by $4.14\times$ while preserving high accuracy ($71.20\%$).
\end{abstract}
