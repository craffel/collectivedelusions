\begin{abstract}
Model merging has emerged as a powerful paradigm for combining multiple task-specific expert adapters into a single, unified backbone without the prohibitive cost of multi-task training. However, existing state-of-the-art dynamic merging and routing schemes rely on a fundamental, yet unexamined, assumption: that activation representations and task boundaries are best modeled in flat Euclidean space ($\mathbb{R}^D$). In this work, we show that this assumption leads to severe \emph{representation crowding} and destructive inter-task \emph{cross-talk} near the origin, causing substantial accuracy degradation under heterogeneous multi-task streams. 
To overcome this limitation, we present \textbf{HyperMerge} (\textbf{Hyperbolic Space Activation Routing and Fusion}), a paradigm-shifting approach that completely redefines the geometric substrate of dynamic model ensembling. HyperMerge projects intermediate activations into the Poincaré Ball ($\mathbb{D}_c^D$), where constant negative curvature provides exponential volume growth that naturally accommodates hierarchical internal feature abstractions and power-law distribution spreads inside deep neural networks. By computing robust task-reference centroids using \emph{Hyperbolic Centroid Alignment} (HCA) via Klein-space Einstein midpoints, and performing non-linear, permutation-invariant ensembling via \emph{Beltrami-Klein Symmetric Blending} (BKSB), HyperMerge isolates conflicting task trajectories and segregates representation cross-talk. 
Evaluated on a 14-layer Analytical Coordinate Sandbox, HyperMerge achieves a joint mean accuracy of \textbf{83.40\% $\pm$ 5.15\%} under both homogeneous and fully heterogeneous streams, exhibiting perfect robustness to stream ordering (0.00\% heterogeneity collapse) and operating safely within the logical expert ceiling of \textbf{99.98\% $\pm$ 0.02\%}. This is a significant improvement over static parameter merging (74.98\% $\pm$ 2.17\%) and PFSR (80.97\% $\pm$ 1.75\% under heterogeneous streams), while performing on par with the top state-of-the-art Euclidean ensembling baselines (SPS-ZCA at 83.05\% $\pm$ 4.95\% and SABLE at 84.03\% $\pm$ 5.15\%) but with a mathematically consistent, Lorentz-weighted, and order-independent geometric formulation. This work demonstrates that the future of modular deep learning lies beyond the flatlands of Euclidean coordinates.
\end{abstract}
