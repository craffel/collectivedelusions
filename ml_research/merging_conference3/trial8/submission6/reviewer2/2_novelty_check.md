# Evaluation Phase 2: Novelty Check and Delta from Prior Work

## Key Novel Aspects
The paper introduces several highly novel conceptual and mathematical elements to the domain of Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) expert ensembling:
1. **Representational Subspace View of LoRA:** Instead of treating LoRA weights as arbitrary static parameters, LSPR views the down-projection weight matrix $A_k$ as defining a geometric subspace ($\mathcal{S}_k = \text{col}(A_k)$) within the high-dimensional activation space of the foundation model.
2. **Co-Designed Weight-Activation Alignment:** While prior works treat weight optimization and routing as separate problems, LSPR introduces a co-designed joint optimization framework. During training, the addition of a lightweight autoencoding reconstruction loss ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{reconstruction}}$) mathematically guides the columns of $A_k$ to align with the primary directions of the task's activation distribution.
3. **Closed-Form QR Projection:** LSPR bypasses parametric gating networks, offline centroids, and classification heads. Instead, it extracts an orthonormal basis $Q_k$ via offline QR decomposition and projects early-layer activations $h_b$ onto these bases to derive routing coefficients.
4. **Geometrical Scale-Invariance:** The similarity score $u_{k, b} = \| h_b Q_k \|_2 / \| h_b \|_2$ is shown to be mathematically equivalent to the cosine of the angle between the activation vector $h_b$ and the $r$-dimensional subspace spanned by $Q_k$. This geometric bound ($[0,1]$) provides an elegant, scale-invariant alternative to empirical calibration parameters.

## Delta from Prior Work
1. **From Training Gating Networks to Closed-Form Projection:** Prior frameworks (e.g., AdaMerging, SPS) require training additional routing parameters or multi-layer statistics. LSPR requires zero trainable routing parameters, resolving routing via a microsecond-level QR decomposition and a single parallel forward pass.
2. **Elimination of Offline Calibration and Parametric Density Estimators:** SOTA training-free routers like SPS-ZCA rely on high-dimensional centroids pre-computed from offline calibration splits, Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC), and expectation-maximization (EM) fitted GMMs for out-of-distribution (OOD) rejection. LSPR achieves both routing and OOD rejection simultaneously with a single geometric score and a lightweight task-agnostic OOD threshold calibration, completely eliminating the need for task-specific calibration datasets or parametric density models.
3. **From Classification-Head Cosine Similarity to Activation-Space Projection:** Methods like SABLE and PFSR route activations based on their cosine similarity to frozen classification-head weights. These "head-dependent" approaches cannot operate in head-free environments (e.g., autoregressive decoders, embedding generators) and suffer from the *Early-Layer Routing Paradox* (requiring all task adapters to execute through the entire depth before routing). LSPR routes at early layers (Layer 3) using activation-space projections, completely decoupling routing from classification heads and executing early layers task-agnostically.
4. **Post-Hoc Warm Alignment:** Unlike standard routing models that fail on public unaligned adapters, LSPR proposes a localized warm-alignment step (freezing $B_k$, tuning $A_k$ for 50-100 steps on 64 representative queries) to rotate the down-projection columns into the activation subspace without degrading downstream capabilities.

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this work is **significant and highly refreshing**. 
- Rather than adding more engineering and algorithmic components (the typical trend in recent model-merging literature), this work utilizes foundational concepts from linear algebra (QR decomposition, orthogonal projections, random projection theory on spheres) to solve a complex deep learning challenge.
- The formulation of the joint reconstruction objective is mathematically elegant and establishes a direct link between model adaptation and representational geometry.
- The "Adapter Sensitivity Theorem" provides a solid theoretical foundation, showing that the magnitude of the adapter update $\Delta y_b$ is upper-bounded by the activation's projection energy onto the column space of $A_k$, mathematically explaining why weight-activation alignment is so effective.
- However, the requirement of co-designed training (or post-hoc warm-alignment) is a key constraint. While previous papers proposed "plug-and-play" routers for completely unaligned off-the-shelf LoRAs, LSPR argues that such plug-and-play claims are mathematically flawed because standard LoRA training leaves the column space of $A_k$ random and unaligned. This realization is itself a novel contribution, exposing a hidden assumption and soundness issue in prior literature.
