# 2. Novelty Check

## Key Novel Aspects of the Paper
1. **Co-designed Joint Training and Routing:** Unlike post-hoc ensembling methods that treat the weights of pre-trained LoRA adapters as fixed and try to build complex routing mechanisms around them, LSPR proposes a co-designed paradigm. It introduces an autoencoding reconstruction objective ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{reconstruction}}$) during the training phase, forcing the down-projection matrix $A_k$ to align directly with the principal components of the task's activation subspace.
2. **Subspace Projection Routing (Offline QR & Online Projection):** The method performs a closed-form QR decomposition of the first-block down-projection matrix ($A_k = Q_k R_k$) to extract an orthonormal basis $Q_k$. At test time, routing is resolved by calculating the projection energy score ($u_{k, b} = \|h_b Q_k\|_2 / \|h_b\|_2$), which is mathematically equivalent to the cosine of the angle between the activation vector and the task subspace.
3. **Layer-Wise Freezing of Routing Coefficients:** To preserve downstream task adaptation capacity and reduce training overhead, LSPR applies the reconstruction loss only to the first adapter layer (Block 4), computes ensembling coefficients at that layer (using activations from Block 3), and freezes/re-uses them to blend activations across all subsequent blocks (Blocks 5 to 12).
4. **Post-Hoc Warm Alignment:** To maintain compatibility with unaligned, off-the-shelf public LoRA adapters, LSPR introduces a localized fine-tuning step. By freezing $B_k$, the classification head, and other blocks, and fine-tuning only $A_k$ at the routing layer for 50--100 steps on the reconstruction loss, it successfully aligns the column space without degrading downstream capabilities.
5. **Sparse-LSPR (Top-$M$ Gating):** To decouple computational complexity from the expert registry size $K$, Sparse-LSPR routes each token to only the top $M \ll K$ active expert subspaces, reducing adapter computation from $\mathcal{O}(B \cdot K \cdot r \cdot D)$ to $\mathcal{O}(B \cdot M \cdot r \cdot D)$.

## Delta from Prior Work
- **From SPS-ZCA (SOTA):** SPS-ZCA is highly data-dependent, requiring a 64-sample offline calibration split per task to compute centroid statistics, followed by multi-dimensional EM-fitted GMM density models for OOD detection, Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC), and Intra-Task Dispersion Calibration (IDC). LSPR replaces this entire pipeline with a single QR decomposition and normalized projection score, requiring zero parameters, zero calibration data, and zero EM fitting at deployment.
- **From SABLE / PFSR:** These methods are "head-dependent" (relying on classification-head similarities) and route at the very end of the network. This creates the "Early-Layer Routing Paradox," forcing task-specific adapters to run in parallel throughout the entire depth. LSPR is head-free and routes at an early layer (Layer 3), allowing early layers of the backbone to execute task-agnostically with no adapters, preserving capacity.
- **From Static Merging (TIES, Task Arithmetic):** Statically merged models suffer from capacity dilution and cannot adapt to mixed, heterogeneous query streams. LSPR is a dynamic ensembling method that performs sample-specific ensembling on-the-fly, completely bypassing batch-level averaging and avoiding "heterogeneity collapse."

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of LSPR can be characterized as **moderate-to-significant**. 
While the individual mathematical components (QR decomposition, orthogonal projection, autoencoding reconstruction loss) are classical and well-established, their synergistic co-designed integration into a unified PEFT training-and-serving framework represents a creative and refreshing departure from the increasingly complex, over-engineered gating networks and post-hoc calibration pipelines in current literature. 

The introduction of **Layer-Wise Freezing**, **Post-Hoc Warm Alignment**, and **Sparse-LSPR Top-$M$ Gating** are highly practical contributions that directly address the core limitations of LSPR (training-time cost, public adapter compatibility, and registry scalability).

However, the empirical novelty is currently bounded by the **purely synthetic nature** of the validation. Because the framework is only evaluated in a low-dimensional, simulated "Isolating Coordinate Sandbox," its real-world viability and novelty on commercial-scale models and standard benchmarks remain a speculative, albeit theoretically rigorous, contribution.
