# 2. Novelty Check

## Key Novel Aspects and the 'Delta' from Prior Work
The core contribution of this work lies in the **pragmatic convergence of post-training quantization, dynamic routing, and model merging** specifically designed for low-power edge microcontrollers. The specific "delta" from existing methods includes:

1. **SPS-ZCA / SABLE to Q-ZCA:** Prior single-pass routing and activation blending methods (SPS-ZCA, SABLE) assume high-precision (FP16 or FP32) representations and computation. SA-QAB takes these concepts and ports them entirely to the integer manifold (INT8/INT4), introducing a highly hardware-efficient cosine similarity computation that avoids costly on-device divisions and square roots via offline pre-normalization and fast fixed-point bit-shifting.
2. **Post-Merge Quantization (PMQ) & Q-Merge vs. DHQ:** Weight-space merging followed by quantization (PMQ) or optimization-based weight merging under quantization (Q-Merge) suffer from representational collapse under non-linearities and are locked into static configurations. SA-QAB proposes a decoupled architecture (DHQ) that keeps the base model at INT4 and the adapters at INT8. This represents a significant shift from static, single-precision parameters to a dynamic serving paradigm where weights remain separate and activations are blended dynamically.
3. **Quantization Scale Recovery (QSR):** Although scale alignment has been explored (e.g., Chaji et al., 2023), SA-QAB applies QSR specifically to neutralize the scale drift between INT4 base features and INT8 adapter features. This is a simple, training-free calibration step requiring only a tiny 64-sample dataset, making it extremely practical.
4. **Diagonal GMM Rejection Gate:** While Gaussian Mixture Models are standard for density estimation, deploying a pre-whitened diagonal GMM directly at the Layer 3 routing manifold with $O(D)$ storage and linear-time execution is an incredibly sensible systems contribution to avoid wasting active compute or introducing routing noise on edge microcontrollers.

## Characterization of Novelty
From a pure machine learning theory standpoint, the components (mixed-precision quantization, routing via cosine similarity, GMM-based outlier detection, scale recovery ratios) are established concepts. 

However, from an **engineering and practical deployment perspective, the novelty is significant**. It represents a highly creative and well-engineered synthesis of existing techniques tailored specifically to physical microcontrollers (e.g., STM32H753XI with $<1$MB SRAM and 2MB Flash). Instead of trying to run expensive backpropagation or backprop-based quantization (which requires training GPUs and is non-portable), SA-QAB achieves stable multi-task routing in an integer-only forward pass. The hardware-specific optimizations (such as CMSIS-NN compatibility, sparse routing with hard maxima, and flash capacity scaling analysis) are exactly what practitioners need to bridge the gap between high-level paper concepts and real-world deployment.
