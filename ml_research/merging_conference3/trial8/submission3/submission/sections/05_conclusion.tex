\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced \textbf{Scale-Aligned Quantized Activation Blending (SA-QAB)}, a forward-pass-only framework for efficient multi-task model fusion under edge constraints. By decoupling base model quantization (INT4) from adapters (INT8), SA-QAB avoids weight-space interference and batch-size collapse. Coordinate Sandbox evaluations show SA-QAB achieves robust \textbf{77.50\% joint accuracy} (\textbf{+58.90\% absolute gain} over static PMQ) while maintaining dynamic modularity for on-the-fly task loading.

\textbf{Future Work and Limitations:} While using synthetic and ViT proxy settings, key limitations and future directions include: (1) lack of physical on-board profiling, which we will address via CMSIS-NN and TensorFlow Lite Micro compiling on an STM32H753XI board to measure actual silicon latencies and power; (2) reliance on vision benchmarks, prompting future expansion to other edge modalities like audio spectrogram transformers (AST) and IoT time-series; and (3) dependence on lightweight QAT for peak performance, motivating advanced training-free PTQ scaling (e.g., SmoothQuant and AWQ outlier-aware scaling) to further bridge the accuracy gap without any training loops. Future work will also adapt SA-QAB to LLM KV-cache routing.
