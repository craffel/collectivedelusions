# Intermediate Evaluation: 1_summary.md

## Main Topic and Approach
The paper introduces **Q-SPS** (Quantized Single-Pass Activation-Space Dynamic Blending) and its execution-gated variant **CG-Q-SPS** (Conditional Gated Q-SPS). This framework is designed for resource-constrained edge-device serving of multiple specialized expert LoRA adapters. The core approach is to quantize the task-specific expert adapters to low-bitwidth symmetric integers (INT8/INT4) and execute the low-rank additions in pure integer precision within a single parallel forward pass ($O(1)$ backbone latency). 

To resolve the execution contradiction of parallel ensembling (where executing all expert pathways is computationally wasteful under near-one-hot routing), **CG-Q-SPS** applies a conditional gating threshold ($\theta = 0.01$) to completely bypass the execution of inactive expert adapters on a per-sample basis.

Additionally, the framework incorporates:
1. **Quantization-Aware Scale Calibration (QASC):** A training-free post-hoc calibration protocol that decouples the scale optimization of down-projection and up-projection matrices sequentially, minimizing Mean Squared Error (MSE) to counteract discretization noise.
2. **Zero-Shot Centroid Alignment (ZCA) Routing with Intra-Task Dispersion Calibration (IDC):** An early-stage (Layer 3) task-agnostic router that computes normalized cosine similarities against pre-computed centroids to derive confident routing coefficients.
3. **Coordinate Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) Safety Shield:** A diagonal GMM fit over the similarity coordinate space to detect and reject out-of-distribution (OOD) queries at Layer 3, reverting execution strictly to the base model.
4. **Systems optimizations for edge CPUs:** Such as **Local Batch Re-Ordering** (sorting heterogeneous batches by predicted active expert paths to maximize cache locality) and **Temporal-Aware Routing Hysteresis** (an EWMA coordinate filter for sequential $B=1$ serving to eliminate routing flicker and cache thrashing).

## Key Findings and Claimed Contributions

1. **Massive Expert Memory Footprint Savings:** Quantizing the expert LoRA adapters to 4-bit precision slashes their combined memory footprint by **87.5%** (from 2.76 MB down to 0.345 MB), allowing dozens of specialized experts to fit natively inside highly restricted on-chip SRAM or L1/L2 caches.
2. **High-Throughput, Lossless Single-Pass Serving:** By executing the parallel low-rank expert paths in native integer precision within a single forward pass, CG-Q-SPS projects a **3.97$\times$ physical speedup** (189.1 ms vs 749.8 ms cumulative latency over 1,024 heterogeneous samples) over state-of-the-art sequential micro-batching pipelines (PFSR + MBH).
3. **Immunization against Gating and Quantization Collapse:** While standard static weight merging with 4-bit quantization (Quantized Uniform Merging INT4) collapses to near-random joint mean accuracy (30.70%), CG-Q-SPS (INT4 + QASC) maintains an outstanding **79.40% Joint Mean Accuracy**, recovering **99.5%** of the unquantized floating-point ceiling (79.80%).
4. **Robust GMM-Based OOD Rejection:** Compressing early-stage Layer 3 features into a low-dimensional similarity coordinate space enables a diagonal Coordinate GMM to achieve an outstanding **AUC of 0.98**, delivering a **95.2% True Positive Rate (TPR)** at only **4.3% False Positive Rate (FPR)**, significantly outperforming raw feature-space baselines and cosine similarity thresholds.
5. **Ablation of Centroid Orthogonalization Redundancy:** The paper mathematically analyzes and evaluates Gram-Schmidt Cross-Centroid Orthogonalization (GS-CCO) and L&ouml;wdin Symmetric Manifold De-Entangling (SMD) as theoretical de-entangling methods. It provides an intellectually honest finding that explicit orthogonalization is actually mathematically redundant and even detrimental due to noise spillover across joint projection spaces. The raw, unorthogonalized ZCA-IDC baseline remains the most robust overall (Routing Accuracy of **94.70%** and Flicker Rate of **10.34%** under severe entanglement $\epsilon = 0.8$).
6. **Empirical Validation and Scalability:**
   - **Pre-trained ViT-Tiny weights:** On a real pre-trained ViT, QASC Dynamic Scaling and its Static Scaling Alternative (which pre-calculates scales offline to allow branchless execution) both dramatically reduce relative reconstruction MSE to **2.80%** (slashing uncalibrated RTN's 6.68% MSE by over half) and achieve a logit Cosine Similarity of **0.9940** and Top-1 class agreement of **84.38%** in a fully-quantized, end-to-end 12-block simulation.
   - **Pragmatic Scaling to LLM-Scale Weights with Outliers:** Under extreme activation outliers (outlier factor of 40.0, representing "attention sinks" in LLMs), the QASC Static/Dynamic Scaling protocols on a $3072 \times 3072$ linear projection layer ($r=16$) reduce relative reconstruction MSE from **13.30%** (RTN) to **9.04%**, proving that the calibration co-design scales successfully to modern billion-parameter LLM configurations.
   - **Projected Energy Savings:** By combining 4-bit expert parameter quantization with sparse conditional gating, CG-Q-SPS (INT4) slashes energy consumption by **56.2%** over sequential micro-batching (0.46 J vs. 0.90 J per batch) and **55.2%** over standard FP32 ensembling (1.05 J per batch).
   - **Adaptive Battery-Aware Gating:** A dynamic thresholding mechanism ($\theta(E_{\text{batt}})$) is proposed to scale the gating threshold upwards to aggressively prune expert pathways as battery levels deplete, allowing edge devices to trade off negligible accuracy for significant dynamic energy savings under low-power regimes.
