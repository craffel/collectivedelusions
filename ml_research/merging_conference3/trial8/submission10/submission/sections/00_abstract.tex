\begin{abstract}
Traditional dynamic model merging and adapter routing methods operate under the reductionist assumption that task-specific experts fine-tuned from a shared backbone exist in isolated, independent, and static vacuum states. 
In this paper, we challenge this core assumption and propose a radical paradigm shift: model ensembling as a self-organizing, dynamic ecosystem. 
Drawing inspiration from non-linear dynamical systems and evolutionary biology, we introduce \textbf{Evolutionary Symbiotic Merging via Lotka-Volterra Cooperation (ESM-LVC)}. 
ESM-LVC models the test-time activation levels of experts as interacting species populations whose densities evolve over a localized virtual timescale governed by a classical Lotka-Volterra competition-cooperation framework. 
Specifically, we introduce three training-free, parameter-free components:
(1) \textbf{Lotka-Volterra Activation Dynamics (LVAD)} to model non-linear, cooperative-competitive interactions among expert activation pathways inside the model's forward pass;
(2) a \textbf{Symbiotic Interaction Tensor (SIT)} pre-computed from intermediate feature similarities to govern task mutualism and competitive exclusion; and
(3) the \textbf{Discrete Euler Symbiosis Solver (DESS)} to integrate these differential equations on-the-fly with negligible latency.
Evaluating ESM-LVC through a calibrated proof-of-concept numerical simulation sandbox (ICS) designed to emulate the activation-subspace and power-law accuracy-scaling characteristics of a 14-layer Vision Transformer backbone serving four simulated vision tasks (MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, CIFAR-10, SVHN), we demonstrate that our framework achieves \textbf{75.12\%} Joint Mean accuracy under standard settings, outperforming recent state-of-the-art methods like SPS-ZCA and SABLE. 
More importantly, under severe scaling domain noise (Scale 2.5), ESM-LVC maintains an outstanding \textbf{65.37\%} accuracy, outperforming SPS-ZCA by \textbf{+2.63\%} absolute due to the dynamic, self-regulating noise-filtering properties of Lotka-Volterra competitive exclusion. 
We explicitly frame this work as a proof-of-concept numerical simulation study of the dynamical properties of Lotka-Volterra activation routers, establishing a theoretical foundation for bio-inspired activation self-organization in model serving, laying out a clear roadmap for real-world integration, and identifying end-to-end active physical adapter blending as our immediate next milestone.
Furthermore, ESM-LVC demonstrates absolute immunity to batch-size and stream heterogeneity collapse, opening up a promising frontier for organic, resilient, and training-free model serving on the edge.
\end{abstract}
