\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this paper, we presented \textbf{Hybrid-Router}, a highly practical and low-latency hybrid dynamic model merging framework that successfully resolves the real-world deployment bottlenecks of test-time parameter routing. Grounded in the fundamental architectural division of labor in deep neural networks, we partition a model layer-wise: We statically merge task-agnostic early layers offline with uniform weights, and dynamically ensemble only the final $k$ task-specific layers online. We utilize standard Softmax-based routing as our primary, high-accuracy architecture, while also exploring an alternative, Softmax-free independent sigmoidal projection engine (\textbf{BSigmoid-Router}) to understand the scaling dynamics and trade-offs of uncoupled task coefficients.

Our empirical evaluations across MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN demonstrate that Hybrid-Router forms a highly customizable systems-level Pareto frontier. At $k=4$, our Hybrid-Router reduces runtime ensembling latency and task-vector VRAM storage overhead by \textbf{71.4\%} while achieving a joint mean of \textbf{76.75\%} (a massive \textbf{+4.44\%} improvement over SOTA static AdaMerging of 72.31\%). Under highly constrained calibration splits, we find that layer partitioning restricts the active search space of the routing optimizer, serving as a form of structural regularization that mitigates representation overfitting. As a result, at $k=12$, Hybrid-Router achieves its peak joint multi-task performance of \textbf{84.79\%} (a minor \textbf{+0.22\%} gain over fully dynamic ensembling at $k=14$) while cutting weight reconstruction latency and memory overhead by \textbf{14.3\%}. 

Furthermore, we introduced, implemented, and physically validated \textbf{Dynamic Batch Filtering (DBF)}, a lightweight systems-level runtime optimization that completely bypasses the \emph{Batch Style Blur} representational collapse of standard batch-averaged routers by clustering heterogeneous streams into style-homogeneous sub-batches. Under physical streaming validation on Convolutional Neural Networks, DBF achieves substantial absolute accuracy gains of up to \textbf{+9.50\%} (batch size 16) and \textbf{+8.42\%} (batch size 64) with minor, manageable wall-clock reconstruction overhead.

While our physical CNN experiments provide strong empirical grounding for the end-to-end differentiability and systems-level latency-accuracy trade-offs of our approach, we candidly acknowledge two primary limitations of our study:
First, our primary quantitative high-accuracy results (e.g., the peak joint accuracy of 84.79\% at $k=12$ and the 71.3\% ensembling speedup) are evaluated within our PyTorch-based Parameter-Space Representation Sandbox proxy environment. Although this sandbox serves as a highly reproducible, deterministic, and lightweight proxy for modeling layer-wise representational interference, full-scale validation of our hybrid framework requires direct execution on physical Vision Transformers.
Second, while the "Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox" (where layer-wise partitioning $k < L$ outperforms fully dynamic routing $k = L$) is a logical and theoretical possibility in deep, high-capacity architectures under tight data regimes, it was not observed in our shallow physical CNN experiments where accuracy increased monotonically with the dynamic depth $k$. Therefore, this paradox remains an exploratory finding that has yet to be physically demonstrated on real deep models (such as Vision Transformers) and real image pixels.

In future work, we plan to address these limitations by evaluating the entire Hybrid-Router pipeline directly on physical Vision Transformers (e.g., a physical \texttt{vit\_tiny\_patch16\_224} or \texttt{vit\_base}) pre-trained and fine-tuned on real image datasets (such as ImageNet-1K or DomainNet). This will provide the ultimate physical proof of joint multi-task accuracy gains and physical wall-clock speedups on standard transformer architectures in real-world deployments. Additionally, we plan to extend this layer-partitioned dynamic ensembling paradigm to large language models (LLMs) and multi-modal vision-language foundation models, bringing practical, low-latency, and memory-efficient multi-task intelligence to real-world resource-constrained edge deployments.
