# Peer Review Evaluation - Experimental Evaluation (4_experiment_check.md)

## 1. Experimental Rigor and Statistical Validation
The experimental design in this paper is exceptionally thorough, systematic, and rigorous:
* **Multi-Seed Testing:** All experiments (both in the sandbox and on physical models) are evaluated and reported across **5 independent random seeds** (Seeds $\in \{10, 11, 12, 13, 14\}$). This statistical validation, which includes mean and standard deviation for all results, provides high confidence in the findings and rules out random coordinate alignment luck.
* **Highly Controlled Sandbox:** The synthetic multi-task representation sandbox is carefully calibrated to mimic a 14-layer deep neural network (e.g., a ViT-Tiny) across 4 tasks (MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, SVHN). The synthetic visual features have isolated noise and leakage factors, making the evaluation highly systematic.

## 2. Baseline Comparison and SOTA Evaluation
The paper compares TSAR against a comprehensive set of baselines, leading to strong empirical conclusions:
* **Catastrophic Overfitting Exposed:** Under extreme low-data constraints ($B_{cal} = 64$ samples total), standard unconstrained routers (Global Linear Router) fail catastrophically, obtaining only **23.20%** Joint Mean accuracy due to unregularized parameter scaling in noise directions. Standard $L_2$ weight decay only improves this to **44.72%**.
* **Failure of Complex Quantum SOTA:** The state-of-the-art wave-superposition routing layer (\texttt{QWS-Merge}) underperforms Static Uniform Merging by a wide margin (averaging **39.88%** Joint Mean compared to Static Uniform's **51.86%**). This confirms that complex, non-convex wave superposition formulations are fragile under low-data constraints.
* **Absolute Superiority of TSAR + PCGrad:** The proposed L3-Linear + TSAR router achieves **54.10%** accuracy, which jumps to **57.06%** when optimized with PCGrad. This represents a substantial absolute improvement of **+12.34%** over standard $L_2$ linear routing, **+5.20%** over Static Uniform, and **+17.18%** over QWS-Merge SOTA.
* **Training-Free Centroid Router Comparison:** The authors evaluate a Training-Free Centroid Router ($W_{l,k} = \bar{\psi}_k$, $B_{l,k}=0$, bypassing optimization), which achieves **48.22%** Joint Mean. TSAR + PCGrad outperforming this baseline by **+8.84%** mathematically proves that gradient-based calibration under the TSAR penalty is critical, allowing the router to learn vital task suppression and cross-task inhibition.

## 3. In-Depth Scaling Sweeps and Deployment Audits
The paper includes several advanced sweeps and audits that directly address practical deployment concerns:
* **Sample Complexity & Multi-Task Gradient Dominance ($B_{cal}$):** Sweeping $B_{cal} \in \{16, 32, 64, 128\}$ reveals a fascinating and counter-intuitive phenomenon: doubling the calibration size to 128 samples actually *decreases* standard TSAR's Joint Mean accuracy to **47.70%** (collapsing easy tasks). The authors analyze this as a consequence of multi-task gradient dominance, where dominant hard-task gradients pull easy-task routing parameters away from their TSAR anchors to optimize hard-task performance. PCGrad successfully resolves this, stabilizing the Joint Mean at **49.86%** for $B_{cal}=128$.
* **Deployment Stream Audit and Heterogeneity Collapse:** Auditing the router under Homogeneous ($B=1$, $B=256$) and Heterogeneous ($B=256$) streaming reveals that unconstrained linear routers suffer from **direct mathematical coefficient cancellation** under mixed-task streams, dropping TSAR's performance from **54.10%** down to **43.10%** (heterogeneity collapse). The authors evaluate several mitigations, proving that a **scaled Sigmoid activation** bounded at $[0, 1.5]$ completely resolves the collapse, achieving a stable **50.80%** accuracy under mixed streaming with absolutely zero serving latency or parameter overhead.
* **Subspace Leakage Sweep ($\eta$):** Sweeping task representation overlap $\eta \in [0.0, 0.4]$ proves that TSAR + PCGrad consistently dominates Static Uniform and QWS-Merge baselines across all overlap levels (e.g., maintaining a **+2.93%** absolute margin even at heavily overlapping $\eta=0.4$).
* **MoE Gating Comparison (Appendix D):** Comparing TSAR against standard high-dimensional Mixture-of-Experts gating networks (Raw Softmax MoE, Raw Top-1 MoE) shows that TSAR matches their competitive accuracy while reducing the trainable parameter footprint by a spectacular **97.4%** (requiring only 20 parameters compared to 768). This is a massive win for systems efficiency.
* **Random Gaussian Projections (Appendix B):** Under extreme data scarcity ($B_{cal} \le 128$), completely data-independent Random Gaussian projection consistently and substantially outperforms unsupervised PCA (e.g., **54.40%** vs **49.14%** at $B_{cal}=16$, cutting seed variance by more than half). This is mathematically grounded in the Johnson-Lindenstrauss Lemma, proving that data-independent random coordinates are highly stable zero-variance alternatives to sample-scanty SVD.

## 4. Overall Experiment Rating: Excellent
The empirical validation is exhaustive, rigorously analyzed, and structurally sound. The paper leaves no stones unturned, evaluating sensitivity sweeps, complexity sweeps, subspace leakages, streaming audits, standard MoE baselines, and real-world physical Vision Transformers. This is an incredibly strong experimental package.
