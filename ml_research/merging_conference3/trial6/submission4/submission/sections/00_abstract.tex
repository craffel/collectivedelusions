\begin{abstract}
Model merging has emerged as a powerful, computationally efficient paradigm for multi-task
learning, enabling parameter-level fusion of specialized expert networks without expensive
retraining. 
While early methods focused on static arithmetic weighting, recent state-of-the-art techniques have
introduced dynamic, sample-specific input routing to adaptively scale task-merging coefficients on
the fly. 
In this work, we expose a critical and previously undocumented vulnerability of these dynamic
routing mechanisms: under extreme calibration data scarcity (e.g., $64$ samples), unconstrained
layer-wise router parameters suffer from severe overfitting, leading to catastrophic
representation-space collapse and a failure to generalize to out-of-distribution (OOD) tasks. 
To resolve this, we present \textbf{Task-Space Anchor Regularization (TSAR)}, a simple,
geometrically grounded classical regularizer that anchors layer-wise routing weights to the
pre-computed centroids (anchors) of pre-trained expert representations in a low-dimensional
projection space. 
We validate our approach through a highly controlled, multi-seed empirical analysis on a 14-layer
representation-space sandbox. 
Our exhaustive experimental results demonstrate that introducing TSAR
($\lambda_{\text{anchor}}=0.1$) optimized with Projecting Conflicting Gradients (PCGrad) achieves a
joint multi-task mean accuracy of \textbf{57.06\%}, outperforming standard $L_2$-regularized linear
routing by \textbf{+12.34\%}, Static Uniform Merging by \textbf{+5.20\%}, and the highly complex,
wave-superposition state-of-the-art (SOTA) (QWS-Merge) by \textbf{+17.18\%} absolute margin. 
Furthermore, we conduct sample complexity and stream audits, analyzing the "heterogeneity collapse"
phenomenon under mixed-task deployment streams, and showing that our proposed Sigmoid-activated TSAR
router achieves a stable \textbf{50.80\%} under heterogeneous streaming, bypassing coefficient
cancellation with zero runtime overhead.
\end{abstract}
