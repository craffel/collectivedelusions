\begin{abstract}
  Model merging has emerged as a powerful paradigm for combining task-specific expert networks without incurring additional retraining costs. However, existing dynamic routing protocols are plagued by severe overfitting (transductive overfitting) on small calibration streams and catastrophic performance degradation (heterogeneity collapse) under realistic batch-averaged edge deployment. To address these vulnerabilities, we propose Rademacher-Regularized Dynamic Model Merging (R2D-Merge), a learning-theoretic framework that establishes formal generalization guarantees for input-dependent model routing. By bounding the empirical Rademacher complexity of the dynamic parameter-space blending function class, we derive Covariance-weighted Frobenius Regularization (CFR)---a task-adaptive quadratic penalty that is pre-computed offline and incurs zero online computational overhead. Empirically, on a Vision Transformer (ViT-Tiny) backbone across four distinct vision tasks, R2D-Merge achieves comparable multi-task accuracy while demonstrating absolute resilience under batch-averaged heterogeneous streams (0.00\% drop), outperforming unregularized baselines by up to 11.50\% and quantum-inspired routers by 5.50\% in accuracy.
\end{abstract}
