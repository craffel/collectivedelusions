# 3. Soundness and Methodology Check

## Technical Soundness
The technical claims, experimental design, and mathematical modeling of this paper are exceptionally sound and rigorous:

*   **Rigorous Mathematical Formulation:** The authors derive a generalized model of Expected Ruggedness $E[R(\alpha_k)]$ that incorporates depth-dependent variance scales ($\sigma_g^2$) and adjacent layer correlation ($\rho_g$). This directly proves how block-wise sharing restricts the degrees of freedom to clip adjacent weight-blending fluctuations.
*   **Highly Tractable and Realistic Sandbox Design:** Unlike typical toy setups where tasks occupy completely orthogonal feature subspaces (which artificially decouples the expert models and makes uniform merging trivial), this sandbox explicitly models severe parameter and representation conflicts. 
    1.  *Shared Semantic Subspace ($D_{shared}=128$):* Contains 10 class prototypes shared across all $K=4$ tasks with shifted label mappings (e.g., prototype $P_c$ mapped to target $(c + k) \pmod{10}$ for task $k$). This creates direct, severe weight-space semantic conflicts.
    2.  *Task-Specific Style Subspace ($D_{style}=64$):* Divided into $K$ independent 16-dimensional blocks providing task identity cues.
*   **Empirical Calibration Ceilings:** To emulate realistic downstream conditions, the authors calibrate input feature noise to match realistic expert classification ceilings (MNIST at 100%, FashionMNIST at 99.44 $\pm$ 0.14%, CIFAR-10 at 97.30 $\pm$ 0.45%, and SVHN at 30.16 $\pm$ 0.89%).
*   **The Transition to Physical Sequential Weight Blending:** The authors do not restrict their study to a virtual-layer sandbox proxy. They formulate a physical sequential weight-space model-merging setup on 3-layer MLP experts. In this setup, representation propagation is sequential; there is no virtual averaging. Weights and biases are physically blended in PyTorch at runtime, and activations propagate through these blended parameters sequentially. This provides a complete and realistic validation environment.

## Intellectual Honesty and Self-Critique
The paper is highly distinguished by its scientific honesty, transparency, and self-critique:

1.  *Sandbox Methodological Artifacts:* The authors explicitly clarify that "layer-averaging collapse" (where independent routers overfit and regress to high-variance coefficients whose average collapses to a uniform distribution) is a virtual-layer sandbox artifact. In physical deep networks, because coefficients are never literally averaged, this literal averaging collapse does not occur. They justify the sandbox as a tractable high-throughput proxy to model optimization ruggedness and overparameterization variance.
2.  *Softmax Empirical Superiority vs. Narrative:* The authors openly acknowledge that under the optimal learning rate of $\eta = 5 \cdot 10^{-2}$, Softmax gating outperforms Sigmoidal gating (80.56% vs 77.59% in the activation sweep), which at first glance seems to contradict their anti-Softmax narrative. They meticulously explain this sandbox advantage (implicit sum-to-one regularization and logit-amplification) and then conduct an open-world audit (OOD random noise and multi-task feature mixing) to empirically demonstrate and validate why independent Sigmoidal routing is superior for physical open-world deployments.
3.  *Addressing High Seed-wise Variance in Physical Merging:* The authors discuss the relatively large standard deviation observed across seeds in physical sequential merging (e.g., $43.20 \pm 22.49\%$ for BWS $M=3$ Heterogeneous). They identify cascading representation drift as the root cause—where a sub-optimal early-layer gating coefficient perturbs the inputs to subsequent layers, causing compounding distortion. They actively evaluate and recommend two concrete stabilization strategies (sequential smoothing regularization and residual links) to manage this variance.

## Areas for Improvement / Minor Limitations
*   *Task-specific scaling ceiling formulation:* The paper proposes learning task-specific ceilings $\sigma_k$ initialized to $\lambda_{max} = 0.3$ and derives the gradient update rule for $\sigma_k$. While the mathematical formulation is elegant and complete, it is presented as a proposed future direction rather than being evaluated empirically in the main text. Including a small empirical pilot or sensitivity sweep for task-specific scaling ceilings would further strengthen the physical merging results, especially for noisy tasks like SVHN.
*   *Inclusion of MLP-expert head architectures in Sandbox:* While the MLP setup is evaluated in the physical sequential model-merging setup, the sandbox features are evaluated using single-layer linear classifiers. Although this is a standard and high-throughput setup, evaluating the sandbox with deeper non-linear classifiers would help isolate whether routing difficulties under Sigmoidal gating are due to weight-space blending limitations or the classifier head's capacity.
