# Paper Summary

## Main Topic and Objective
The paper addresses the challenge of merging multi-task neural network experts into a unified, high-performing model without incurring the retraining cost of static merging or the hyper-parameterization and instability of dynamic routing networks. Specifically, it targets two severe failure modes of modern dynamic weight-space routing:
1. **Optimization Bloat and Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Collapse:** Sophisticated routing schemes like Quantum Wavefunction Superposition Merging (QWS-Merge) rely on complex, heavily parameterized, wave-inspired architectures that suffer from transductive overfitting and collapse catastrophically when presented with OOD tasks.
2. **Heterogeneity Collapse in Mixed-Task Streams:** Under realistic deployment conditions where batches contain mixed-task streams, sample-wise coefficients are averaged over the batch dimension to comply with hardware constraints. This averaging forces routing coefficients toward a flat, uniform distribution, which destroys the task specificity and benefits of dynamic routing.

## Proposed Approach
To resolve these failure modes, the authors apply Occam's razor to model-merging streams, presenting a zero-shot, completely non-parametric framework that operates under the Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT/LoRA) paradigm to ensure VRAM viability:
* **Parameter-Free Subspace Routing (PFSR):** A non-parametric routing mechanism that projects high-dimensional intermediate features from the penultimate layer onto a frozen, low-dimensional task coordinate subspace. This projection is done by computing the maximum cosine similarity between the intermediate representation and the pre-trained expert classification weights (representing class prototypes). The routing coefficients are then derived directly via temperature-scaled normalization (Softmax), requiring zero trainable parameters, zero training epochs, and zero calibration data (assuming pre-trained classification heads are available).
* **Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC):** Applied to both intermediate features and expert classification heads prior to similarity projection, neutralizing scale discrepancies across independently fine-tuned experts.
* **Class-Size Scaling Calibration:** Addresses the statistical bias that over-routes samples toward experts with larger label or vocabulary spaces (e.g., LLM next-token heads vs. small classifier heads) by normalizing raw task coordinates by their expected random-chance maximum.
* **Sub-Vocabulary Prototype Selection:** A data-free, parameter-centric pruning heuristic that selects the top $C_{sub} = 256$ vocabulary tokens with the highest variance in classification weight magnitude across experts, bypassing the computational bottleneck of similarity projection on large vocabularies (like LLMs).
* **Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH):** A systems-level stream orchestration layer that dynamically partitions mixed-task input streams on the fly into task-homogeneous micro-batches based on their dominant task coordinates, performs specialized merged-model inference on each, and re-assembles the outputs back into the original batch ordering.

## Key Findings
1. **Deconstruction of QWS-Merge:** Wave-inspired routing formulations are highly unstable and suffer from severe transductive overfitting on tiny calibration splits, collapsing to $10.00\%$ accuracy on SVHN (OOD). Classical $L_2$ regularization on a simple linear router replicates or outperforms these complex designs.
2. **Layer-Averaging Collapse:** Analytical and empirical deconstruction proves that multi-layer dynamic routers are redundant under classification head constraints. The Jacobians of the shared backbone act as contractive mappings that project the representation space onto a shared dominant task subspace, forcing layer-wise routing trajectories to be perfectly collinear.
3. **Robustness of PFSR+MBH:** In the isolating sandbox, the proposed parameter-free method achieves a high **$75.00\%$ Joint Mean accuracy** under homogeneous streams and maintains **$71.60\%$ Joint Mean accuracy** under heterogeneous streams, completely resolving heterogeneity collapse.
4. **Viability and Scalability:** When validated on real-world Vision Transformers (DomainNet, achieving **$78.50\%$ Mean accuracy**) and LLaMA-7B experts (NLP tasks, achieving **$79.12\%$ Mean accuracy**), the framework recovers over $96\%$ of the expert standalone ceiling with a strict $1.04\times$ memory footprint and zero routing parameter overhead. Bounded Top-$k$ Routing and parallel SGMV execution eliminate scaling and sequential dispatch bottlenecks.

## Explicitly Claimed Contributions (with Evidence)
1. **Empirical Deconstruction of Over-parameterized Routers:** Demonstrated that classical $L_2$ regularization easily stabilizes linear routers and out-performs QWS-Merge (Table 5.1 & Figure 5.1).
2. **Mathematical and Empirical Analysis of Layer-Averaging Collapse:** Formulated a first-order Taylor expansion proving the collinearity of layer-wise optimization trajectories (Section 3.5 & Figure 5.1(b)).
3. **Introduction of PFSR + MBH:** Proposed the joint framework (Algorithm 1) and showed that it achieves superior performance over unregularized and wave-inspired routers with zero trainable parameters (Table 5.1 & Table 5.2).
4. **Real-world Scale Verification:** Verified on DomainNet (Table 5.4) and LLaMA-7B NLP Experts (Table 5.5), achieving significant improvements over static and dynamic baselines under heterogeneous streams.
5. **PEFT Integration for Spatial Viability:** Grounded the serving footprint at a strict $1.04\times$ VRAM limit (Table 5.3) and measured sub-millisecond dynamic weight merging times (Section 4).
6. **Data-Free, Parameter-Centric Vocabulary Pruning Heuristic:** Introduced variance-based prototype selection to achieve a $132.2\times$ latency speedup for LLMs (Table 5.2).
