# Novelty Check

## Key Novel Aspects
1. **Completely Non-Parametric Gating for Model Merging**: Instead of training a parametric router (which is prone to overfitting and OOD collapse), the paper introduces **Parameter-Free Subspace Routing (PFSR)**. This method is completely zero-shot and has *zero* trainable parameters, identifying tasks purely through cosine similarity between high-dimensional features and the frozen pre-trained expert classification heads.
2. **Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH)**: Rather than trying to design complex, "robust" neural network architectures that are immune to heterogeneous streams, the paper elegantly shifts the burden of robustness to the data orchestration layer. It partitions heterogeneous input streams on the fly into homogeneous micro-batches, executes specialized model merging on each micro-batch sequentially or in parallel, and re-assembles the outputs.
3. **Rigorous Analysis and Mathematical Proof of Layer-Averaging Collapse**: The authors provide a first-order Taylor expansion proof demonstrating why layer-wise multi-layer routers are mathematically redundant when experts are fine-tuned from a shared initialization and evaluated under a single classification head. The proof shows that Jacobians act as contractive mappings, projecting representation spaces onto a shared dominant task subspace and causing all layer-specific routing gradients to be collinear.
4. **Statistical Class-Size Scaling Calibration**: Addresses extreme-value statistical bias (arising when computing raw maximum cosine similarity across highly asymmetrical vocabulary sizes, such as a 32,000-word LLM next-token head vs. a 10-class image head) with a training-free scaling factor derived from the expected maximum of random Gaussians.
5. **Data-Free, Parameter-Centric Vocabulary Selection**: Introduces a variance-based prototype selection heuristic to select informative vocabulary indices based purely on expert head parameters, avoiding any text data dependencies.

## Delta from Prior Work
- **Static Merging (Task Arithmetic, TIES-Merging)**: Static merging schemes construct a single compromise model that suffers from parameter sign-conflicts and spatial interference. PFSR+MBH dynamically adapts to incoming inputs, resolving spatial interference and recovering nearly $97\%$ of the expert standalone ceiling.
- **Parametric Dynamic Routers (QWS-Merge, L3-Softmax Router)**:
  - Prior dynamic routing networks require a test-time calibration split (e.g., 64 samples) and optimization (e.g., AdamW). This introduces transductive overfitting and catastrophic collapse on OOD tasks. PFSR requires zero training, zero parameters, and zero calibration split data in standard registries, maintaining perfect robustness.
  - Prior layer-wise dynamic routers suffer from a "Robustness-Accuracy Illusion" where Softmax constraints simply force routing coefficients to hover near a flat, uniform average under all conditions, sacrificing performance. PFSR+MBH achieves maximum task-specific performance while completely bypassing heterogeneity collapse.
  - The analytical proof of Layer-Averaging Collapse explicitly unmasks the redundancy of multi-layer routers (like L3-Softmax/L3-Linear) compared to a simple, global single-layer router, showing that complex layer-wise parameters are merely fitting transductive noise.

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this paper is **significant, refreshing, and highly valuable**. Rather than following the typical deep learning trend of proposing increasingly complex, over-engineered architectures (such as the wave-inspired quantum formulations of QWS-Merge), the authors apply Occam's razor to achieve outstanding performance. The paper's novelty lies not in making the neural network more complex, but in making it *simpler*—stripping away all routing parameters and solving stream heterogeneity through elegant data-stream engineering. 

Furthermore, the theoretical proof of Layer-Averaging Collapse provides a strong foundation that will influence future research, discouraging researchers from building redundant layer-wise routing schemes in model merging. The co-design of systems-level serving mechanisms (SGMV kernels, LoRA adapters) with parameter-free model-merging shows that the authors have deeply thought through the practical, hardware-level implications of their minimalist approach.
