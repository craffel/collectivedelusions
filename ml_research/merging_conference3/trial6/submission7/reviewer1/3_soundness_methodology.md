# Soundness and Methodology Evaluation

## Clarity of the Description
The methodology is exceptionally well-described, rigorous, and structured. 
- The mathematical formulations are clear and physically intuitive, flowing logically from raw cosine projection (Eq. 1), class-size scaling calibration (Eq. 2), temperature-scaled Softmax (Eq. 3), stream partitioning (Eq. 4-5), batch aggregation (Eq. 6), parameter merging (Eq. 7), and output re-assembly (Eq. 8).
- The inclusion of **Algorithm 1 (PFSR + MBH + UNC Model Merging Framework)** is a stellar addition, providing a step-by-step roadmap that details the entire pipeline—from representations extraction to unit-norm normalization, stream partitioning, tailored parameter merging, and index scatter-gather re-assembly. This level of transparency makes the framework highly reproducible and easy to integrate into modern serving frameworks.
- The theoretical deconstruction and first-order Taylor expansion proof of **Layer-Averaging Collapse** are outstanding, explaining exactly why multi-layer dynamic parameter routing is mathematically redundant and systematically outperformed by a single global router. The assumptions (contractive Jacobian mappings evaluated at shared initializations under PEFT) are explicitly laid out and analyzed with high scientific integrity.

## Appropriateness of Methods
- **Parameter-Free Subspace Routing (PFSR)**: Highly appropriate and elegant. Leveraging the semantic class prototypes already learned by the pre-trained expert heads to identify task-specific representation coordinates is a brilliant application of Occam's razor. It eliminates the need for expensive training phases, calibration data, and parameter-heavy routing layers.
- **Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH)**: Solving the parameter-level averaging bottleneck (heterogeneity collapse) at the data-stream level is highly logical and practical. It preserves the task specificity of the merged expert weights and guarantees optimal inference without architectural constraints.
- **Class-Size Scaling Calibration (Eq. 2)**: Standard extreme-value theory shows that the maximum of $C_k$ random variables grows with $\sqrt{2\log C_k / d}$. Normalizing raw similarities by this factor is statistically sound and crucial when merging highly asymmetrical experts (e.g., LLMs next-token heads vs. localized classification heads).
- **Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC, Eq. 15)**: Essential to neutralize local representation scale imbalances across independently trained experts.
- **Variance-Based Prototype Selection (Eq. 12)**: A brilliant, data-free heuristic to select informative classification head elements based purely on expert parameter variances, resolving vocabulary bottlenecks while avoiding private text dependencies.

## Potential Technical Flaws and Limitations
1. **Computational Latency and Sequential Dispatch of MBH on Edge CPUs**: Under highly heterogeneous streams, sequential dispatching of $G \le K$ active micro-batches runs sequentially, which scales latency linearly with the number of active tasks. While parallel SGMV kernels (e.g., Punica) can compress this sequential overhead on high-end GPUs, compiling custom CUDA extensions is non-trivial and may fail on resource-constrained consumer edge hardware or CPU-only cloud nodes. The authors' recommendation of a hard Top-1 routing fallback ($k=1$) in these low-power environments is a vital, practical mitigation.
2. **Infrastructure-Serving Complexity Trade-off**: As the authors honestly disclose, the framework shifts complexity from the model parameters to the underlying systems-serving infrastructure (on-the-fly partitioning, dynamic parameter merging, sequential dispatching, and output scatter re-assembly). This is a crucial trade-off: practitioners must weigh model-level simplicity (which avoids optimization collapse) against system-level serving simplicity.
3. **Representational Drift under Full Fine-Tuning**: PFSR and UNC rely on the assumption that penultimate representation manifolds remain semantically aligned, which is guaranteed under PEFT (LoRA). However, under severe representational drift during full fine-tuning with large learning rates, the representation manifolds undergo topological divergence, degrading routing specificity. The proposed mitigations (such as representational alignment objectives) are theoretically sound but reintroduce optimization constraints during the fine-tuning phase, slightly relaxing the zero-shot minimalist claim.
4. **Generalization Boundaries (Non-classification Experts)**: For continuous value regression or generative diffusion networks that completely lack classification heads, raw similarity projection is undefined. The proposed unsupervised $K$-means centroid anchors solve this boundary but reintroduce a minor dependency on a small calibration split, relaxing the strict "zero calibration data" claim.

## Reproducibility
The paper achieves an **excellent** standard of reproducibility:
- Every mathematical operation is explicitly written down and justified.
- The step-by-step pipeline is completely formalized in Algorithm 1.
- All hardware specifications (NVIDIA A100 GPU, Intel Xeon CPU) and hyperparameter settings (e.g., temperature $\tau=0.001$, $C_{sub}=256$, number of components $J=4$, ridge regularization $\epsilon=10^{-4}$) are fully disclosed.
- The authors provide extensive micro-benchmarks and sensitivity sweeps (Tables 3, 5, 6, 7), ensuring that any practitioner can replicate the exact system latency, vocabulary scaling, and OOD rejection behaviors reported.
