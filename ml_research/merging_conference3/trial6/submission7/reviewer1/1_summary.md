# Summary of the Paper

## Main Topic and Approach
This paper tackles the problem of dynamic weight-space routing in neural network expert model merging under heterogeneous deployment streams. While dynamic model-merging routing has shown promise in dynamically blending expert parameters based on representation activations at test time, existing methods introduce highly parameter-intensive, complex gating mechanisms (such as wave-inspired metaphors or unregularized multi-layer routers) and suffer from two critical issues:
1. **Optimization Bloat & Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Overfitting**: Over-parameterized gating layers suffer from severe overfitting on tiny test-time calibration sets and fail catastrophically on OOD inputs.
2. **Heterogeneity Collapse**: Under mixed-task streaming scenarios, sample-wise coefficients are averaged over the batch to comply with accelerator requirements, which forces the coefficients toward a flat, uniform average, destroying task-specific specialization.

To address these challenges, the authors advocate a minimalist philosophy, introducing a zero-shot, parameter-free framework consisting of:
- **Parameter-Free Subspace Routing (PFSR)**: Projects high-dimensional penultimate layer representations onto a low-dimensional task coordinate subspace by calculating maximum cosine similarity against frozen pre-trained expert classification weights. It uses temperature-scaled Softmax to derive routing coefficients with zero training, zero trainable parameters, and zero calibration data.
- **Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC)** and **Class-Size Scaling Calibration**: Resolve cross-expert scale mismatch and statistical bias in asymmetrical output registries (e.g., small classification heads vs. massive next-token heads) through training-free statistical normalization.
- **Micro-Batch Homogenization (MBH)**: Partitions mixed-task streams into homogeneous micro-batches on the fly, allowing specialized parameter merging on task-specific sub-batches and re-assembling the outputs, completely shielding the model from heterogeneity collapse.
- **PEFT (LoRA) Integration**: Binds the framework under Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning to ensure spatial GPU VRAM viability, keeping active expert parameters as low-rank adapters ($<1\%$ footprint).

## Key Findings and Claims
1. **Empirical Deconstruction of Over-parameterized Routers**: The paper proves that wave-inspired routing mechanisms like QWS-Merge suffer from severe transductive overfitting and catastrophic OOD collapse (e.g., dropping to $10\%$ accuracy on SVHN), which can be fully resolved or outperformed by simple $L_2$ regularization on standard linear primitives.
2. **Layer-Averaging Collapse Proof**: The authors mathematically prove and empirically demonstrate that under shared classification head constraints, layer-wise dynamic parameters follow collinear optimization trajectories, rendering multi-layer routing networks redundant and inferior to a simple, global single-layer router.
3. **High Joint Accuracy & Robustness of PFSR+MBH**: On a synthetic Isolating Coordinate Sandbox, PFSR+MBH achieves a Joint Mean accuracy of $75.00\%$ under homogeneous streams and $71.60\%$ under heterogeneous streams with zero parameters, outperforming complex parametric baselines (such as QWS-Merge which collapses to $47.50\%$).
4. **Real-World and LLM Scaling**: On Vision Transformers (ViT-Base on DomainNet) and large-scale NLP experts (LLaMA-7B), PFSR+MBH recovers up to $97.5\%$ of the standalone expert ceilings under highly heterogeneous streams with zero parameters and negligible serving overhead.
5. **Dynamic Adaptation & Vocabulary Mitigation**: The paper proposes Subspace Dimension Reduction and Sub-Vocabulary Prototype Selection to reduce projection latency over massive vocabulary spaces by up to $132.2\times$, while demonstrating plug-and-play addition/deletion of task experts with zero joint calibration or training.

## Explicitly Claimed Contributions and Supporting Evidence
- **Analytical Proof of Layer-Averaging Collapse**: Derived using a first-order Taylor expansion and contractive Jacobian mappings under localized PEFT perturbations. Supported by sandbox experiments comparing L3-Linear against a global Linear Router, showing that layer-wise routers are systematically outperformed by a single global layer.
- **PFSR + MBH Framework**: Fully detailed in Algorithm 1, integrating UNC, MBH, statistical scaling calibration, OOD rejection, and SGMV parallelization. Supported by main sweep benchmarks (Table 1), showing a Joint Mean accuracy of $75.00\%$, and stream audits (Table 2), showing $71.60\%$ Joint Mean on mixed streams.
- **Statistical Class-Size Scaling Calibration (Eq. 2)**: Supported by Table 5, demonstrating that raw maximum similarity is highly biased toward high-vocabulary experts ($16.00\%$ routing accuracy for $C_2=10$ vs $100\%$ for $C_1=32000$), and applying Eq. 2 restores unbiased routing ($94.00\%$ and $98.00\%$ accuracies).
- **Unit-Norm Calibration (UNC, Eq. 15)**: Supported by Table 4, showing that UNC restores routing performance from $25.00\%$ to $75.00\%$ when expert heads have severe scale imbalances.
- **Generalization to Non-classification Experts**: Supported by proof-of-concept $K$-means clustering evaluations on the sandbox, yielding $60.30\%$ accuracy using unsupervised coordinate anchors on small calibration splits.
