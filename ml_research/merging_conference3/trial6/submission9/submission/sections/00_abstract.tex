\begin{abstract}
Dynamic model merging has emerged as a promising paradigm for multi-task model fusion, allowing specialized expert parameters to be combined on-the-fly depending on the input context. However, existing dynamic routing methods collapse spatial tokens via global average pooling before routing, causing severe spatial vulnerability under occlusion and "heterogeneity collapse" when processing mixed-task batches. In this paper, we present a systematic simulation analysis of routing dynamics in token space. We introduce \textbf{Cross-Attention Multi-Expert Routing (CAM-Router)}, an empirically driven framework that retains full spatial token sequences and employs trainable task-expert queries to dynamically attend to localized feature regions. Additionally, to resolve the zero-sum competitive bottleneck of traditional Softmax constraints, we apply independent bounded sigmoidal gating. We validate CAM-Router through extensive, multi-dimensional parameter sweeps and stress tests simulated on a 14-layer compact Vision Transformer coordinate sandbox across disparate simulated tasks (MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, SVHN). Our exhaustive evaluation demonstrates that CAM-Router achieves a breakthrough simulated Joint Mean Accuracy of \textbf{57.07\%}, representing a \textbf{+15.10\%} absolute improvement over the static uniform baseline (41.97\%) and outperforming the quantum SOTA QWS-Merge by \textbf{+32.17\%}. Furthermore, under fair structural evaluations, CAM-Router displays extraordinary robustness, maintaining a stable accuracy of \textbf{53.63\%} under systematic spatial occlusions (up to 80\% patch masking) and remaining completely robust at \textbf{55.47\%} under large mixed task batch sizes ($B=256$) via Decoupled Historical Gating (DHG), where standard pooling-based methods collapse.
\end{abstract}
