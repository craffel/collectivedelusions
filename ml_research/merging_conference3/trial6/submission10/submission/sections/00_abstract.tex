\begin{abstract}
While dynamic model merging has emerged as a powerful paradigm for multi-task inference, calibrating dynamic routing heads in low-data regimes often leads to catastrophic overfitting or representational collapse, particularly on high-conflict datasets. In this paper, we propose Task-Correlation Prior Regularization (TCPR), a simple yet highly effective approach to guide routing head optimization using pre-computed cross-task similarity priors. We formulate two variants---parameter-space (TCPR-Param) and representation-space (TCPR-Rep) similarity---and apply them to regularize a decoupled, independent Bounded Sigmoidal Router (BSigmoid-Router). Through exhaustive empirical validation using a Vision Transformer backbone across four diverse image classification datasets, we demonstrate that TCPR consistently prevents high-conflict task collapse and bridges the performance gap to specialist experts. Our rigorous ablation studies and logarithmic hyperparameter sweeps over $\beta \in [10^{-6}, 10^2]$ confirm that incorporating task-relatedness priors provides a robust, scale-invariant pathway for low-data model merging, surpassing previous complex wave-interference methods without test-time optimization overhead.
\end{abstract}
