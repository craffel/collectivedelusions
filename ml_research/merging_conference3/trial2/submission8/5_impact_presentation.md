# 5. Impact and Presentation

## Major Strengths
1. **High Practical Utility & Cost-Efficiency:** The paper directly addresses key edge-deployment constraints (storage, memory bandwidth, network-transmission bandwidth) by compressing task vectors by 90% to 95%. Storing these vectors in sparse formats like CSR enables a 5x to 10x reduction in raw storage footprint, making the deployment of dozens of task experts on memory-bounded edge hardware extremely viable.
2. **Training-Free & Low Overhead:** The post-hoc compression is completely training-free and relies on simple magnitude-based pruning (sorting updates, which takes milliseconds). This avoids the compute- and memory-intensive calculations of second-order methods (Hessian or Fisher diagonals) and fragile test-time optimization, aligning perfectly with resource-constrained environments.
3. **Intellectual Honesty and Scientific Nuance:** The authors are highly commended for reporting a "negative" finding with exceptional clarity: training-stage loss-landscape flatness (via SAM) does not provide a coordinate-aligned sparsification buffer under well-converged regimes compared to standard AdamW. The two optimization schemes exhibit nearly identical resilience when paired with norm-preserving rescaling. This is a very valuable and refreshing insight for the community.
4. **The Saliency Double-Bind Insight:** Formulating the Saliency Double-Bind trade-off between global scaling scale distortion and layer-wise scaling variance blowup is an elegant theoretical contribution. It provides a strong, logical justification for why practitioners should prefer simple global Uniform Pruning (NP-BTVP-U) over complex layer-wise schemes.
5. **Rigorous Validation & Statistical Significance:** Reporting means and standard deviations across 3 independent seeds on a diverse 4-dataset test suite with state-of-the-art baselines (TIES, DARE) ensures high scientific integrity.

## Areas for Improvement
1. **Generalizability to Larger Models and LLMs:** While the CLIP ViT-B/32 backbone is a standard and realistic vision encoder, the weight-space model merging community is heavily focused on Large Language Models (LLMs) (e.g., LLaMA, OPT). Extending this deterministic rescaling framework to LLMs and evaluating on text instruction datasets would dramatically increase the significance and reach of the work.
2. **"Norm-Preserved" Misnomer Clarification:** The title and framework name include "Norm-Preserved," but Section 3.3 and Appendix Section A mathematically demonstrate that the $1/p$ scaling factor actually *amplifies* (rather than strictly preserves) the expected $L_1$ norm by $2.58\times$ (Gaussian) to $3.30\times$ (Laplace). While this amplification is a beneficial "Signal-Strength Boost" (as the authors clarify), the name "Norm-Preserved" is mathematically a bit of a misnomer. A brief sentence in the intro/methodology explicitly pointing this out or renaming the framework to "Scale-Corrected" or "Signal-Preserved" would prevent confusion.
3. **Main-Text Integration of Quantization:** The authors mention preliminary quantization results in Appendix Section E, showing that combining 10% sparse task vectors with INT8 quantization yields an additional 4x storage saving with only 0.12% accuracy degradation. This is an incredibly powerful practical result! Moving this analysis or at least a brief summary of it to the main text of the paper would further amplify its practical impact and significance for real-world deployments.

## Overall Presentation Quality
The presentation is **excellent**. The writing is clear, direct, and concise. The logical flow is seamless, starting from the edge deployment motivation, through the mathematical formulation of the pruning strategies and rescaling, to the empirical results and edge-deployment storage analysis. Figures 1 and 2 are high-quality, readable, and directly support the narrative. The related work is comprehensive and accurately positions the paper relative to prior literature.

## Potential Impact / Significance
The paper has **high significance and impact**, particularly for the practical deployment of multi-task networks. By providing a training-free, robust, and mathematically grounded compression framework that maintains near-dense performance under 90% sparsification, this work offers direct, actionable solutions for edge AI and IoT. It also shifts research focus away from overly complex layer-wise coefficient optimization and toward simple, uniform scale preservation, which is highly valuable for building robust parameter-space representations in the wild.
