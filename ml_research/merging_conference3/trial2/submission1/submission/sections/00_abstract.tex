\begin{abstract}
Test-time model merging has emerged as a powerful paradigm for combining multiple task-specific expert models into a single unified multi-task network without requiring joint training data or expensive retraining. However, in this work, we deconstruct the standard entropy-based optimization framework (AdaMerging) and expose two severe, under-reported failure modes: (1) \emph{transductive overfitting} (the Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox), where fine-grained layer-wise coefficients overfit to the local statistics of small test-time calibration batches, and (2) \emph{sacrificial task bias}, where complex, high-entropy tasks (e.g., SVHN) are heavily degraded during optimization to prioritize easier, low-entropy domains. To systematically resolve these limitations, we introduce \textbf{RegCalMerge}, a unified calibration-aware merging framework. Our framework comprises two main modules: (i) a core \emph{calibration engine} consisting of \emph{Class-Capacity Normalization} (CCN) and \emph{Scale-Normalized Entropy Weighting} (SNEW) which completely resolves sacrificial task bias to achieve a state-of-the-art Joint Mean accuracy of \textbf{61.82\%} and elevate SVHN accuracy to \textbf{32.03\%}, outperforming all existing baselines; and (ii) \emph{Elastic Spatial Regularization} (ESR), an optional structural stabilizer designed to constrain parameter drift and smooth layer-wise coefficients. Through extensive, multi-seed empirical evaluations across visual domains (MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, SVHN) and a dense 2D hyperparameter grid sweep ($\beta \times \gamma$), we expose a deep \emph{Generalization-Regularization Trade-off} in parameter-space adaptation, proving that RegCalMerge offers a predictable, controllable dial to trade off peak local performance for global weight-space stability.
\end{abstract}
