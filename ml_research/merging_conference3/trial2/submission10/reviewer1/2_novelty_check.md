# 2. Novelty and Literature Context Check

## Theoretical and Technical Delta from Prior Work
The submission proposes a deconstructive, critical analysis of adaptive test-time model merging, specifically focusing on the state-of-the-art **AdaMerging** (Yang et al., ICLR 2024) framework. 

The paper's key technical "delta" from prior work includes:
1. **Intra-Task Layer Shuffling**: A diagnostic control that permutes optimized layer-wise coefficients across layers of the network to test their structural specialization.
2. **Spatial Averaging**: A post-hoc regularization method that replaces optimized layer-wise coefficients with their spatial mean, acting as a low-pass filter to smooth out transductive overfitting while retaining task-level scales.
3. **The Spatial Averaging Paradox**: The counter-intuitive finding that direct task-wise optimization fails and degrades performance, whereas indirect layer-wise optimization followed by post-hoc spatial averaging succeeds.
4. **Multi-Task Gradient Imbalance Theory**: A mathematical and conceptual explanation of the paradox, attributing it to uncalibrated prediction entropy under low-dimensional weight-space bottlenecks.
5. **Calibrated Prediction Entropy**: A proposed remedy that normalizes task losses at initialization, used as an experimental control to prove that low-dimensional bottlenecks are fundamentally incompatible with joint prediction entropy minimization.

## Contextualization and Attribution of Ideas
The paper correctly identifies and situates its baseline methods, such as **Task Arithmetic** (Ilharco et al., 2023), **TIES-Merging** (Yadav et al., 2023), **DARE-Merging** (Yu et al., 24), and **AdaMerging** (Yang et al., 2024). It also attributes the foundational test-time adaptation technique to **Tent** (Wang et al., 2021). The historical progression from static, grid-searched merging scales to dynamic, test-time adapted scales is well-articulated.
