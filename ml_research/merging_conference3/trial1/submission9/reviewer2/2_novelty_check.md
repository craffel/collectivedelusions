# Evaluation Component 2: Novelty and Scholarly Delta Check

## 1. Positioning within the Existing Literature
Model merging literature has recently bifurcated into two main trends:
1. **Heuristic Parameter-Conflict Resolvers:** Methods such as **Ties-Merging** (Yadav et al., 2023) and **DARE** (Yu et al., 2024) use magnitude-based pruning (trimming/dropping) and sign-election voting to resolve parameter-level interference. However, these methods rely on heuristic hyperparameters and do not address systematic scale mismatches across tasks/layers.
2. **Algebraic and Optimization-Heavy Systems:** Methods such as **OrthoMerge** (2025) and **SAIM/SVD Isotropic** (2025) map weight matrices onto Riemannian manifolds or use Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to balance representation scales. Other approaches like **AdaMerging** (Yang et al., 2024) and **SyMerge** (2025) rely on active test-time gradient updates via entropy minimization or teacher-student pseudo-labels, which require unlabeled data and incur massive inference latencies.

The proposed **RMS-Scale** and **PF-RMS** methods establish a third, **minimalist** paradigm. They operate entirely in parameter space, requiring no data, no active optimization loops, and no SVDs, running in strictly linear $O(K \cdot N)$ time.

## 2. Scholarly "Delta" from Prior Art
To assess the scholarly "delta" rigorously, we compare the submission's core concepts with closely related prior works:

*   **Comparison to DisTaC:** DisTaC (Distribution-aware Task Arithmetic) recognizes that task vector norm mismatches (caused by heterogeneous fine-tuning schedules) degrade merging performance. However, DisTaC typically relies on manual scaling factors or complex distributions, whereas the proposed RMS-Scale introduces an automated, closed-form, layer-wise normalization and scale calibration loop.
*   **Comparison to FroM (Frobenius Norm-Based Adaptive Model Merging):** FroM uses the Frobenius norm of task vectors to weight different tasks during merging (i.e., assigning a single coefficient per task). In contrast, the submission's **RMS-Scale** performs *layer-wise, element-wise normalization* to force task vectors into an isotropic, unit-variance representation *before* averaging, and then rescales the result. This is a much finer, layer-level structural realignment.
*   **Comparison to SVD Isotropic (SAIM-like) and OrthoMerge:** These methods balance representation scales via SVD of the weight matrices, which scales cubically $O(d^3)$. The submission's **Frobenius Parity Proof (Section 3.6)** provides a vital scholarly contribution by demonstrating that layer-wise RMS normalization is mathematically equivalent to parameter-count-scaled Frobenius-norm normalization. On real CLIP ViT-B/32 weight tensors, this is shown to yield *identical* activation cosine alignment to SVD Isotropic, but at a **100x speedup**, bypassing the cubic SVD bottleneck entirely.
*   **Comparison to REPAIR and FeatCal:** REPAIR (2023) and FeatCal (2025/2026) align statistics of intermediate pre-activations to resolve feature drift. However, they are **data-dependent**, requiring a forward pass on a calibration dataset. The proposed RMS-Scale and PF-RMS are **entirely data-free**, operating purely on weight tensors.
*   **The Delta of PF-RMS:** The derivation of the parameter-free variant PF-RMS is highly novel. When averaging $K$ normalized task vectors, high-dimensional parameter conflicts and partial task orthogonality cause a natural shrinkage of the update magnitude by a factor of $\alpha^l = \text{RMS}(\bar{\tau}_{\text{norm}}^l) \le 1.0$. PF-RMS analytically resolves this shrinkage layer-by-layer by dynamically inverting the alignment ratio ($\lambda^l = 1/\alpha^l$), which converges precisely to the high-dimensional orthogonal limit of $\sqrt{K}$ as the dimension increases.

## 3. Characterization of Novelty
We characterize the novelty of this paper as **significant and conceptually elegant**:
- **Conceptual Novelty:** While normalization (like RMSNorm or Weight Standardization) is widely used in neural architectures, applying it as a closed-form, layer-wise, and data-free *post-hoc model-merging operator* with analytical shrinkage compensation ($1/\alpha^l$) is a highly creative and original combination of existing geometric and deep learning insights.
- **Methodological Realignment (Occam's Razor):** The paper challenges the recent "complexity creep" in model-merging literature. It shows that expensive, SVD-based or test-time active optimization pipelines can be matched or exceeded by simple, two-line element-wise scale calibrations. This represents a valuable corrective force in the field.
- **Self-Citation Detail:** The paper cites `evance2026minimalist` ("Minimalist Paradigm in Parameter Space Optimization", Journal of Elegant Machine Learning, 2026) by the author Emily Vance. While self-citation is acceptable, a scholarly reviewer must note that the present work expands this minimalist philosophy directly to model merging and high-dimensional foundation models.
