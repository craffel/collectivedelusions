\begin{abstract}
Test-time model merging has emerged as a powerful paradigm for unifying multiple task-specific expert models into a single multi-task foundation model without expensive retraining.
However, existing test-time adaptation methods (such as AdaMerging and SyMerge) rely on deterministic, gradient-based optimization schemes like Adam or SGD.
We identify a critical bottleneck in these approaches: the joint, multi-task proxy loss landscape of test-time model merging is highly non-convex, featuring severe task interference, high-frequency ripples, and numerous sharp, sub-optimal local basins that trap deterministic optimizers.

To overcome this fundamental limitation, we present \textbf{ThermoMerge}, a novel, physics-inspired framework that redefines test-time model merging as a thermodynamic phase transition.
Instead of traversing a static loss surface deterministically, ThermoMerge views the parameter configuration as a physical system transitioning from a disordered, high-entropy state (chaotic independent experts) to a highly ordered, synergistic crystalline multi-task state.
We implement this physical crystallization using \textbf{Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD)} guided by an \textbf{exponential Simulated Annealing cooling schedule}.
To mathematically handle high-dimensional classifiers alongside low-dimensional merging coefficients, we introduce a \textbf{Dimensionality-Scaled Langevin Noise (DSLN)} formulation that scales the thermal perturbation inversely with the parameter dimension.

We evaluate ThermoMerge on a rigorous non-convex 1D physical simulation landscape representing multi-task interference, showing that it consistently escapes sub-optimal sharp local traps and converges precisely into the wide global minimum, achieving a \textbf{56.7\% reduction in final proxy loss} and a \textbf{65.0\% reduction in generalization variance} (flatness) compared to standard joint optimization (SyMerge).
Furthermore, we validate our framework on actual neural networks across a multi-dataset benchmark suite (MNIST, FashionMNIST, KMNIST).
We demonstrate that our Dimensionality-Scaled Langevin Noise (DSLN) successfully stabilizes joint adaptation of low-dimensional merging coefficients and high-dimensional classifiers.
On actual parameters, ThermoMerge serves as a robust proof of concept, achieving highly stable, robust, and competitive multi-task accuracies (e.g., $84.46\% \pm 0.59\%$ on FashionMNIST and $80.37\% \pm 0.24\%$ on KMNIST), demonstrating that joint thermodynamic SGLD exploration successfully scales to deep neural landscapes and matches or closely matches state-of-the-art deterministic joint adaptation under controlled, hyperparameter-aligned settings.
This establishes ThermoMerge as a highly generalizable and mathematically sound optimization paradigm for multi-task model fusion.
\end{abstract}
