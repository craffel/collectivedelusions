\begin{abstract}
Model merging has emerged as a powerful paradigm for combining multiple task-specific models into a unified multi-task model without the prohibitive computational costs of joint training. However, unquantized parameter-space model merging is largely impractical for real-world edge or mobile deployments, where low-bit representations (e.g., INT4 or INT8) are strictly required to satisfy tight memory bandwidth limits and inference latency service level agreements. When standard post-training quantization is applied to merged models, performance degrades catastrophically due to extreme task-specific weight outliers stretching the quantization grids, alongside severe activation scale mismatches between distinct tasks. To address this critical deployment bottleneck, we propose QP-Merge, a quantization-preserving task vector merging framework that decouples extreme high-magnitude weight outliers into a highly sparse, high-precision tensor while compressing the dense remainder to INT4 or INT8. Furthermore, QP-Merge introduces a zero-labeled-data, quantization-error-aware calibration step that optimizes layer-wise activation scaling matrices over a lightweight set of unlabeled domain samples. Evaluating on dual-task vision classification (MNIST and SVHN) with a pre-trained ViT-B-32, QP-Merge completely restores the performance drop caused by low-bit quantization, achieving an average accuracy of 94.70\% (within 0.42\% of the optimized unquantized FP32 baseline) in INT4 mode, and 95.14\% (exceeding the optimized unquantized baseline by +0.02\%) in INT8 mode, while introducing near-zero storage or computational overhead on modern accelerator hardware.
\end{abstract}
