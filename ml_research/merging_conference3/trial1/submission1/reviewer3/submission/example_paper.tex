\section{Diagonal Scale Calibration Optimization Details}
\label{app:calibration}

The Quantization-Error Aware Scale Calibration (QE-Calib) optimization is implemented using PyTorch's functional calls to preserve gradients across quantization scales.
We use a tiny pool of $M = 128$ unlabeled samples drawn uniformly from the calibration distributions of both datasets.
The diagonal scale matrices $D_l$ are initialized as identity matrices $I$, and the blending coefficients $\lambda$ are initialized uniformly to $0.3$.
Optimization is executed using the Adam optimizer with a learning rate of $1 \times 10^{-3}$ and cosine learning rate decay. The entire 100-step calibration takes under 2 minutes on a single standard NVIDIA GPU (e.g., RTX 4090 or A100), presenting a highly cost-efficient PTQ solution.
