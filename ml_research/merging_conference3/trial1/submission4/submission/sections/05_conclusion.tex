\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this work, we introduced \textbf{FluidMerge} (Fluid-Dynamic Parameter Coalescence), a novel framework that reimagines the model merging paradigm by modeling parameter trajectories during test-time adaptation as a continuous-time advection-diffusion fluid flow. In doing so, we bridge deep learning weight optimization with the physics of viscous fluid mixtures, formulating self-supervised teacher gradients as advection forces and parameter-space diffusion as structural fluid viscosity.

To resolve the formidable representational \emph{domain shift barrier} and the coordinate-dependency of grid Laplacians, we successfully designed, implemented, and evaluated two core techniques---Expert-Weighted Initial Boundary Conditions and Fisher-Information-based Viscosity---across eight diverse classification tasks using a Vision Transformer (ViT-B-32). Our empirical evaluations demonstrate that while post-hoc self-training from raw base weights is severely limited by distribution shift, initializing the fluid trajectory at the Task Arithmetic boundary condition places the model in the multi-task basin. Combining this boundary condition with Fisher-Information viscosity successfully prevents representation collapse, stabilizes continuous-time integration, and outperforms standard $L_2$ weight anchoring and competitive test-time baselines. By shifting the paradigm from static weight averages to continuous physical dynamics and mathematically sound viscosity operators, this work lays a rigorous foundation for highly stable, synergistic multi-task parameter coalescence.
