# Intermediate Review Report 1: Summary of the Paper

## 1. Main Topic and Objective
This paper presents a highly rigorous, methodology-focused sanity check and representational analysis of the layer-wise model merging paradigm in deep neural networks (specifically CLIP ViT-B/32). Recent state-of-the-art (SOTA) test-time adaptation merging methods, such as AdaMerging and SyMerge, claim that optimizing merging coefficients layer-by-layer is critical to successfully navigating destructive interference between diverse task experts. The objective of this work is to stress-test this fundamental assumption and investigate whether the learned layer-wise, task-wise coefficients ($\lambda^l_k$) represent actual, physically meaningful layer-specific representational contributions or if they are largely an optimization noise artifact or transductive overfitting behavior.

## 2. Approach and Methodology
The authors construct a **Sanity-Checking and Interpretability Suite** designed to stress-test optimized merging coefficients. They evaluate these coefficients using three main diagnostic treatments, a representational similarity check, and two additional empirical sweeps across **3 independent random seeds (42, 100, 2026)**:
*   **Diagnostic Treatment 1: Intra-Task Layer Shuffling (Shuffle Treatment):** Randomly permuting the optimized coefficients of each task across the 13 layers (12 transformer blocks + 1 projection layer). This tests if the exact layer-wise assignment is critical.
*   **Diagnostic Treatment 2: Task-Wise Spatial Averaging (Mean Treatment):** Replacing the layer-wise coefficients with their average across all layers for that task, reducing the parameter count from $L \times K$ (52 parameters) to just $K$ parameters (a 92.3% reduction in search space). This tests if layer-wise granularity is redundant.
*   **Diagnostic Treatment 3: Norm-Bounded Perturbation (Noise Treatment):** Injecting relative Gaussian noise into the optimized coefficients to evaluate the sensitivity and flatness of the merging optimization landscape.
*   **Representational Similarity (CKA) Analysis:** Computing the linear Centered Kernel Alignment (CKA) similarity index between the independent task expert backbones and the merged backbone at Layer 6 on CIFAR-10 inputs to analyze if the optimized layer-wise coefficients correlate with physical feature alignments.
*   **Empirical Validation of Explicit Coefficient Regularization (Appendix B):** A pilot study evaluating a proximity regularization penalty ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{calibration}} + \beta \sum ||\lambda^l_k - 0.3||^2_2$) across a sweep of $\beta \in [0.0, 1.0]$.
*   **Calibration Sample Size Sweep (Appendix C):** Analyzing the impact of calibration sample size $N_{\text{cal}} \in [8, 128]$ on transductive overfitting.
*   **Coefficient Variance Profiles (Appendix D):** Visualizing the layer-wise coefficient profiles of the 13 groups under different optimizers.

To ensure rigorous optimization, the authors evaluate two distinct optimization paradigms:
1.  **Zero-Order Adaptive 1+1 Evolution Strategy (1+1 ES)** (run for 500 steps).
2.  **First-Order Gradient Descent (Adam GD)** using functional autograd (run for 200 steps).

The experiments are conducted on a multi-task vision benchmark using a pre-trained **CLIP ViT-B/32** backbone and 4 classification datasets: **MNIST**, **FashionMNIST**, **CIFAR-10**, and **SVHN**. Calibration is performed using an unlabeled calibration split.

## 3. Key Findings
*   **The Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox:** Under unconstrained zero-order Adaptive 1+1 ES search, layer-specific variation is shown to be a high-frequency optimization noise artifact: replacing complex layer-wise parameters with their flat spatial average per task acts as a spatial regularizer, actually improving average test accuracy from $85.07 \pm 0.47\%$ to $85.21 \pm 0.11\%$. Under first-order Adam GD, the optimizer finds a highly precise, delicate configuration of layer coefficients that is extremely sensitive to shuffling (causing a $5.43\%$ drop in average performance and a $15.69\%$ collapse on CIFAR-10) or spatial averaging (causing a $10.35\%$ collapse on CIFAR-10). However, this delicate layer-specificity fails to outperform the unoptimized Task Arithmetic baseline ($84.44 \pm 0.37\%$) on unseen test data while introducing 4x greater seed variance, revealing it as a delicate transductive overfitting artifact on the small calibration set.
*   **The SVHN Rescue vs. CIFAR-10 Collapse Trade-off:** The paper reveals that average performance metrics mask severe single-task degradations. Collapsing layer-wise parameters to their spatial mean breaks the joint entropy task-bias and rescues the sacrificial task (SVHN) by improving its accuracy by up to 6.38%, but does so at the direct cost of collapsing the highly complex, non-linear CIFAR-10 task by up to 10.35%. This proves that layer-specificity has genuine functional importance for complex domains.
*   **Extreme Landscape Flatness:** Injecting up to **50% relative Gaussian noise** into the optimized coefficients has virtually no negative impact on the merged model's performance under both optimizers, confirming that the model merging optimization landscape resides in an exceptionally flat basin.
*   **CKA vs. Downstream Accuracy Decoupling:** While spatial averaging improves activation similarity on average ($+0.0069$ CKA for 1+1 ES and $+0.0036$ CKA for Adam GD), high-level kernel alignment is a poor predictor of downstream classification performance because activation correlation decouples from weight-space decision boundary integrity, with CIFAR-10 accuracy collapsing by over 10% under averaging despite maintaining high activation correlation ($>0.95$ CKA).
*   **Identification of Joint Entropy Minimization Task-Bias:** The authors identify that joint prediction entropy minimization naturally sacrifices performance on complex, high-entropy tasks (like SVHN) to minimize total loss, as simpler tasks (like MNIST) have cleaner gradients and stable prediction patterns.
*   **Proximity Coefficient Regularization (Appendix B):** Incorporating the proposed $L_2$ proximity penalty successfully stabilizes test performance, preventing the catastrophic optimization drift on CIFAR-10 and achieving peak performance ($86.57\%$) at $\beta=0.5$.
*   **Calibration Size Threshold (Appendix C):** Small calibration sets ($N_{\text{cal}}=8$) suffer from severe transductive overfitting, whereas scaling up to 128 images per task stabilizes and generalizes performance ($87.06\%$ average accuracy).

## 4. Explicitly Claimed Contributions
1.  **Exposing the Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox:** Demonstrating how unconstrained test-time adaptation of layer-wise merging coefficients on small calibration sets leads to transductive overfitting.
2.  **Introduction of the Sanity-Checking Interpretability Suite:** Formulating layer-wise shuffling, task-wise spatial averaging, and norm-bounded noise perturbations as standard diagnostic control checks for model merging.
3.  **Discovery of the SVHN Rescue vs. CIFAR-10 Collapse Trade-off:** Proving that simple average metrics mask catastrophic single-task performance collapse, and showing that layer-specific hierarchies are functionally critical for complex tasks.
4.  **Exposing the CKA-Accuracy Decoupling:** Showing that linear CKA representational similarity is a poor predictor of fine-grained classification accuracy due to decoupling between activation alignment and decision boundary integrity.
5.  **Exposing Joint Entropy Minimization Task-Bias & Proposing Coefficient Regularization:** Identifying how joint entropy objectives naturally sacrifice complex tasks and formulating explicit coefficient regularization as a concrete solution.
