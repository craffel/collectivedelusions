# Soundness and Methodology Check

## Soundness Rating: Excellent

## Critique of Mathematical Rigor
- **Spectral Consensus Formulation:** The use of SVD to analyze the horizontally concatenated task updates $\mathbf{M}^{(l)}$ is mathematically sound. Horizontally concatenating task vectors allows the columns of $\mathbf{M}^{(l)}$ to represent joint updates, and the left-singular vectors of SVD naturally extract the principal coordinate directions of output parameter variation.
- **Eckart-Young-Mirsky Theorem (Theorem 3.1):** The proof is correct. It provides a formal guarantee that GSC-Merge constructs the mathematically optimal low-rank representation under the Frobenius norm, establishing a strict upper bound on representational drift.
- **Implicit Regularization (Proposition 3.2):** The proof is correct and mathematically precise. It correctly proves that the Grassmannian projection operator $P^{(l)}$ is a non-strict contraction (or non-expansive mapping) under both spectral and Frobenius norms. However, the conceptual explanation of why GSC-Merge mitigates the Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox contains a minor mathematical looseness. The authors state that GSC-Merge "restricts the active parameter search space of the optimizer from $d_{out}$ dimensions to a low-dimensional $r$-dimensional subspace." In reality, the learnable parameters being optimized are the merging coefficients $\alpha^{(l)} \in \mathbb{R}^K$. Therefore, the optimizer's parameter search space is $\mathbb{R}^K$ (where $K$ is the number of tasks, e.g., 4) in both unconstrained OFS-Tune and GSC-Merge. The true regularizing effect of GSC-Merge does not come from reducing the optimizer's parameter space, but rather from projecting the task vectors $V_k^{(l)}$ onto the low-rank subspace. This ensures that the blended task updates $\sum_k \alpha_k^{(l)} \tilde{V}_k^{(l)}$ are restricted to a $K$-dimensional subspace of the $r$-dimensional consensus space, effectively filtering out high-frequency, task-specific noise in the tail singular vectors of $\{V_k^{(l)}\}$. While the core regularizing argument holds, the authors should clarify this distinction to maintain absolute mathematical precision.

## Critique of Architectural Choices and Mitigations
- **Task-Conditional Parameter Swapping:**
  - *Context:* The framework merges linear projection layers while keeping lightweight normalization, biases, and embeddings task-specific, which are swapped dynamically at test-time.
  - *Soundness:* This is a highly pragmatic and mathematically justified choice. Normalization layers (like LayerNorm) track running feature statistics and mean/variance boundaries. Directly merging normalization parameters across highly disparate visual domains (e.g., MNIST digits vs. CIFAR-10 natural objects) ruins feature calibration and leads to catastrophic representation collapse. 
  - *Robustness:* To ensure methodological rigor, the authors explicitly address this requirement in Section 3.7. Crucially, they perform an ablation study in Section 4.4 ("Truly Task-Agnostic Model Merging") where non-target parameters are strictly kept at their pre-trained base values. The results demonstrate that GSC-Merge (with $\gamma = 0.5$) remains highly competitive and matches unconstrained performance, proving that the spectral alignment benefits are robust even under a strict task-agnostic setting.
- **SVD Computational Scalability:**
  - *Context:* Full SVD has a cubic complexity of $\mathcal{O}(d_{out}^2 \cdot K \cdot d_{in})$ per layer, which could be a bottleneck for Large Language Models (LLMs).
  - *Soundness & Mitigation:* The authors explicitly address this in Section 3.7 and Appendix A by implementing **Randomized SVD**. They provide CPU benchmarks across various model layer scales (ViT-Tiny, ViT-Base, LLaMA-7B reduced layer). They demonstrate a massive **23.56$\times$ speedup** on the LLaMA-7B sized layer (reducing SVD computation from 10.45 seconds to just 0.44 seconds per matrix) with a negligible reconstruction error difference (under $2.5\%$ for $\gamma=0.1$). This thoroughly mitigates any scalability concerns.
- **SVD Projection Directions:**
  - *Context:* SVD can be projected onto the output activation space (left-singular vectors), input activation space (right-singular vectors), or bilaterally.
  - *Soundness & Mitigation:* The authors justify their left-singular output-space projection choice. To provide empirical validation, Appendix C presents a systematic pilot study comparing the three projection directions. The output-space projection achieves an absolute accuracy lead of up to **26.14%** over input-space and bilateral projections, which is explained by coordinate-alignment in downstream layers.
- **Derivative-Free Optimization (Section 3.8):** Formulates the usage of CMA-ES and Bayesian Optimization for parameter search under extremely low data regimes, offering robust gradient-free alternatives to Adam.

## Conclusion on Soundness
The methodology is **exceptionally sound, transparent, and rigorous**. Every major design choice is backed by formal mathematical proofs, extensive empirical pilot studies, or clear scaling mitigations, leaving no unaddressed gaps in methodology.
