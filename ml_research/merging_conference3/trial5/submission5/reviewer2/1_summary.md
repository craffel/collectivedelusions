# 1. Summary of the Paper

## Main Topic and Approach
This paper provides a rigorous methodological and empirical deconstruction of Quantum Wavefunction Superposition Merging (QWS-Merge), a stylized "quantum-inspired" state-of-the-art model-merging framework. Model merging seeks to combine multiple task-specific expert networks into a single multi-task model without expensive joint retraining. QWS-Merge claims state-of-the-art results by formulating merging coefficients as a quantum superposition that collapses via a wave-like cosine phase-interference function in parameter Hilbert space. 

The authors deconstruct QWS-Merge within a controlled "Isolating Coordinate Sandbox" designed to separate routing dynamics from weight-space coordinate misalignment. They show that QWS-Merge is highly unstable, collapses in low-data regimes, and performs worse than static uniform merging. As a simple classical alternative, the authors propose the **Layer-wise Low-dimensional Classical Router (L3-Router)**, utilizing standard linear channels and classical $L_2$ weight decay (regularization). They evaluate three variants: L3-Linear, L3-Tanh (bounded), and L3-Softmax (normalized). Furthermore, the authors mathematically expose the "layer-averaging collapse" phenomenon, which explains why a simple global classical **Linear Router** baseline outperforms all multi-layer models. Finally, the authors audit deployment streams under heterogeneous (mixed-task) batches, identify "heterogeneity collapse" in dynamic routers, and critically deconstruct the "Robustness-Accuracy Illusion" in normalized routing activations (specifically, L3-Softmax).

## Key Findings
1. **Instability of Quantum-Inspired Formulations:** QWS-Merge completely collapses under rigorous validation, achieving a Joint Mean accuracy of only **36.10%** (worse than static uniform merging's **43.40%**), and drops to **2.00%** on out-of-distribution (OOD) SVHN.
2. **Superiority of Simple Layer-wise Routing:** Replacing the cosine wave formulation with the proposed **L3-Linear** classical router avoids collapse, achieving **63.10%** Joint Mean (a **+27.00%** absolute improvement over QWS-Merge).
3. **The Ultimate Baseline Confounder:** The simplest possible baseline—a global, unregularized classical **Linear Router**—outperforms all multi-layer models, achieving the highest Joint Mean of **67.20%**. This exposes a major blind spot in current model-merging literature.
4. **Task Heterogeneity Collapse:** Real-world heterogeneous mixed-task batches cause severe accuracy drops due to coefficient batch-averaging (dropping Linear Router from **67.20%** to **51.10%** and QWS-Merge to **10.80%**).
5. **Robustness-Accuracy Illusion:** While the **L3-Softmax** router appears highly stable under heterogeneous streams (dropping by only 4.10% absolute), its absolute accuracy is consistently inferior. This relative robustness is merely an artifact of Softmax constraints forcing dynamic routing coefficients toward a mediocre, uniform-like average.
6. **Generalization to CLIP-ViT-B/16:** A real-scale visual model-merging pilot confirms these trends on actual task-specific weight manifolds: QWS-Merge collapses to **41.20%**, whereas L3-Linear generalizes stably to **84.80%** (outperforming QWS-Merge by **+43.60%** absolute).

## Explicitly Claimed Contributions and Evidence
- **Rigorous Methodological Deconstruction of QWS-Merge:** Proves that the wave cosine activation is functionally unstable and over-parameterized. Evidence is provided via Table 2 (performance results) and Appendix E (learning rate sensitivity sweeps).
- **Introduction of L3-Router:** Formulates simple, low-dimensional classical alternatives with a **16.7%** parameter reduction (saving 56 parameters, from 336 to 280 parameters). Evidence is provided in Section 3.3 and Table 1.
- **Closed-Form Mathematical Proof of Layer-Averaging Collapse:** Proves that averaging layer-wise coefficients collapses the parameter space back to a single-layer routing space. Evidence is provided in Section 3.5.
- **Identification and Audit of Task Heterogeneity Collapse:** Reveals that dynamic routing collapses under mixed-task deployment streams. Evidence is provided in Section 4.4 and Table 3.
- **Exposing the "Robustness-Accuracy Illusion" of Softmax:** Conceptually and mathematically deconstructs why relative stability under Softmax can mask absolute inferiority. Evidence is provided in Appendix D.
- **Scale-Validation Pilot and Actionable Deployment Roadmap:** Provides a scale-validation pilot on CLIP-ViT-B/16 (Section 4.5) and a concrete roadmap detailing Triton-based parallel dynamic assembly kernels, including detailed FLOPs and memory bandwidth equations (Appendix A).
- **Extensive Robustness Audits:** Explores task correlation (Appendix G), multi-seed robustness (Appendix F), true layer-by-layer merging without averaging (Appendix H), and projection dimension sensitivity (Appendix I).
