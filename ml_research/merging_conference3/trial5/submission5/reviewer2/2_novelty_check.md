# 2. Novelty Check

An evaluation of the key novel aspects, the 'delta' from prior work, and the characterization of novelty (e.g., incremental, significant).

## Characterization of Novelty
The novelty of this paper is **highly significant and refreshing**, particularly as a methodological "sanity check" and deconstruction of the growing trend of "quantum-inspired" or overly complex mathematical metaphors in machine learning. Instead of merely proposing yet another complex architecture, this paper performs a critical "audit" of the existing state-of-the-art (QWS-Merge) and systematically reveals that simpler, classical linear baselines with basic regularization (L2 weight decay) perform far better.

## The 'Delta' from Prior Work
1. **Deconstruction of QWS-Merge:** Prior work (QWS-Merge) claimed that quantum superposition and wave cosine phase interference in parameter Hilbert space are necessary for optimal test-time model merging. This paper represents the first work to systematically strip away the quantum vocabulary and isolate the true routing dynamics. It proves that the wave-like cosine activation function is functionally unstable and leads to catastrophic collapse.
2. **Layer-Averaging Collapse Proof:** While prior model-merging literature frequently employs layer-wise specialized routing, this paper is the first to provide a closed-form mathematical proof demonstrating that when coefficients are averaged to merge a unified classification head, the multi-layer routing parameter space collapses back to a single-layer routing space. This exposes a foundational redundancy in layer-wise routing literature.
3. **Task Heterogeneity Collapse & Stream Audit:** Standard test-time adaptation and dynamic merging evaluations assume homogeneous test streams (where all samples in a batch belong to the same task). This paper introduces a highly realistic "deployment audit" under heterogeneous (mixed-task) streams, identifying a severe and previously unaddressed vulnerability: "heterogeneity collapse" caused by batch-wise coefficient averaging.
4. **The "Robustness-Accuracy Illusion":** Prior work often praises Softmax-constrained routing or TTA methods for showing minimal accuracy degradation under domain shifts. This paper introduces a novel conceptual deconstruction showing that this apparent "robustness" is merely an artifact of Softmax simplex normalization forcing routing coefficients toward a mediocre, uniform-like average, masking absolute performance inferiority.
5. **L3-Router Framework:** While simple, the formulation of L3-Router (Linear, Tanh, Softmax) under identical low-dimensional projections represents a clean, transparent, and parameter-efficient (16.7% reduction in routing parameters) classical benchmark that serves as a solid baseline for future model-merging research.

## Assessment of Novelty and Practical Value
From a **practitioner's standpoint**, the novelty here is exceptionally high and valuable. Rather than adding academic bloat, it actively deflates over-engineered academic complexity in favor of simple, deployable classical linear baselines. The identification of "heterogeneity collapse" and the mathematical proof of "layer-averaging collapse" are highly valuable insights for engineers trying to deploy these models in production environments. The proposed hardware-level roadmap (with LoRA and custom Triton kernels) bridges the gap between synthetic sandboxes and physical hardware deployment, making this paper highly practical.
