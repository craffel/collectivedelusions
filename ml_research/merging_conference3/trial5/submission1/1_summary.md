# 1. Summary of the Paper

## Goals
The main goal of the paper is to identify, formalize, and resolve a critical optimization failure in adaptive test-time model merging (TTA-MM), which the author terms the **Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox**. During online deployment, optimizing layer-wise merging coefficients using unsupervised objectives like Shannon entropy minimization on local, unlabeled transductive test streams causes the coefficients to overfit to transductive noise. This overfitting manifests as high-frequency spatial oscillations of the coefficients across adjacent layers of the neural network depth, which disrupts internal hierarchical representation flow, leading to catastrophic representation collapse and poor multi-task generalization.

To resolve this, the paper proposes **Riemannian Curvature-Regularized Test-Time Model Merging (RCR-Merge)**, a second-order geometric framework that regularizes the spatial Total Variation of merging coefficients. It weights adjacent-layer penalties by the geometric mean of the layers' pre-trained base curvatures (estimated via the trace of the diagonal Fisher Information Matrix). This establishes analytical coordinate barriers that shield highly sensitive bottleneck layers from coefficient fluctuations while permitting flexible adaptation in robust regions.

## Methods
The RCR-Merge framework is composed of a three-step sequential pipeline:
1. **Offline Base Curvature Estimation:** The diagonal trace of the Fisher Information Matrix (FIM) of the pre-trained base model $\theta_0$ is computed over a minuscule calibration set ($|D_{\text{cal}}| = 64$ samples) and normalized to serve as a spatial Riemannian metric across depth.
2. **Test-Time Adaptation via Entropy Minimization:** During online deployment, Shannon entropy of prediction probability distributions is minimized on unlabeled incoming streams to adapt the layer-wise merging coefficients $\boldsymbol{\lambda}$.
3. **Riemannian Curvature-Weighted Total Variation (RCR-TV) and Absolute Anchoring:** The objective is regularized by RCR-TV (penalizing adjacent-layer squared difference of coefficients scaled by the geometric mean of their pre-trained base curvatures) and an absolute coordinate anchor penalty that bounds global coefficient joint-drift.
4. **Gradient Norm Balancing (GNB):** A fully unsupervised, scale-invariant initialization heuristic balances the regularizer's maximum sensitivity gradient (evaluated at a worst-case spectral graph Laplacian perturbation) against the initial entropy loss gradient norm at step 0, completely removing scale-dependent hyperparameter tuning for the regularization weights.

The method is evaluated using a 30-seed simulation study on a synthetic Coupled Model II Landscape emulator and a Stage-wise Modular Transition Landscape, as well as real-world model-merging pilot studies on `bert-base-uncased` and `vit-base-patch16-224` architectures.

## Claims & Key Findings
- **Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox Validation:** Unconstrained AdaMerging collapses catastrophically, dropping below the static Uniform Baseline on both Coupled Covariance (-10.31% absolute drop) and Decoupled Isotropic Euclidean (-2.25% absolute drop) metrics.
- **RCR-Merge Superiority:** RCR-Merge successfully resolves this collapse, outperforming the Uniform Baseline (+3.06% absolute) and unconstrained AdaMerging (+10.33% absolute) under Coupled Covariance, and achieving outstanding results under the primary unbiased Decoupled Isotropic Euclidean metric (+3.05% and +5.68% absolute improvements).
- **Conformal Soft Barriers vs. Rigid Subspaces:** On modular transition landscapes (Stage-wise Modular Transition Landscape), RCR-Merge outperforms the global polynomial baseline PolyMerge ($d=2$) by **+2.32%** (coupled) and **+2.44%** (decoupled) absolute accuracy. This proves that global polynomial constraints suffer from curve deformation (Runge's phenomenon) across discrete block boundaries, whereas local conformal barriers adapt perfectly.
- **Robustness and Scalability:** The pre-trained base curvature approximation is exceptionally robust to online parameter drift (remaining indistinguishable from an oracle dynamic FIM up to 50% drift). Under extremely long-term streams (2,000 steps), a threshold-triggered local charting mechanism successfully re-estimates FIM and resets anchors, reducing variance and maintaining peak performance.
- **Real-World Feasibility:** Pilot studies on BERT-Base (NLP) and ViT-B/16 (CV) show that RCR-Merge prevents wild spatial coefficient oscillations, protects unadapted task accuracy (preserving 100% and 57.50% multi-task average accuracy vs. 75% and 47.50% for unconstrained), and runs with near-zero computational overhead.

## Detailed Summary of Contributions
1. **Paradox Formulation:** Identifies and formalizes the Overfitting-Optimizer Paradox in adaptive model merging, showing how unconstrained entropy optimization fits transductive noise and deforms representations.
2. **Geometric Framework:** Proposes RCR-Merge, a novel second-order regularizer that models parameter space as a Riemannian manifold with FIM trace weighting.
3. **Unsupervised Self-Scaling (GNB):** Develops Gradient Norm Balancing (GNB) and Dynamic GNB (D-GNB) to dynamically scale spatial and absolute anchoring penalties in a fully automated, scale-invariant, and hyperparameter-free manner at step 0.
4. **Theoretical Guarantees:** Proves Lemma 3.3 (Coordinate-level spatial barrier) and Theorem 3.4 (Representation drift bounding under spatial oscillations). Uses Spectral Graph Theory to model noise suppression as a Laplacian smoothing low-pass filter.
5. **Rigorous Empirical Verification:** Demonstrates state-of-the-art results across 30 random seeds, resolve evaluation circularity through unbiased Decoupled Isotropic Euclidean evaluations, and provides successful real-world multimodal pilot implementations on full-scale BERT and Vision Transformer models.
