# Evaluation Step 1: Summary of the Paper

## 1. Main Topic & Problem Statement
The paper addresses the challenge of **multi-task model consolidation** in deep learning via **parameter-space model merging**. Specifically, it investigates **dynamic model merging**, where the parameter-space merging coefficients are dynamically determined on a per-input or per-batch basis during inference. 
The paper focuses on deconstructing and critically evaluating a recently proposed dynamic model merging method, **Quantum Wavefunction Superposition Merging (QWS-Merge)**, which uses complex quantum-mechanical metaphors (representing parameters as eigenstates and using wave phase-interference equations) to compute routing coefficients. The authors apply Occam's razor to investigate whether these complex quantum metaphors are truly necessary, or if their performance can be matched or exceeded by simple, well-tuned classical baselines.

## 2. Technical Approach & Proposed Framework (BC-Router)
To systematically isolate and control confounding variables in dynamic weight routing, the paper introduces the **Bounded Classical Router (BC-Router)** framework, consisting of three main variants:
1. **Bounded Linear Router (BL-Router):** Constrains sample-level routing coefficients to a strict maximum ceiling ($\lambda_{\max} = 0.3$) using a Softmax scaling multiplier. This is designed to isolate the "Over-Scaling Confounder" (where unregularized routing coefficients approach $1.0$, pushing the model outside its convergence basin and causing collapse).
2. **Global Router with Layer-wise Scaling (GLS-Router):** Combines a single shared global routing projection head with trainable layer-specific task-scaling amplitudes ($R_k^{(l)}$) initialized to $0.3$. This isolates the "Layer-wise Specialization Confounder" (where QWS-Merge's layer-specific routing heads give it a capacity advantage over global baselines).
3. **Bounded Sigmoidal Router (BSigmoid-Router):** Replaces the standard Softmax function with independent, uncoupled Sigmoids, scaled by $\lambda_{\max} = 0.3$. This is designed to resolve the "Softmax Zero-Sum Competitive Bottleneck" where tasks are forced to compete for a shared routing budget during calibration.

## 3. Key Findings & Empirical Evidence
The authors evaluate these models using a capacity-constrained Vision Transformer backbone (`vit_tiny_patch16_224`) trained to high convergence across four distinct vision datasets: MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, and SVHN. The key findings include:
- **Paradigm Distinction (TTA vs. Offline Calibration):** QWS-Merge ($83.97 \pm 0.53\%$) and unsupervised test-time adaptation via AdaMerging ($89.30 \pm 0.00\%$) represent fundamentally different paradigms. AdaMerging requires active optimization (gradients and backward passes) during inference, which introduces massive latency ($495.0$ ms per batch) and vulnerability to stream noise. In contrast, offline-calibrated dynamic routing (like QWS-Merge or BSigmoid-Router) is optimized once on a tiny 64-sample calibration set and runs as a pure forward pass during inference with zero active optimization and minimal latency ($18.5$ ms per batch).
- **Deconstructing Classical Failures via L2 Regularization:** The unregularized classical Linear Router collapses on SVHN ($74.00 \pm 16.14\%$) due to low-data overfitting on the 64-sample calibration set. Applying standard L2 regularization (weight decay $\gamma = 1 \times 10^{-4}$) to the routing projection weights completely resolves this collapse, boosting SVHN accuracy to **$91.73 \pm 3.71\%$** (outperforming QWS-Merge by $+12.00\%$) and joint mean accuracy to $82.80 \pm 4.85\%$.
- **The Structural Under-Scaling Flaw of Softmax Bounding:** The Softmax-based BL-Router collapses catastrophically on SVHN ($31.73 \pm 16.03\%$) even with L2 regularization ($43.20 \pm 8.02\%$). This is deconstructed as a structural design flaw: capping the Softmax sum at $0.3$ forces each task to a meager $0.075$ scale under high uncertainty, which is too small to transfer specialized expert capabilities.
- **Resolving the Softmax Zero-Sum Bottleneck:** Our proposed Softmax-free **BSigmoid-Router** replaces Softmax with independent Sigmoids, completely resolving this competitive bottleneck to achieve a highly stable **$83.73 \pm 1.93\%$** joint homogeneous accuracy and **$83.96 \pm 2.27\%$** heterogeneous stream accuracy ($B=1$), outperforming QWS-Merge's $83.29 \pm 0.36\%$.
- **QWS-Merge as a Structural Regularizer:** The unregularized layer-wise GLS-Router exhibits extreme sensitivity to the calibration set across seeds (producing standard deviations up to $24.30\%$ on SVHN), collapsing to $64.80 \pm 3.53\%$ on FashionMNIST. This reveals that QWS-Merge's complex wave projection equations serve as a highly effective structural regularizer that stabilizes optimization in extremely low-data regimes.
- **Batch-Averaging Bottleneck on Heterogeneous Streams:** On interleaved streams, as batch size $B$ scales to 256, batch-averaged routing coefficients collapse to uniform values. This batch-averaging acts as a regularizing smoother for weaker/imbalanced Softmax routers but acts as a destructive capacity bottleneck for highly specialized sigmoidal routers.

## 4. Explicitly Claimed Contributions (with Evidence)
1. **Deconstruction of QWS-Merge:** The paper deconstructs QWS-Merge's wave projection, showing that standard L2 regularization and Softmax-free sigmoidal routing are sufficient to match or outperform its accuracy.
2. **Identification of Key Confounders:** It isolates the over-scaling confounder, the layer-wise specialization confounder, and the Softmax zero-sum competitive bottleneck.
3. **The BC-Router Framework:** It proposes and evaluates BL-Router, GLS-Router, and BSigmoid-Router to demonstrate how classical routers can be stabilized and optimized.
4. **Comprehensive Empirical Benchmarking:** It presents thorough homogeneous and heterogeneous stream evaluations across batch sizes, alongside ablation studies on calibration set size and L2 regularization sensitivity.
5. **Practical Complexity and Latency Analysis:** It profiles inference latency (Appendix A), showing that BSigmoid-Router is over $25\times$ faster than AdaMerging and highlighting PyTorch's tensor copying overhead as the primary physical bottleneck of weight-space dynamic routing.
