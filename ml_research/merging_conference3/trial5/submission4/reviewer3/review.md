# Peer Review

## 1. Strengths and Weaknesses

### Strengths
- **Rigorous Application of Occam's Razor:** The central strength of this work lies in its critical methodological deconstruction of Quantum Wavefunction Superposition Merging (QWS-Merge). The authors perform a crucial scientific service to the machine learning community by questioning the necessity of highly complex, mathematically over-engineered quantum metaphors. They apply Occam's razor to demonstrate that standard, classical baselines can match or outperform these flashy mathematical frameworks when optimized properly.
- **Elegant, Simple, and Effective Solutions:** Rather than replacing one complex metaphor with another, the paper champions simplicity. It shows that standard L2 regularization (weight decay) applied to a classical Linear Router completely resolves the reported SVHN collapse, outperforming QWS-Merge on SVHN by **$+12.00\%$** ($91.73\%$ vs. $79.73\%$). Furthermore, their proposed **BSigmoid-Router** replaces Softmax with independent, uncoupled Sigmoids—a remarkably clean, elegant, and parameter-efficient formulation (only 772 parameters) that completely bypasses the zero-sum competitive bottleneck during calibration, achieving stable and balanced multi-task performance.
- **Deconstruction of Structural Design Flaws:** The mathematical deconstruction of the Softmax-based coefficient bounding is high-signal. The authors identify and explain a subtle but severe structural under-scaling design flaw: capping Softmax-bounded routing coefficients at $0.3$ caps the global sum of coefficients at $0.3$, restricting individual task vectors to a meager scale ($0.075$) under uncertainty and causing the model to collapse. This structural bottleneck is beautifully resolved by the independent sigmoidal activations in the BSigmoid-Router.
- **Exemplary Honesty and Scientific Integrity (The Generalist-Specialist Paradox):** The authors highlight a fundamental limit of the entire weight-space dynamic routing sub-field: *none of the trainable methods outperform a simple, parameter-free static Uniform Merge in overall multi-task accuracy*. They explain that parameter merging is a zero-sum game of capacity; dynamically steering weights toward one task pulls them away from others, degrading overall average performance. Highlighting this "practical utility paradox" and defining the narrow operational and niche scenarios where dynamic routing is actually beneficial is an outstanding example of scientific transparency.
- **Thorough and Reproducible Evaluation:** The paper features robust evaluations across multiple random seeds, sensitivity analyses of regularization strength ($\gamma$), a calibration-set size ablation study (64, 128, 256 samples), and concrete hardware latency benchmarks demonstrating that the proposed feed-forward BSigmoid-Router is over **$25\times$ faster** than online test-time adaptation (AdaMerging).

### Weaknesses
- **Empirical Sandbox Scope:** The experiments are restricted to a compact Vision Transformer backbone (`vit_tiny_patch16_224`, 5.7M parameters) and four vision datasets (MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10, SVHN). While this environment is mathematically ideal for isolating and deconstructing parameter-routing dynamics without confounding scaling factors, verifying whether these scale-regularization and independent sigmoidal routing insights generalize to larger-scale backbones (e.g., Swin, ViT-Base/Large) and massive LLM families (e.g., LLaMA-1B/3B) would further strengthen the significance of the paper.
- **Localized Overfitting in Layer-wise Amplitudes (GLS-Router):** The authors find that their unregularized GLS-Router collapses on FashionMNIST ($64.80 \pm 3.53\%$) because its 56 layer-wise scaling parameters $R_k^{(l)}$ overfit during calibration. While they correctly identify this optimization gap and recommend applying weight decay directly to layer-specific amplitudes in future designs, providing a concrete formulation or a quick experiment showing that regularizing these amplitudes rescues GLS-Router's performance would make the baseline analysis more complete.
- **Online Streaming Adaptation Dynamics:** On heterogeneous streams, AdaMerging (TTA) is modeled statically using its offline-calibrated joint mean accuracy to avoid prohibitive real-time gradient optimization latency. While this is a reasonable and practical simplification, it fails to capture the actual online temporal dynamics (parameter drift, label shifts, and temporal sequence noise) of online Test-Time Adaptation, leaving the stream benchmark as an optimistic upper bound for AdaMerging.

## 2. Detailed Evaluation of Dimensions

### Soundness
* **Rating:** Excellent
* **Justification:** The technical claims in the paper are exceptionally well-supported by rigorous experimental design and sound mathematical analysis. The authors ensuring that the task experts are trained to *true convergence* resolves potential baseline-distortion flaws in prior pipelines. They utilize multi-seed reporting to capture statistical variance. Crucially, the mathematical deconstructions of the Softmax structural under-scaling flaw and the optimization behavior of unregularized routers on tiny calibration datasets are technically sound and verified through thorough sensitivity analyses (L2 weight decay sweeps and calibration data-scaling ablations). The empirical results completely back up their claims.

### Presentation
* **Rating:** Excellent
* **Justification:** The paper is beautifully written, exceptionally clear, and tightly structured. The narrative flow is compelling: it starts by questioning complex metaphors, introduces a systematic framework of controlled baselines, presents exhaustive empirical findings, and finishes with a mature, self-critical discussion of the physical boundaries and operational niches of weight-space routing. All mathematical formulas, variables, figures, and tables are clearly defined, aligned, and highly readable. The contextualization relative to prior work (static merging, test-time adaptation, and dynamic routing) is outstanding.

### Significance
* **Rating:** Excellent
* **Justification:** This paper serves as a major wake-up call and benchmark for the model-merging and dynamic routing communities, reminding researchers that proper baseline regularization and elegant, simple activation schemes must be prioritized over over-engineered mathematical metaphors. By introducing the Softmax-free **BSigmoid-Router** and conceptually bridging model merging with established token-level Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) literature, it offers a highly practical, plug-and-play solution for lightweight parameter-space MoEs in real-world Edge AI systems where latency and computation budgets are paramount. The paper's insights are highly significant and likely to influence future LLM expert-consolidation research.

### Originality
* **Rating:** Excellent
* **Justification:** The originality of this submission lies not in the creation of a massive, complex new architecture, but in its creative, rigorous combination of standard principles to deconstruct false progress and build elegant baselines. The scientific approach of variable-isolation via targeted classical router variants (the BC-Router framework) is highly original. Furthermore, deconstructing the structural under-scaling flaw of Softmax bounding, identifying that exotic wave projections act as a structural regularizer under low-data budgets, and proposing independent sigmoidal activations as a macro-level parameter MoE represent highly novel and valuable conceptual insights.

---

## 3. Overall Recommendation
* **Score:** 5: Accept
* **Justification:** This is an exceptionally solid, high-impact, and technically sound paper that applies Occam's razor to deconstruct exotic mathematical metaphors in model merging. It demonstrates that a simple classical routing head with standard L2 regularization completely resolves the reported SVHN collapse (outperforming the SOTA wave formulation by $+12.00\%$), and introduces a simple, elegant, Softmax-free sigmoidal router (**BSigmoid-Router**) that achieves stable, balanced multi-task performance with zero test-time latency and an ultra-compact parameter footprint (772 parameters). 

While the empirical scope is restricted to a compact Vision Transformer backbone and four vision tasks, this controlled sandbox is ideal for isolating parameter-routing dynamics. The paper is outstandingly clear, rigorously evaluated, highly reproducible (with a public repository), and exhibits exemplary scientific honesty by discussing the practical utility limits of dynamic weight-space routing. It represents a top-tier contribution to the sub-field of model merging and AI consolidation, and should be accepted.
